deforestation /diːˌfɔːr.əˈsteɪ.ʃən/ (n) sự phá rừng
  • Deforestation is destroying animal habitats.
  • The biggest driver of deforestation is agriculture. Famers chop down trees in order to plant crops like soybeans, palm trees and cocoa, or to make room to raise livestock for beef. Logging operations which provide the world's wood and paper products also cut countless trees each year. Forests are also destroyed as result of growing urban sprawl, as land is developed for dwellings.

    extinction /ɪkˈstɪŋk.ʃən/ (n) sự tuyệt chủng (a situation in which something no longer exists)
  • The extinction of the dinosaurs occurred millions of years ago.
  • Many species of plants and animals are extinction.
  • (adj) extinct: tuyệt chủng ( existing)
  • There is concern that the giant panda will soon become extinct.
  • In addition to helping regulate the earth's climate, forests provide habitats for over 80% of the plants and animals that live on land. But deforestation destroys these habitats, diminishing biodiversity. Some estimate that four to six thousand rainforest species go extinct each year. This also affects more than two billion people who rely on forests as sources of food and shelter.
