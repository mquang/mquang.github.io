certain /ˈsɝː.tən/ (determiner)
1. cụ thể (nhưng không muốn đề cập đến tên)
(used to talk about a particular person, thing, group of things etc without naming them or describing them exactly)

2. nào đó (used before a noun when it is difficult to describe)
(adj) chắc chắn, chắc (confident and sure, without any doubts)		 (determiner)
1.
  • Certain members of the audience may disagree with what I'm about to say.
  • The library’s only open at certain times of day.
  • I promised to be in a certain place by lunchtime.
  • For certain personal reasons I shall not be able to attend.

    • 2.
  • I felt there was a certain coldness in her manner.
  • The song has a certain appeal, but I'm not sure what it is.

    • (adj)
    (syn) sure
  • I’m absolutely certain that I left the keys in the kitchen.
  • I felt certain that I’d passed the test.
  • Now, are you certain about that?
  • I'm not certain how much it will cost.
    		•
    potentially /poʊˈten.ʃəl.i/ (adv) có khả năng (with a possibility to occur or become reality in the future)
  • Any major airport or station is potentially a terrorist target.
  • a potentially dangerous situation
  • Because he did not study for the final test, Mark could potentially fail the class.
  • Driving over the speed limit could potentially cause a car accident.
  • (n, adj) potential: tiềm năng
    • (n)
  • You have the potential to reach the top of your profession.
  • When he saw I had some potential, he gave me extra coaching.

    • (adj)
  • a potential
  • He was eager to talk with potential customers.
    		•
    belief /bɪˈliːf/ (n) lòng tin, niềm tin, sự tin tưởng (the feeling that something is definitely true or definitely exists) (n)
  • a strong belief in God
  • His battle against cancer has strengthened his belief in God.
  • her sincere belief that her brother was not the murderer
  • Recent scandals have shaken many people's belief in politicians.
  • (v) believe: tin, tin tưởng
  • I don’t believe he’s only 25.
  • Believe me, she's not right for you.
  • People used to believe (that) the earth was flat.
    		•
    beaker /ˈbiː.kɚ/ (n) cốc đong dùng trong phòng thí nghiệm (a glass cup with straight sides that is used in chemistry for measuring and heating liquids)
    lab /læb/ (n) phòng thí nghiệm (a room or building with scientific equipment for doing scientific tests or for teaching science, or a place where chemicals or medicines are produced)
    a laboratory
  • She works in a chemistry lab.
  • a research
  • Students learning computer studies spend two days each week in a computer lab and four days in the classroom.
    		•
    bummed (adj) unhappy, sad, depressed, disappointed
    usually with out
  • Kevin was rather bummed about how his relationship finished with Jenny.
  • Todd just heard that he didn't get the job, and he's really bummed out.
  • I'm bummed out when I hear bad news.
    		•
    give it a shot (idiom) thử (give it a try, to try to do it)
    = give it a try

    = give it a go

  • A: "Do you want to try driving my car, to see how you like it?"
    B: "Yeah, sure, I'll give it a shot."
  • Are you having trouble fixing the printer? Let me give it a shot.
