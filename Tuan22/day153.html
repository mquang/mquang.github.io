lecture /ˈlek.tʃər/ (n)
1. bài giảng, tiết học (a long talk on a particular subject that someone gives to a group of people, especially to students in a university)


2. (cho) 1 bài (an act of criticizing someone or warning them about something in a long serious talk, in a way that they think is unfair or unnecessary)		 1.
  • His lecture was complicated and difficult to follow.
  • Have you been to any of Professor MacPherson’s lectures?
  • I couldn't help drifting off in the middle of that lecture - it was so boring!

    • 2.
  • My father caught me and gave me a long lecture about the dangers of drink.
  • My dad gave me a lecture on the evils of alcohol last night.
    		•

  • a precious

  • This letter is really precious to me because it's the last one that Pete ever wrote.
  • Diamonds and rubies are precious stones.

  • The dog's barking scared the burglar off.

  • If you charge as much as that, you'll scare customers off.
