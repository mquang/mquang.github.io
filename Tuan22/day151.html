Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
conference /ˈkɒn.fərəns/ (n) hội nghị (a large formal meeting where a lot of people discuss important matters such as business, politics, or science, especially for several days)
  • Representatives from over 100 countries attended the International Peace Conference in Geneva.
  • She is attending a three-day conference on AIDS education.
  • The conference will be held in Glasgow.
    		•
    phenomenal /fəˈnɑː.mə.nəl/ (adj) amazing, very great or impressive
  • He has a phenomenal memory.
  • Angela can do a phenomenal amount of work in one day.
  • The results have been phenomenal.
    		•
    confession /kənˈfeʃən/ (n) sự thú nhận, thú tội
  • I have a confession to make - I've lost that book you lent me.
  • I've a confession to make - I lied about my age.
  • After hours of questioning by police, she made a full confession.
  • (v) confess: thú nhận, thú tội; thú thật là
    - to admit, especially formally or to the police, that you have done something wrong or illegal
    - to admit something that you feel ashamed or embarrassed about
  • He confessed that he had stolen the money.
  • I confess (that) I know nothing about computers.
    		•
    intrude /ɪnˈtruːd/ (v) xen vào sự/đời sống riêng tư của người khác, trở thành kẻ không mời mà đến, kẻ phá đám (to interrupt someone or become involved in their private affairs, in an annoying and unwanted way)
  • I didn't realize your husband was here, Dr Jones - I hope I'm not intruding.
  • I'm sorry to intrude, but I need to talk to someone.
  • Sorry, I didn't mean to intrude. I didn't realize you were on the phone.
  • Excuse me, sir. I don't mean to intrude, but you have a phone call.
  • Would I be intruding if I came along with you?
    		•
    statue /ˈstæ.tʃuː/ (n) tượng, bức tượng, pho tượng (an object made from a hard material, especially stone or metal, to look like a person or animal)
    Lenin statue
  • A statue has been built to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the poet's birthday.
  • Churchill’s statue stands outside the parliament building.
  • a statue of
    • angle /ˈæŋ.ɡəl/ (n)
    1. góc (the space between two lines or surfaces at the point at which they touch each other, measured in degrees)


    2. góc cạnh, khía cạnh
  • A right angle is an angle of 90°.
  • The angles of a square are 90 degrees.
  • the angle between walls and ceiling
  • Draw a 130° angle in your notebooks.

    • 2.
  • Look at every angle of the situation.
  • You can look at the issue from many different angles.
    		•
    red-eye /ˈred.aɪ/ (n) chuyến bay đêm (a flight taken at night)
  • We caught the red-eye from LA and got to New York at five this morning.
  • She caught the red-eye back to New York.
  • Instead of the red-eye flight home, he took a nine o'clock flight the following morning.
    		•
    run into someone (phrasal verb) tình cờ gặp lại, tình cờ bắt gặp (to meet someone by chance)
  • I ran into Mike on Seventh Avenue.
  • Oh, I didn't tell you that I ran into Mary when I was at the movie theater.
  • I ran into an old friend at the concert.
  • I ran into my boss at the supermarket.
