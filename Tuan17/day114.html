revolutionary /ˌrevəˈluː.ʃəner.i/ (adj)
1. (công nghệ, loại thuốc) mới, cuộc cách mạng (thay đổi) (completely new and having a great effect)

2. (liên quan đến, thuộc) cách mạng (relating to a political or social revolution)		 1.
  • Penicillin was a revolutionary drug.

    • 2.
  • My father taught me several revolutionary songs.
  • a revolutionary leader/idea
  • He was arrested for taking part in revolutionary activities.
  • (n) revolution:
    1. cuộc cách mạng
    1.1. a change in the way a country is governed, usually to a different political system and often using violence or war

    1.2. a very important change in the way that people do things


    2. vòng xoay, sự xoay vòng
    • 1.1.
  • The French Revolution changed France from a monarchy to a republic.
  • After the revolution, France became a republic.
  • The August Revolution in Vietnam

    • 1.2.
  • The last decade has seen a revolution in telecommunications.
  • In the last ten years there has been a revolution in education.
  • We are seeing a revolution in medical technology.
  • We are going through a digital revolution.
  • the fourth industrial revolution

    • 2.
  • The earth makes one revolution every 24 hours.
  • The earth makes one revolution around the sun in about 365 days.
  • The shaft spins at 950 revolutions per minute.
