imitation /ˌɪm.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən/ (adj) giả (made to look like something else)
(n) bắt chước, giả giọng (an act of copying the way somebody talks and behaves)
 (adj)
  • an imitation leather jacket

    • (n)
  • A child learns to talk by imitation.
  • She can do a wonderful imitation of a blackbird's song.
  • his imitation of an American accent
  • He does an imitation of Barack Obama.
  • (v) imitate: bắt chước, giả (to behave in a similar way to someone or something else, or to copy the speech or behaviour, etc. of someone or something)
  • He imitated her accent perfectly.
  • vegetarian products which imitate meat
  • No computer can imitate the complex functions of the human brain.
  • He tried to imitate my Scots accent and we both laughed.

    mess /mes/ (n)
    1. tình trạng bừa bộn, bẩn thỉu


    2. mớ hỗn độn
    - a situation that is full of problems, usually because of a lack of organization or because of mistakes that somebody has made
    - a person whose life is full of problems they cannot deal with


    3. trông lôi thôi, lếch thếch, nhếch nhác (a person who is dirty or whose clothes and hair are not tidy)
  • He makes a terrible mess when he's cooking.
  • Jem's house is always in a mess.
  • Sorry – the place is a bit of a mess.
  • When I got home, the house was a complete mess.

    be meant to do something 1. có ý định, nhằm mục đích
    intended to (do or be something)

    2. được cho là, đáng lẽ
    - supposed to (be or do something)
    - used to talk about what is desirable, expected    		 1.
  • I was never meant to teach.
  • We were meant to have gone away this week, but Debbie's ill so we couldn't go.
  • The diagram is meant to show the different stages of the process.
  • Christmas time is meant to bring people together.
  • The party was meant to have been a thank-you to the casino staff from the management.
    • YouTube: Rachel's English
    someone meant to do something: định
    When you did one action, but you wanted to do something else, you say that you "meant to" do the 2nd action
  • Do you think she meant to say 9 a.m. instead of 9 p.m.?
  • “Oh, hey Debbie. Sorry, I meant to call someone else.”
  • Sorry, I meant to call you earlier but my phone died.

    • 2.
  • The buses are meant to arrive every 15 minutes.
  • Brighton is meant to be very beautiful at this time of year.
  • Beaujolais is meant to be a really good wine.
  • Why are you watching TV? You’re meant to be working.
  • (v) mean: có ý (to intend to do something or intend that someone else should do something)
    • mean - meant - meant
  • I’m sure she didn’t mean it (=she did not intend to upset or hurt someone).
  • I didn’t mean to upset you.
  • He may sound a bit rude at times, but he means well (=intends to be helpful or kind, even if it does not seem like that).
  • You don’t really mean that, do you?
