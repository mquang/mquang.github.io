embarrassing /ɪmˈbærə.sɪŋ/ (adj) (mô tả câu chuyện, sự vật, sự việc, tình huống) khiến mình/ai đó lúng túng, bối rối, ngượng
  • It's embarrassing to be caught telling a lie.
  • My most embarrassing moment was trying to introduce a woman whose name I couldn't remember.
  • It was so embarrassing - I couldn't remember his name!
  • The doctor asked me a lot of embarrassing questions about my sex life.
  • It was so embarrassing having to sing in public.
  • (adj) embarrassed: (mình/ai đó cảm thấy) lúng túng, bối rối, ngượng
  • She felt embarrassed about undressing in front of the doctor.
  • He was so embarrassed - his face went brick-red.
  • She looked a bit embarrassed by all the praise.

    storage /ˈstɔːrɪdʒ/ (n) kho (the process of keeping something in a particular place until it is needed; the space where things can be kept)
  • They moved to a house with lots of storage space.
  • I put some of my things in storage.
  • The side rooms are used for storage.
  • The strawberries are put into cold storage for several months.
  • We need to get our furniture out of storage.
  • (v) store: cất vào kho; chứa (đồ); dự trữ, tích trữ (to put or keep things in a special place for use in the future)
    • store something away/up
  • I've stored my thick sweaters and jackets (away) until next winter.
  • Squirrels are storing up nuts for the winter.
  • What we lack in this house is space to store things.
    		•
    fault /fɑːlt/ (n) lỗi (mistake)
  • I’m really sorry – it’s all my fault.
  • Why should I say sorry when it's not my fault?

    enterprise /ˈen.t̬ɚ.praɪz/ (n) doanh nghiệp (an organization, especially a business)
  • private enterprise
  • state/public enterprise
    • especially British English (=one owned by the government)
  • The road is bordered by shopping centers, restaurants, retail outlets, and other commercial enterprises.

    overall /ˌoʊ.vɚˈɑːl/ (adv, adj)
    1. tổng thể, nhìn chung (generally, in general)

    2. tổng toàn bộ; (kết quả, người chiến thắng) chung cuộc (including everything or everyone; in total)
    (ns)
    1. bộ quần áo lao động, bộ quần áo bảo hộ
    - a loose coat worn over other clothes to protect them from dirt, etc.
    - a piece of clothing shaped like a coat that you wear over your clothes in order to protect them while you are working.


    2. quần yếm
    		 1.
  • The overall situation is good, despite a few minor problems.
  • Overall, it has been a good year.
  • Overall, the project was successful.
  • He made a few mistakes but did well overall.

    • 2.
  • What will it cost, overall?
  • The company will invest $1.6m overall in new equipment.
  • The project budget is around $25 million overall.
  • Williams came fifth overall.
  • The overall winner, after ten games, will receive $250,000.
  • The overall cost of the trip is $500.

    get rid of /rɪd/ (idiom)
    1. bỏ, loại bỏ (to throw away or destroy something you do not want anymore)

    2. hết

    3. thoát khỏi (ai) (to find a way to make someone leave because you do not want the person to be with you any longer)    		 1.
  • We got rid of all the old furniture.
  • It’s time to got rid of .
  • Governments should be encouraged to get rid of all nuclear weapons.

    • 2.
  • He opened the windows to get rid of the smell.
  • I can’t get rid of this headache/cough.

    • 3.
  • I finally got rid of my little sister by telling her scary stories.
  • Are you trying to get rid of me?
  • He stayed on talking and talking. She finally got rid of him by saying she had to make dinner.

    bright /braɪt/ (adj)
    1. sáng, sáng chói (full of light; shining strongly)


    2. thông minh (intelligent; able to learn things quickly)

    3. (tương lai) sáng lạn

    4. (mắt) sáng, (nụ cười) tỏa nắng
  • The lights are too bright in here - they're hurting my eyes.
