flap /flæp/ (v) đập phần phật, đập cánh phành phạch


(informal) to behave in an excited or anxious way
  • A small bird flapped its wings furiously and flew off.
  • The bird flapped its wings and flew away.
  • Flags flapped in the breeze above their tents.

    • (informal)
  • There’s no need to flap!
  • There's no need to flap - I've got everything under control.
  • Don't flap - there's plenty of time to cook before they arrive.
    		•
    dozen /ˈdʌzən/ (determiner) 1 tá (12) (twelve)


    (informal) rất nhiều (many, a lot of)
  • a dozen eggs
  • Could you get me half a dozen (= six) eggs when you go to the supermarket?
  • Chris, Helen, and half a dozen others went on holiday together.

    • (informal)
  • I've spoken to him dozens of (= many) times, but I still don't know his name!
  • I’ve heard this story a dozen times before.
  • I went there dozens of times.
  • I have a dozen reports to read.
    		•

    1. thân thiện thái quá (friendly in an unpleasant way that does not seem sincere – used to show disapproval)

    2. lầy nhầy, nhơm nhớp bẩn

    • 2.
  • Please remove your slimy boots before coming into the house.
  • Something green and slimy was on the rock.
  • (n) slime: chất nhờn, trơn ướt (bùn, cá, ốc sên)
    (a sticky liquid substance that is unpleasant to touch, such as the liquid produced by fish and snails and the greenish-brown substance found near water)

    go away (phrasal verb)
    1. biến đi


    2. đi du lịch, đi công tác (to leave your home in order to spend time somewhere else, usually for a holiday)

    3. biến mất, hết (mùi, đau bệnh, thâm tím) (to stop existing or being noticeable)
  • Go away and leave me alone!
  • Major Ferguson opened a window, and shouted to the waiting reporters: "You're wasting your time. Go away!"

    • 2.
  • We left our dog in kennels when we went away on holiday.
  • I really missed her when she went away.
  • I’m going away on a business trip next week.
  • I'm going away next week. Would you mind feeding the cat for me?

    • 3.
  • The smell went away when he opened the window.

  • I wish I could make this headache go away.

  • 50 people voted against the new proposal.

  • We’ll be competing against the best companies in Europe.
  • The evidence is against him.

    pardon /ˈpɑːr.dən/ (v)
    - xin lỗi vì ..., xin lỗi cho tôi hỏi (used to politely ask if you can interrupt someone, ask them a question, or tell them something)

    - tha lỗi, tha thứ (to forgive someone for something)
    pardon me for interrupting/asking/saying
  • Pardon me for interrupting, but there's a client to see you.
  • Pardon me for saying so, but you don’t look well.
  • Pardon me, is that seat taken?
  • Pardon me, do you speak English?
  • He could never pardon her for the things she had said.
    • (exclamation) Parden me?: xin lỗi bạn có thể nhắc lại được không; sao cơ? (used to politely ask someone to repeat something they have said because you did not hear it)


  • ‘Hurry up Jonathan!’ ‘Pardon?’ ‘I said hurry up!’
  • ‘You're very quiet today.’ ‘Pardon?’ ‘I said you're very quiet today.’
