Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
precisely /prəˈsaɪs.li/ (adv) đúng (exactly; used to emphasize the accuracy of what you are saying)
  • We arrived at the hotel at precisely 10:30.
  • That's precisely what I meant.
  • The fireworks begin at eight o'clock precisely.
  • She’s precisely the kind of person we’re looking for.
  • (idiom) more precisely: đúng hơn là, chính xác hơn là (used to show that you are giving more detailed and accurate information about something you have just mentioned)
  • The problem is due to discipline , or, more precisely, the lack of discipline, in schools.
    		•
    railyard (n) an area having a network of railway tracks and sidings for storage and maintenance of cars and engines
    donation /doʊˈneɪ.ʃən/ (n) từ việc ủng hộ, quyên góp; việc quyên góp, ủng hộ tiền
    (money or goods that are given to a person or an organization in order to help them, or the act of giving them)
  • Would you like to make a donation (=give money) to our charity appeal?
  • (n) donor:
    1. người cho, người hiến tặng     (a person who gives some of their blood or a part of their body to help someone who is ill)

    2. nhà tài trợ (a person or an organization that makes a gift of money, clothes, food, etc. to a charity, etc.)
    • 1.
  • a donor
  • After a 10-month wait, a suitable donor was found.

    • 2.
  • The charity received £50 000 from an anonymous donor.
    		•
    reflection /rɪˈflek.ʃən/ (n) ảnh phản chiếu (an image that you can see in a mirror, glass, water or on any reflective surface)
  • She was looking at her reflection in the mirror.
  • (v) reflect:
    1. phản chiếu

    2. phản ánh, thể hiện (ở)
    • 1.
  • He saw himself reflected in the .
  • I could see my face reflected in his glasses.

    • 2.
  • The statistics reflect a change in people's spending habits.
  • The extra hours you worked will be reflected in your paycheck this month.
  • Her remarks accurately reflect the views of most of us.
  • The condition of the house is reflected in its low price.
    		•
    slightly /ˈslaɪt.li/ (adv) 1 chút (a little)
  • She's slightly taller than her sister.
  • January’s sales were slightly better than average.
  • He was someone I already knew slightly.
  • (adj) slight: một chút (small in amount or degree)
  • a slight improvement
  • a slight change of plan
  • He's got a headache and a slight fever.
    		•
    grade /ɡreɪd/ (n)
    1. (US) điểm (a letter or number that shows the quality of a student’s work)

    2. lớp
    (in the US school system) one of the levels in a school with children of similar age
    (v)
    1. (US) chấm điểm (to judge the quality of a student’s work by giving it a letter or number)

    2. phân loại (to separate things into different groups according to quality, size, importance etc)
    		 (n1)
  • She got good grades on her exams.
  • 70 per cent of students achieved Grade C or above.

    • (n2)
  • Sam is in (the) second grade.
  • My son will be starting third grade this fall.

    • (v1)
  • I spent all weekend grading papers.

    • (v2)
  • The fruit is washed and then graded by size.
    		•
    mark /mɑːrk/ (n)
    1. dấu, vết (bẩn, trầy, lằn...)

    2. dấu, ghi chú, ký hiệu

    3. (UK) điểm
    (v)
    1. đánh dấu, ký hiệu, ghi chú (to write or draw a symbol, line, etc. on something in order to give information about it)

    2. (UK) chấm điểm
    		 1.
  • The children left dirty marks all over the kitchen floor.
  • There was a red mark on his arm.

    • 2.
  • What do those marks in the middle of the road mean?
  • Put a mark by the names of the most interesting candidates.

    • 3.
    (n, syn, US) grade
  • What mark did you get in the biology exam?
  • You scored full marks in the test - ten out of ten!
  • You get two marks for each correct answer.

    • (v1)
  • Prices are marked on the goods.
  • The teacher marked her absent.

    • (v2)
  • I spend at least six hours a week marking.
  • If you don't hand your homework in on time, I won't mark it.
