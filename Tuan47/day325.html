    expose /ɪkˈspoʊz/ (v)
    1. phơi bày, làm lộ ra, vạch trần
  • to remove what is covering something so that it can be seen
  • to deliberately make something publicly known because you believe that it is wrong or illegal


    • 2. đặt ai đó vào hoàn cảnh, tình cảnh nhiều mối nguy hiểm, nguy hại, tổn hại rình rập (to put somebody/something in a place or situation where they are not protected from something harmful or unpleasant)
    		 1.
    (syn) reveal
  • He damaged his leg so badly in the accident that the bone was exposed.
  • My job as a journalist is to expose the truth.
  • She was exposed as a liar and a fraud.

    • 2.
  • Children are being exposed to new dangers on the internet.

  • Some workers at the power station were exposed to high levels of radiation.
