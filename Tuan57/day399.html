Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
staggering /ˈstæɡ·ər·ɪŋ/ (adj) (shocking because of being extremely large)
  • Nursing care costs a staggering $15,000 per week!
  • In 1988 the drop-out rate reached a staggering 92 percent.
    		•
    precious little/few rất ít, gần như là không có
  • I had precious little time for reading.
  • There are precious few places round here where you can get good Indian food.
  • We get precious few tourists here in the winter.
    • YouTube: Rachel's English
    bon appetit /ˌbɑːn.æp.əˈtit/ (exclamation) chúc ngon miệng (a phrase, originally from French, meaning "good appetite", said to someone who is about to eat, meaning "I hope you enjoy your food")
    also bon appétit
  • Here is your dinner, Darling. Bon appetit!
    		•
    rumble /ˈrʌm.bəl/ (v) (bụng) sôi ùng ục (to make a continuous low sound)
  • Please excuse my stomach rumbling - I haven't eaten all day.
  • We could hear thunder rumbling in the distance.
    		•
    dig in (phrasal verb) ăn đi (used to tell somebody to start to eat)
  • The food's getting cold - dig in!
  • Help yourselves, everybody! Dig in!
    • YouTube: Learn English With TV Series
    omit /oʊˈmɪt/ (v) bỏ sót, quên, bỏ qua (to fail to include someone or something, either deliberately or because you forget)
    (syn) leave sb/sth out
  • She was omitted from the list of contributors.
  • She omitted to mention that she would be away next week.
  • If you are a student, you can omit questions 16–18.
    		•
    trivial /ˈtrɪvi.əl/ (adj) không nghiêm trọng, không quan trọng, không đáng kể (not important or serious; not worth considering)
  • Please don’t leave out any details, no matter how trivial they may seem.
  • The differences between the two products are trivial, so why don’t we promote both of them to see which one the market will go for?
    		•
    as per (preposition) theo như (according to)
  • They made payments of $1,000 a year, as per agreement.
  • We shall proceed as per your instructions.
    		•
