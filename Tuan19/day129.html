considerate /kənˈsɪdər.ət/ (adj) quan tâm, ân cần, chu đáo (showing kind awareness of another's feelings)
  • It was very considerate of you to let us know you were going to be late.
    • YouTube: VocabularyHelp - Learn English Words With Meaning
  • (v) consider:
    1. cân nhắc, xem xét, suy nghĩ     (to think about something carefully, especially before making a choice or decision)


    2. nghĩ đến, để ý đến (cảm xúc của người khác)
    (to think about something, especially the feelings of other people, and be influenced by it when making a decision, etc.)

    3. (được) cho là, cho rằng; nghĩ rằng (to think of someone or something in a particular way or to have a particular opinion)
    • 1.
  • We're considering buying a new car.
  • He was considering what to do next.
  • I am considering investing some money.
  • We're considering selling the house.


    • 2.
  • You should consider other people before you act.
  • Have you considered my feelings?
  • Did you consider your mother and how she's going to feel about you leaving?

    • 3.
  • We don't consider her to be      right for the job.
  • I consider myself lucky that I only hurt my arm in the accident.
  • He is currently considered (to be) the best British athlete.
    		•
    volition /vəˈlɪ.ʃən/ (n) ý muốn, mong muốn, quyết định của tự bản thân người được nói đến, không phải do ai khác ép buộc, tác động
    of own volition
  • You go to work by your own volition every day: nobody forces you to go.
  • The singer wished it to be known that he had left the band (out) of his own volition (= it was his decision).
  • It was announced that he resigned of his own volition (= because he chose to), but I believe he was forced out.
  • Helena left the company of her own volition.
    		•
    voluntary /ˈvɑː.lən.teri/ (adj) tự nguyện, tự giác (done, made, or given willingly, without being forced or paid to do it)
  • She does voluntary work for the Red Cross two days a week.
  • (n) volunteer: tình nguyện viên, người tình nguyện, xung phong
  • I need some volunteers to help with the washing-up.
  • I need someone to rake the yard. Any volunteers?
    		•
    commitment /kəˈmɪt.mənt/ (n) cam kết, ràng buộc (something that you have promised you will do or that you have to do)
  • The psychiatrist will follow Stan's progress during his two-month commitment at the Bellevue mental health facility.
  • He's busy for the next month with filming commitments.
  • Are you ready to make a long-term commitment?
  • Marriage is a lifelong commitment.
  • His lack of commitment to the project was easy to see.
  • Players must make a commitment to play for a full season.
  • You’re young and healthy and you have no commitments – you can do whatever you want.
  • He couldn’t join the class because of work commitments.
  • (v) commit:
    1. hứa sẽ có cam kết, trách nhiệm với việc gì     (to promise sincerely that you will definitely do something, keep to an agreement or arrangement, etc.)

    2. làm những hành động sai trái (tự tử, cướp, giết, hiếp, khủng bố...) (to do something illegal or something that is considered wrong)
    • 1.
  • Anna wants to get married, but Bob’s not sure he wants to commit.
  • Borrowers should think carefully before committing themselves to taking out a loan.

    • 2.
  • She tried to commit suicide by   slashing her wrists.
  • He was sent to prison for a        crime that he didn't commit.
    		•
    counselor /ˈkɑʊn·sə·lər/ (n, US)
    1. luật sư (a lawyer)

    2. cố vấn (học tập, hôn nhân...) (a person whose job is to provide advice, help, or encouragement)
    (n, UK) counsellor
    2.

    (n) lời tư vấn, lời khuyên
    • (v)
  • My job involves counselling unemployed people on/about how to find work.

    • (n)
  • Listen to the counsel of your elders.
