stipulate /ˈstɪp.jə.leɪt/ (v) quy định (to say exactly how something must be or must be done)
  • The law stipulates that new cars must have seat belts for the driver and every passenger.
  • We have signed a contract which stipulates when the project must be completed.
    		•
    lavish /ˈlæv.ɪʃ/ (adj) xa hoa (large in amount, or impressive, and usually costing a lot of money; luxurious)
  • lavish
  • They lived a very lavish lifestyle.
  • Every room in the five-star hotel was filled with lavish furnishings.
  • The lavish house had ten bedrooms, seven baths, and an indoor swimming pool.
    		•
    bona fide /ˌboʊ.nə ˈfaɪ.di/ (adj) chân chính, thật sự (real, legal or true)
  • Make sure you are dealing with a bona fide company.
  • You’re a bona fide member of the team now.
  • When she married the prince, the commoner became a bona fide royal.
    		•
    in plain view/sight (phrase) ở nơi hớ hênh (in a place that is easy to see or notice, especially when it should be hidden)
  • Don't leave in plain sight.
  • When you leave your office, check that all doors and files are locked. Leave nothing of value or sensitive information in plain view.
  • Never leave your laptop unlocked in the office or within plain sight in your vehicle.
    		•
    espionage /ˈes.pi.ə.nɑːʒ/ (n) hành vi gián điệp, tình báo (the activity of secretly getting important political or military information about another country or of finding out another company’s secrets by using spies)
  • Two members of the embassy staff had been expelled for espionage.
  • Three people were accused of engaging in espionage against a rival firm.
    		•

  • malicious gossip
  • He complained that he'd been receiving malicious phone calls.

    		•
    allegation /ˌæl.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (n) lời cáo buộc (a statement, made without giving proof, that someone has done something wrong or illegal)

  • Several of her patients have made allegations of professional misconduct about/against her.
  • He has resigned amid corruption allegations.
