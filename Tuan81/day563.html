advent /ˈæd.vənt/ (n) sự ra đời
  • Life was transformed by the advent of the steam engine.
  • With the advent of e-commerce, shopping has become a few taps of the finger. Shopping has never been so convenient. If you look at many developed countries, you can see a new type of market emerging.
    		•
    inbound /ˈɪn.baʊnd/ (adj) đến (travelling towards a particular point or place rather than leaving it)
    (syn) incoming
  • The company receives around 100 inbound calls per day.
  • At least 1,000 passengers were stranded at the airport as inbound flights throughout the day were diverted to Portland, Ore.
  • (adj, opp) outbound: rời đi (travelling or being taken or sent away from a particular point)
  • There has been an increase in outbound traffic leaving Toronto airport for the Caribbean resorts.
    		•
    favour /ˈfeɪ.vɚ/ (n)
    1. việc mình làm giúp/cần người khác làm giúp (a kind action that you do for someone)

    2. sự ủng hộ, tán thành, chấp thuận (the support or approval of something or someone)
    (US) favor
    1.
  • Could you do me a favour - would you feed my cat this weekend?
  • Thanks very much. I'll return the favour one day.

    • 2.
  • These plans are unlikely to find favour unless the cost is reduced.
  • He sent her presents in an attempt to win her favour.
    		•
    fever /ˈfiː.vɚ/ (n) sốt (a medical condition in which a person has a temperature that is higher than normal)
  • Andy has a fever and won’t be coming into work today.
    		•
    as much as (phrase) gần như, gần (almost)
  • He as much as admitted that it was his fault.
  • You can pay as much as £200,000 for a one-bedroom flat in central London.
    		•
    slack /slæk/ (adj)
    1. chùng, lỏng lẻo (not tight; loose)

    2. ế ẩm
    (of business) not having many customers or sales; not busy
    (v) làm việc kém năng suất, chểnh mảng (to work less hard than you usually do or should do)
    		 (adj1)
  • These tent ropes are too slack - they need tightening.

    • (adj2)
  • Business is always slack at this time of year.

    • (v)
  • You'll be in trouble if you're caught slacking on the job.
  • She called me into her office and accused me of slacking and taking too many holidays!
    		•
    propensity /prəˈpen.sə.t̬i/ (n) thiên hướng (a tendency to behave in a particular way, especially a bad way)
  • There is an increased propensity for people to live alone.
    		•
    mutilate /ˈmjuː.tɪ.leɪt/ (v) cắt xén; phá hoại, hủy hoại bằng việc cắt xén
  • to damage somebody’s body very severely, especially by cutting or tearing off part of it
  • to destroy an idea or a piece of art or entertainment

    		•  1.
  • Her body had been mutilated beyond recognition.
  • The body had been badly mutilated.

    • 2.
  • Intruders slashed and mutilated several paintings.
  • They have mutilated a beautiful film by making these changes.
    		•
    charge off (ph.v) tuyên bố số nợ không có khả năng được thu hồi, khoanh nợ
  • The bank charged off $82.9 million in bad loans on the third quarter.
  • We have to charge off the debt as hopeless.
  • (n) charge-off: tuyên bố của chủ nợ rằng khoản nợ không có khả năng được thu hồi (a statement by a creditor (= someone who is owed money) that a debt will probably not be paid and is counted as a loss)
    		•
    statutory /ˈstætʃ.ə.tɔːr.i/ (adj) theo luật định (​fixed by law; that must be done by law)
  • In France everybody is entitled to a statutory 25 days a year holiday.
  • The statutory fine for this offence is $250.
