empower /ɪmˈpaʊər/ (v) ủy quyền, cho phép
(syn) authorize
  • The courts were empowered to impose the death sentence for certain crimes.
  • This amendment empowers the president to declare an emergency for a wide range of reasons.

    one another (pronoun) nhau, lẫn nhau, về nhau, cho nhau (used when you are saying that each member of a group does something to or for the other people in the group)
    (syn) each other
  • The two countries went to war with one another over oil prices.
  • We all try and help one another.
  • I think we've learned a lot about one another in this session.
  • A: How’s Maria? Do you see her often?
    B: She’s great. We phone each other/one another regularly.
    		•
    step down (ph.v) từ chức, từ bỏ vị trí (to give up a job or position)
  • (also ph.v) step aside: từ chức, từ bỏ vị trí (to leave your job or official position, because you want to or because you think you should)
  • She stepped down as captain of the team.
  • Morris should step aside until the investigation is completed.
    		•
    embezzle /ɪmˈbez.əl/ (v) biển thủ công quỹ (to secretly take money that is in your care or that belongs to an organization or business you work for)
  • Two managers were charged with embezzling $400,000.
  • An American banker, accused of embezzling $13 million, yesterday gave himself up to the authorities.
    		•
    sanction /ˈsæŋk.ʃən/ (n)
    1. lệnh cấm vận (official orders or laws stopping trade, communication etc with another country, as a way of forcing its leaders to make political changes)

    2. sự phê chuẩn, cho phép (official permission or approval for an action or a change)
    		 1.
  • The UN Security Council imposed sanctions on the country to curb its nuclear program.

    • 2.
  • These changes will require the sanction of the court.
  • No decision can be taken without the sanction of the committee.
  • Jackson got into trouble for having a pool party without the sanction of the country club board.
    		•
    falsify /ˈfɒl.sɪ.faɪ/ (v) làm giả hồ sơ, giấy tờ (to change documents, figures, records, etc. in order to deceive people)
  • The certificate had clearly been falsified.
  • You don't look like a woman who would falsify a document to get a job, but you did.
  • (n) falsification: hành vi làm giả hồ sơ, giấy tờ
  • Five people were fired for inappropriate behavior and falsification of records.
    		•
    retaliate /rɪˈtæl.i.eɪt/ (v) trả thù, trả đũa (to hurt someone or do something harmful to someone because they have done or said something harmful to you)
    (syn) revenge
  • In the movie’s sequel, the captured villain will break out of prison and retaliate against the superheroes that put him there.
  • (n) retaliation: hành vi trả đũa
  • Retaliation against an individual for reporting harassment or unlawful discrimination, or for participating in an investigation of a claim of harassment or unlawful discrimination, is a serious violation of this policy and, like harassment or unlawful discrimination itself, will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
    		•
    proceeds /ˈproʊ.siːdz/ (n) khoản tiền thu được (the money that is obtained from doing something or selling something)
  • The proceeds of today's will go to several local charities.
  • She sold her car and bought a piano with the proceeds.
