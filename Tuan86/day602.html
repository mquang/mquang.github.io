buck /bʌk/ (n, informal) 1 đô (a dollar)
  • When I was a waitress, I made a hundred bucks each night.
  • (n) cent: 1/100 dollar (a unit of money worth 0.01 of a dollar, 1 dollar = 100 cents)
  • (n) penny: 1/100 pound (UK); 1/100 dollar (US, Canada)
    • (ns) pennies/pence
  • The bus fare is 80 pence.

    meditation /ˌmed.ɪˈteɪ.ʃən/ (n) thiền (the practice of focusing your mind in silence, especially for religious reasons or in order to make your mind calm)

  • She found peace through yoga and meditation.
    		•
    bandwidth /ˈbændwɪtθ/ (n)
    1. băng thông (a measurement of the amount of information that a particular computer network or internet connection can send in a particular time. It is often measured in bits per second.)


    2. (availability or capacity for doing something)    		 1.
  • Upgrade to a higher bandwidth to increase your download limits.

    • 2.
  • The team does not have the bandwidth to take on new projects at the moment.
  • Frankly, Rick, I don’t think I have the bandwidth to write this article.
    		•
    purge /pɜːdʒ/ (v) loại bỏ (to get rid of something or someone)
  • The system automatically purges unread emails after two weeks.
  • Myrna is so superstitious that she hired a spiritualist to purge her new house of evil spirits.
    		•
    as such (phrase) (represent a noun previously mentioned)
  • I'm an English teacher, and as such, I notice a lot of mistakes native speakers make.
  • He's the former president and as such, he has a security detail 24/7.
  • (phrase) not ... as such: (used to say that the word you are using to describe something is not exactly correct)
  • She’s not really a maid as such; she just helps out in the house sometimes.
  • There isn’t a garden as such, just a little vegetable patch.
    		•
    cutoff /ˈkʌt.ɒf/ (n)
    1. việc ngừng/dừng/cắt điều gì đó (the act of stopping the supply of something)

    2. thời điểm/mốc mà tại đó ngừng/dừng việc gì (a fixed point or limit at which something is stopped)
    also cut-off
    1.
  • The government announced a cut-off in overseas aid.
  • The US has announced a cutoff of military aid to the country.

    • 2.
  • 31 March is the cutoff date for applications to be accepted.
  • The cutoff for blood donations is usually age 65.
    		•
    prop /prɒp/ (v) chống đỡ (to support something by leaning it against something, or by putting something else under, next to, or behind it)
  • This window keeps on closing - I'll have to prop it open with something.

  • I propped my bike against the wall.
  • He was reading in bed, propped up by pillows.

    unattended /ˌʌn.əˈten.dɪd/ (adj) không được trông, để ý, giám sát (not being watched or taken care of)

  • Don't leave unattended valuables at your workstation.
  • According to the report, most accidents occur when young children are left unattended in the home.
