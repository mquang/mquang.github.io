Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
confrontational /ˌkɑːn.frənˈteɪ.ʃən.əl/ (adj) gắt gỏng, hung hăng (tending to deal with people in an aggressive way that is likely to cause arguments, rather than discussing things with them)
  • He has a very aggressive, confrontational way of talking to people.
  • You should try to be less confrontational if you want to work well in a team.
    		•
    put in (ph.v)
    1. dành thời gian, công sức (cho công việc) (to spend a particular amount of time or to make a particular amount of effort doing something)

    2. lắp đặt (to fix something such as equipment in the place where it will be used and connect it so that it is ready to be used)

    3. nói chen vào (to say something that interrupts someone who is speaking)
    		 1.
  • Wendy has been putting in more hours at the office recently.
  • People often feel pressure to put in extra hours.
  • Dorothy had put in a lot of hard work during her six years as chairperson.
  • She often puts in twelve hours' work a day.
  • If you want to be successful, you need to put in the effort.
    • also put something into doing something
  • He's putting a lot of work into improving his French.

    • 2.
  • They’re coming to put the new kitchen in next week.
  • We're having a new shower put in.

    • 3.
  • ‘How old are you?’ ‘Sixteen.’ ‘I’m sixteen too, ’ put in Dixie.
    		•
    bemused /bɪˈmjuːzd/ (adj) hoang mang, bối rối (slightly confused; not knowing what to do or how to understand something)
    (syn) bewildered
  • I was completely bemused by the fact that he was going to get married.
  • I was bemused at his sudden anger.
  • He looked slightly bemused by all the questions.
    		•
    preoccupied /ˌpriːˈɑː.kjə.paɪd/ (adj) bận tâm, suy nghĩ, để tâm quá nhiều đến việc nào đó (thinking or worrying about something too much so that you do not pay attention to other things)
  • She's been very preoccupied recently because her mother has been very ill.
  • What’s wrong with Cindy? She seems a little preoccupied.
  • Many greedy people spend so much of their time being preoccupied with money that they forget about the really great things in life.
    		•
    shilly-shally /ˈʃɪl.iˌʃæl.i/ (v) chần chừ (to waste time or take too long to make a decision)
  • Stop shilly-shallying and make up your mind.
    		•
    tell somebody off (ph.v) mắng (to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong)
    (syn) scold
  • The teacher told me off for swearing.
  • I’m going to get told off for being late.
  • (n) telling-off: (the act of speaking angrily to someone because they have done something wrong)
  • She gave her son quite a few tellings-off for playing truant.
  • He gave me a good telling-off for forgetting the meeting.
    		•
    Come again? (informal) bạn có thể nói/nhắc lại được không? (used to ask someone to repeat something that you have not heard or understood)
    Other ways to say this:
    - Could you please repeat what you just said?
    - Can you say that again?
    - Sorry. What did you say?
    - Pardon me?
    - Excuse me?
  • Sorry, Mike, come again? The connection is bad and I couldn't hear you.
  • Sally: Do you want some more carrots?
    Mary: Come again?
    Sally: Carrots. Do you want some more carrots?
    		•
    second hand (n) kim giây
  • (adj, adv) second-hand: đồ cũ đã qua sử dụng (not new; having been used in the past by someone else)
  • This bike is second-hand but it's still in good condition.
  • (n) hour hand: kim giờ
  • (n) minute hand: kim phút
    		•
    clockwise /ˈklɑːk.waɪz/ (adj, adv) xuôi/cùng chiều quay của kim đồng hồ
  • Turn the clockwise.
  • Screw the lid on clockwise.
  • (adj, adv) anticlockwise/ counterclockwise: ngược chiều quay của kim đồng hồ
    		•
    stopgap /ˈstɒp.ɡæp/ (n) giải pháp tạm thời (something intended for temporary use until something better or more suitable can be found)
  • Hostels are used as a stopgap until the families can find permanent accommodation.
    		•
