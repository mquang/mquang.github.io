Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
whom /huːm/ (pronoun, formal) ai/người mà (used instead of "who" as the object of a verb or preposition)
  • (formal) There was only one person to whom the old man spoke.
  • (less formal) There was only one person who the old man spoke to.
  • I met a man with whom I used to work.
    (I met a man who I used to work with.)
  • With whom am I speaking?
  • To whom this may concern.
    		•
    whose /huːz/ (pronoun, determiner) của ai (used in questions to ask who something belongs to)
  • Whose ?
  • Whose keys are those?
  • Do you know whose car that is?
    		•
    woman /ˈwʊm.ən/ (n) một người phụ nữ, phụ nữ (số ít) (an adult female human being)
  • She's a really nice woman.
  • She is Ireland's first woman president.
  • (ns) women: phụ nữ (số nhiều)
  • Women don’t like me.
  • Women are more sensitive.
  • (n) man: một người đàn ông, đàn ông (số ít) (an adult male human being)
  • He is an average working man trying to make ends meet.
  • Don’t keep Hansen waiting – he’s a busy man.
  • (ns) men: đàn ông (số nhiều)
  • There were two men and a woman in the car.
    		•
    perhaps /pəˈhæps/ (adv) có lẽ, có thể
  • used to say that something may be true, but you are not sure
  • used to give your opinion, when you do not want to be too definite
  • used when making a rough estimate/guess

    		•
    (syn) maybe
  • He hasn't written to me recently - perhaps he's lost my address.
  • ‘Are you going to come?’ ‘Perhaps. I'll see how I feel.’
  • This is perhaps her finest novel.
  • Perhaps 200 people were there.
    		•
    sever /ˈsev.ər/ (v) cắt đứt
  • to break or separate, especially by cutting
  • to end a connection with someone or something

    		•
  • There is no way to ensure that connectivity will never be severed.
  • Electricity cables have been severed by the storm.
  • Martin’s hand was severed in the accident.
  • The US severed diplomatic relations with Cuba in 1961.
  • She has severed all contact with her (ex-husband) family.
    		•
    Further to (preposition, formal) (used in business letters to refer to an earlier letter, conversation, meeting, etc.)
  • Further to our recent telephone conversation, we are writing to confirm details of the forthcoming conference.
  • Further to my letter of last Wednesday, may I remind you that the outstanding balance has still not been paid.
    		•
    user story (n) tài liệu mô tả sơ lược yêu cầu về các tính năng, chức năng cần có trong sản phẩm đứng từ góc nhìn của người dùng/khách hàng (In software development and product management, a user story is an informal, natural language description of features of a software system. They are written from the perspective of an end user or user of a system, and may be recorded on index cards, Post-it notes, or digitally in project management software. Depending on the project, user stories may be written by different stakeholders like client, user, manager, or development team.)
  • A key component of agile software development is putting people first, and a user story puts end users at the center of the conversation. These stories use non-technical language to provide context for the development team and their efforts. After reading a user story, the team knows why they are building, what they're building, and what value it creates.
  • For the individual developer, a user story by itself, without the cross-team conversation, is less descriptive than a traditional requirement.
    		•
    reply /rɪˈplaɪ/ (v) trả lời, đáp, phản hồi
  • "Where are you going?" I asked. "Home," he replied.
  • I try to reply to letters the day I receive them.
  • Has Ian replied to your letter yet?
  • (v, Indian English) revert: trả lời, đáp, phản hồi (to reply)
  • Please revert by email.
    		•
    revert to /rɪˈvɜːt/ (ph.v) quay trở lại
  • to return to a former state; to start doing something again that you used to do in the past
  • to return to an earlier topic or subject

    		•
  • After a few weeks, everything reverted to normal.
  • When they divorced, she reverted to using her maiden name.
  • Try not to revert to your old eating habits.
  • So, to revert to your earlier question...
    		•
    OoO (business phrase) (an abbreviation for Out of Office, a phrase often used in professional contexts to indicate that someone is unavailable for work (usually because they are on vacation))
    EOD (business phrase) cuối ngày (abbreviation for end of day)
  • I need results by EOD.
  • Hi team, by Thursday EOD all the outstanding tasks need to be addressed without fail. Scrum Masters if we are missing any tasks we will be responsible for addressing them to the management.
