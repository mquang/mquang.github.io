play along (ph.v) giả bộ đồng tình, nhập cuộc (to pretend to with someone or something, especially in order to get what you want or avoid annoying them)
  • I decided that I would play along with Larry for a while and see what would happen.
  • She felt she had to play along or risk losing her job.
  • My friend asked me to play along with the joke.

    play out (ph.v) tiến triển, diễn ra (to develop in a particular way)
  • Let's wait and see how the plays out.
    		•
    play something by ear (idiom) tùy theo tình hình, hoàn cảnh, không theo sự chuẩn bị hoặc lên kế hoạch từ trước (to decide how to deal with a situation as it develops rather than by having a plan to follow)
  • Yes, I'd love to play tennis but I'm not sure what the weather is going to be like this weekend. Let's play it by ear.
  • My parents are complete opposites: my mother likes everything planned in detail and my father always wants to play things by ear.
  • I don't know if I want to go shopping this weekend. I'm tired. Let's play it by ear.
    		•

    keep /kiːp/ (v)
    1. giữ nguyên, giữ gìn (một trạng thái/vị trí) (to (cause to) stay in a particular place or condition)

    2. giữ lại, giữ gìn (to continue to have something and not lose it or get rid of it)

    3. cất giữ (to put or store something in a particular place)

    4. giữ chân (to delay someone or prevent them from doing something)
    keep - kept - kept
    1.
  • Can you keep the dog outside, please?
  • She had trouble keeping her balance.
  • Keep your room tidy.
  • Keep quiet! I’m trying to watch the game.

    • 2.
  • We decided to keep our old car instead of selling it.
  • I kept his letters for years.

    • 3.
  • Keep your passport in a safe place.
  • Where do you keep the sugar?
  • The documents are all kept under lock and key.

    • 4.
  • She kept me talking on the phone for half an hour.
  • You're an hour late—what kept you?
  • (collocation) keep your promise/word/appointment: giữ lời, giữ hẹn (to do what you have promised or agreed to do; to go where you have agreed to go)
  • How do I know you’ll keep your word?
  • He failed to keep his appointment at the clinic.
  • (collocation) keep a secret: giữ bí mật (to not tell anyone about a secret that you know)
  • Can you keep a secret?
