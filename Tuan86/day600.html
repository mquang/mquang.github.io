Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
shortcut /ˈʃɔːt.kʌt/ (n) lối tắt, đường tắt
  • a quicker or shorter way of getting to a place
  • a quicker way of doing something in order to save time or effort

    		•
  • You can take a shortcut across the field.
  • I know a shortcut to town.
  • There is no shortcut to mastering a foreign language.
    		•
    outnumber /ˌaʊtˈnʌm.bɚ/ (v) nhiều hơn, áp đảo về số lượng (to be greater in number than someone or something)
  • In nursing, women tend to outnumber men by four to one.
  • In our office the women outnumber the men three to one.
  • Knowing that they were outnumbered by their opponent, the small army decided to retreat from the battle.
    		•
    outdo /ˌaʊtˈduː/ (v) vượt trội hơn người khác (to be better or more successful than someone else at doing something)
    outdo - outdid - outdone
    (synonym) surpass
  • He always tries to outdo everybody else in the class.
  • Sometimes small firms can outdo big business when it comes to customer care.
  • You've outdone yourself (= done better than you have done before).
  • (phrase) not to be outdone: không để thua kém, không để người khác hơn mình (in order not to let someone else do better or seem better than you)
  • Pat was wearing an outrageous purple dress, so, not to be outdone, I put on my new gold suit.
  • Not to be outdone, other computer manufacturers are also donating machines to schools.
    		•
    additive /ˈæd.ɪ.t̬ɪv/ (n) chất phụ gia (a substance that is added to food in order to improve its taste or appearance or to keep it fresh and stop it from decaying)
  • Too much of the food we eat nowadays is full of chemicals and other additives.
    		•
    dismember /dɪˈsmem.bər/ (v) cắt, chặt xác (to cut or tear the dead body of a person or an animal into pieces)
  • It is inhumane to sell dismembered limbs of the animals, such as rhinoceros or elephants, in the market place.
  • The police found the dismembered body of a young man in the murderer's freezer.
  • His dismembered body was dumped near Hoover Dam.
    		•
    twilight /ˈtwaɪ.laɪt/ (n) xế chiều, chập tối (the period just before it becomes completely dark in the evening)
    (syn) dusk
  • We went for a walk along the beach at twilight.
  • (n) twilight years: độ tuổi xế chiều, những năm cuối của cuộc đời (the final years of a person's life, especially those spent in retirement)
  • The woman is getting older. She is now in the twilight years of her age.
  • In his twilight years, he became more mellow and stopped yelling at people.
    		•
    malformation /ˌmæl.fɔːrˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (n) dị tật
  • Some fetal malformations cannot be diagnosed until late in pregnancy.
  • The slight malformation in his left hand was corrected by surgery.
    		•
    stowaway /ˈstoʊ.əˌweɪ/ (n) người đi chui (a person who hides in a ship or plane before it leaves, in order to travel without paying)
  • The crews fortuitously discovered a stowaway in the ship's cool chamber.
  • (ph.v) stow away: đi chui (to hide on a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle in order to escape from a place or to travel without paying)
  • A boy was caught trying to stow away on a plane.
  • Anyone caught stowing away will be forced to work in the kitchens as a way of paying for their passage.
    		•
    noteworthy /ˈnoʊtˌwɝː.ði/ (adj) quan trọng, đáng chú ý (deserving attention because of being important or interesting)
  • The advent of computers was one of the noteworthy milestones in the mankind's history.
    		•
