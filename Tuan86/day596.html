Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
on the same page (idiom) đồng quan điểm, có cùng ý hiểu
  • (of two or more people) in agreement about something (such as how things should be done)
  • multiple people having the same understanding

    		•
  • I want to make sure we're all on the same page before we proceed with the plan.
  • Let us discuss this internally first so that we are on the same page before we speak to the customer.
  • Since you were not present yesterday, I’ll quickly update you about what happened so that we are on the same page.
    		•
    kill two birds with one stone (idiom) một công đôi việc (to succeed in achieving two things in a single action)
  • Bike-to-work schemes are a great way of killing two birds with one stone: getting more exercise while cutting down on the cost of your daily commute.
  • He dropped his kids to school while going to work, killing two birds with one stone.
  • I have to go to the bank, and on the way back, I’ll pick up the groceries as well, killing two birds with one stone.
    		•
    run for (ph.v) tranh cử (to compete in an election)
  • He's running for mayor again this year.
    		•
    overkill /ˈəʊvəkɪl/ (n) nhiều quá mức cần thiết, nhiều quá mất hay (much more of something than is needed or suitable)
  • The outfit was cute, but the accessories were overkill.
    		•
    rig /rɪɡ/ (v) gian lận (to dishonestly arrange the result of an election or competition before it happens)
    (n) giàn khoan dầu (a large structure that is used for getting oil from the ground under the sea)
    		 (v)
  • There were claims that the election was rigged.

    • (n)
  • Her husband works on a rig in the North Sea.
  • The 2014 China-Vietnam oil rig crisis, refers to the tensions between China and Vietnam arising from the Chinese state-owned China National Offshore Oil Corporation moving its Hai Yang Shi You 981 (known in Vietnam as "Hải Dương - 981") oil platform to waters near the disputed Paracel Islands in South China Sea, and the resulting Vietnamese efforts to prevent the platform from establishing a fixed position. On May 2, China unilaterally placed an oil-drilling rig in waters 120 miles from Vietnam’s coast – near islands claimed by both countries and well within Hanoi’s 200-mile exclusive economic zone set by international law. From the outset, approximately thirty Vietnamese vessels tried to intervene, but were repelled by the eighty-plus Chinese ships protecting the rig.
    		•
    hold off (ph.v) tạm hoãn (to delay doing something)
  • Hold off on running the report until you get the final numbers from me.
  • I know you want to start on that project, but hold off until I get these questions answered by the boss.
    		•
    hold back (ph.v)
    1. giữ, cản (to restrain someone, something, or an animal from moving forward or crossing something)

    2. kìm nén cảm xúc (to stop yourself from expressing how you really feel)

    3. kìm hãm sự phát triển (to prevent the progress or development of somebody/something)
    		 1.
  • If a friend was about to fight someone, you might hold him back.
  • Please hold back your dog.
  • The police were unable to hold back the crowd.

    • 2.
  • She just managed to hold back her anger.
  • She bit her lip to hold back the tears.

    • 3.
  • They felt the British economy was being held back by excessive government controls.
  • Do you think that mixed-ability classes hold back the better students?
    		•
    hold in (ph.v)
    1. kìm nén cảm xúc, giữ trong lòng (to not allow your feelings to show)

    2. nhịn tiểu/ỉa/nôn... (giữ ở trong người) (to stop something from moving outwards; to keep oneself from releasing some bodily function)
    		 1.
  • It can be bad for you to hold in anger.
  • I've been holding in my anger for long enough. Now I'm going to tell you how I really feel!
  • I tried not to laugh, but I just couldn't hold it in.

    • 2.
  • I had to pee, but there was no toilet nearby so I had to just hold it in.
  • I was trying desperately to hold my tummy in.
  • Please hold in your burps. It's really disgusting to let them out like that.
    		•
    hold out (ph.v)
    1. kháng cự (to continue to defend a place that is being attacked)

    2. ôm hy vọng
  • to think that something is likely to happen or succeed
  • to wait for a better offer, a better situation or anything better to come along

    		•  1.
  • We can only hold out for a few more hours.
  • They won’t be able to hold out much longer against these attacks.

    • 2.
  • Negotiators aren’t holding out much hope of a peaceful settlement.
  • I don’t hold out much hope that they’ll come.
  • He is holding out for a better offer.
    		•
    hold down (ph.v)
    1. giữ cho khỏi bị bay (to hold something so it does not move upwards)

    2. duy trì được một công việc một khoảng thời gian (to succeed in keeping a job)
    		 1.
  • Hold down the picnic blanket so it doesn’t blow away.
  • They put a cloth on the grass and stones on the edge to hold it down.

    • 2.
  • I just can’t hold down a job. (=I just can’t keep a job for very long, I keep getting fired.)
    		•
