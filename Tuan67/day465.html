Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
exploit /ɪkˈsplɔɪt/ (v) khai thác, bóc lột
  • to use something well in order to gain as much from it as possible
  • to treat a person or situation as an opportunity to gain an advantage for yourself
  • to treat somebody unfairly by making them work and not giving them much in return

    		•
  • We need to make sure that we exploit our resources as fully as possible.
  • She realized that her youth and inexperience were being exploited.
  • They were hoping to exploit any weaknesses the other team might have.
  • I will exploit their weakness to achieve my goal.
    		•
    transient /ˈtræn.zi.ənt/ (adj) tạm thời (lasting for only a short time; temporary)
  • If you are a transient person, you never remain in one place for too long.
  • The snow is transient and will melt as soon as the sun appears.
  • A glass of whisky has only a transient warming effect.
    		•
    accrue /əˈkruː/ (v) tăng dần/tích lũy dần theo thời gian, cộng dồn
  • to increase in amount over a period of time
  • to allow something to increase in amount over a period of time

    		•
  • Interest on this savings account accrues at the rate of 4 percent.
  • The firm had accrued debts of over $6m.
  • She has accrued $80,000 of student debt in her educational pursuit.
    		•
    repatriate /ˌriːˈpeɪ.tri.eɪt/ (v) trả lại (người về nước), cho hồi hương; kiếm về, thu về, gửi về (của cải vật chất khai thác được ở nước ngoài) (to send or bring someone, or sometimes money or other property, back to the country that he, she, or it came from)
  • The government repatriated him because he had no visa.
  • After the war, prisoners were repatriated.
  • The business generates 58% of its sales outside of the United States and will likely repatriate $800 million in foreign earnings.
  • Japanese companies continue to repatriate some overseas assets.
    		•
    thereby /ˌðerˈbaɪ/ (adv) do đó (because of this; as a result of this action)
    (syn) thus
  • Regular exercise strengthens the heart, thereby reducing the risk of heart attack.
  • Many of the Arctic tourist destinations have been exploited by outside companies, who employ transient workers and repatriate most of the profits to their home base. But some Arctic communities are now operating tour businesses themselves, thereby ensuring that the benefits accrue locally.
    		•
    venture /ˈven.tʃɚ/ (n) dự án (kinh doanh mới, mạo hiểm) (a new business project or activity, that involves taking risks)
  • The directors of the company refused to undertake such a risky venture.
  • A disastrous business venture lost him thousands of dollars.
  • His bankruptcy was the result of several reckless business ventures.
  • The project was a joint venture between the two schools.
  • Westinghouse will take a 48 percent stake in the joint venture and invest $ 2.5 million.
    		•
    the norm /nɔːrm/ (n) chuẩn
  • One child per family is fast becoming the norm in some countries.
  • Older parents seem to be the norm rather than the exception nowadays.
  • As a child, the little girl fought the norm and refused to wear skirts or dresses.
    		•
    undermine /ˌʌn.dɚˈmaɪn/ (v) phá hoại, hủy hoại (dần dần) (to make someone less confident, less powerful, or less likely to succeed, or to make something weaker, often gradually)
  • He's been trying to undermine her position by spreading rumours about her.
  • If you continually criticize children you can seriously undermine their confidence.
  • Individuals who abuse their power or position by participating in corruption create unfair competition, undermine a company’s integrity and cause damage to business reputations.
    		•
