Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
seduce /səˈduːs/ (v)
1. quyến rũ (to persuade somebody to have sex with you, especially somebody who is younger or who has less experience than you)


2. cám dỗ, làm hứng thú, thích thú (to make someone want to do something by making it seem very attractive or interesting to them)		 1.
  • Are you trying to seduce me?

    • 2.
  • Nowadays you have to seduce students into learning through colorful graphics or exciting adventure themes.I wouldn't normally stay in a hotel like this, but I was seduced by the fabulous location.
  • They were seduced into buying the washing machine by the offer of a free flight.
  • (adj) seductive: quyến rũ, có sức cám dỗ
    + sexually attractive
    + attractive in a way that makes you want to have or do something
  • a seductive black dress
  • a seductive woman
  • Seductive plates full of scrumptious shrimp and mouthwatering steak made the guests mouth water.
  • the seductive power of advertising
    		•
    sole /soʊl/ (adj) duy nhất, đơn lẻ một mình
  • only; single
  • not shared with anyone else
    • (n) lòng bàn chân (the bottom surface of the foot)
    		 (adj)
  • My sole reason for coming here was to see you.
  • Jack was the sole survivor of the crash.
  • I am the sole woman in the engineering class, and my brother is the sole man in the nursing class.
  • She has sole responsibility for the project.
  • She has the sole responsibility for a large family.

    • (n)
  • The hot sand burnt the soles of their feet as they walked along the beach.
    		•
    breadwinner /ˈbredˌwɪn.ər/ (n) trụ cột gia đình (the member of a family who earns the money, or most of the money, to support the others)
  • Men are often expected to be the breadwinner in a family.
  • Many families now have two breadwinners with both husbands and wives working.
  • He's the sole breadwinner for the family.
    		•
    As a matter of fact (phrase)
  • used to add something more to what you have just said
  • used to say that you do not agree with what someone has just said or that the opposite of what they say is true
  • used to emphasize what you are saying, often because it is surprising.
    		•
    also in fact, in actual fact, actually
  • I don't work. In fact, I've never had a job.
  • We're not losing population in our town. As a matter of fact, people are moving in.

  • "Are you new around here?"
    "As a matter of fact, I’ve lived here for ten years."
    		•
    hierarchy /ˈhaɪ.rɑːr.ki/ (n) hệ thống (cấp bậc) (a system, especially in a society or an organization, in which people are organized into different levels of importance from highest to lowest)
  • She's quite high up in the management hierarchy.
  • Jane chose to become a server at the restaurant because the chain’s hierarchy would allow her to move into management.
  • Even on commercial airlines, there is an economic hierarchy that separates passengers into financial classes.
    		•
    clique /klɪk/ (n) hội, đám, tụi (a small group of people who seem unfriendly to other people)
  • There's a clique at work that never talks to anyone else.
  • Elaine did not receive an invitation to Rachel’s birthday party because she is not a part of the popular clique.
    		•
    torture /ˈtɔːr.tʃər/ (v) tra tấn, hành hạ
  • to hurt somebody physically or mentally in order to punish them or make them tell you something
  • to cause great physical or mental pain to someone

    		•
  • The prisoners were routinely tortured.
  • In the U.S., it is illegal to torture people in order to get information from them.
  • Tom was tortured and murdered.
  • Rachel sat alone for hours at home, tortured by jealousy.
  • Stop torturing yourself.
  • Mary's back pain was torturing her.
    		•
    hence /hens/ (adv) vì thế; đó là lý do vì sao ... (that is the reason or explanation for; therefore)
  • The firm is owned by Mark Atkins, hence the name - MA Advertising.
  • The area was full of black smoke from factories, hence the name "the Black Country."
  • She's just found out she failed her exams, hence her bad mood.
  • During the accident, Jim broke his leg, and hence will not be able to play in the football game.
    		•
    sophisticated /səˈfɪs.tə.keɪ.t̬ɪd/ (adj)
    1. (of a person) able to understand difficult or complicated ideas

    2. phức tạp, tinh vi
    (of a machine, system, etc.) clever and complicated


    3. hiểu biết, thạo đời (having a lot of experience of life, and good judgment about socially important things such as art, fashion etc)    		 2.
  • highly sophisticated systems
  • The software grows more sophisticated over time.

    • 3.
  • a sophisticated, witty American
  • Mark is a smart and sophisticated young man.
    		•
    lunatic /ˈluː.nə.tɪk/ (n) người ngu ngốc, điên rồ, mất trí (a foolish or crazy person)
    (adj) ngu ngốc, điên rồ, mất trí (crazy, ridiculous or extremely stupid)
    (syn) maniac
    (n)
  • He drives like a lunatic.

    • (adj)
  • a lunatic criminal
  • lunatic ideas
    		•
    take into account (phrase) để ý, chú ý đến, lưu tâm đến, suy xét đến…
    (to think about, or consider someone or something)
    also take account of sth
  • I hope you'll take Bill and Bob into account when you plan the party.
  • I hope my teacher will take into account the fact that I was ill just before the exams when she marks my paper.
  • You had better take into account the business of partner before you decide whether cooperate with their company or not.
    		•
