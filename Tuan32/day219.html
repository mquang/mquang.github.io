Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
virtually /ˈvɜː.tʃuə.li/ (adv) hầu như, gần như (almost)
(syn) practically
  • Their twins are virtually identical.
  • This year's results are virtually the same as last year's.
  • Virtually all the children come to school by bus.
  • (adj) virtual:
    1. gần như

    2. ảo, giả lập, qua mạng (done using computer technology over the internet and appearing to exist but not existing in the physical world)
    • 1.
  • Finding a cheap place to rent is a virtual impossibility in this area.

    • 2.
  • Visit our website for a virtual tour of the museum.
  • Students will have more access to resources and support, both virtual and physical.
  • Virtual learning is designed to extend educational experiences. It does not try to replicate them. In virtual learning environments, students access resources and interact in ways they would not or could not in the physical classroom.
    		•
    brag /bræɡ/ (v) khoe khoang, nói phách
    (disapproving) to talk too proudly about something you own or something you have done
    (syn) boast
  • She's always bragging about how much money she earns.
  • They bragged that their team had never been beaten.
  • I’m not bragging but I think I did very well in the interview.
    		•
    preference /ˈprefərəns/ (n) sở thích, sự thích (cái gì) hơn, sự ưu tiên
  • Many people expressed a strong preference for the original plan.
  • I have a preference for sweet food over spicy.
  • Let's make a list of possible speakers, in order of preference.
  • In some countries, there is still a strong preference for sons.
  • (v) prefer: thích (cái gì) hơn
  • Do you prefer hot or cold weather?
    • prefer sb/sth to sb/sth
  • I prefer red wine to white.
  • He prefers watching football to playing it.
    • prefer to do something
    prefer doing something
  • I prefer to travel by train.
  • I prefer to read a book.
  • We prefer going abroad for our vacation.
  • (adj) preferred: ưa thích hơn, mong muốn hơn (liked or wanted more than anything else)
  • The earlier train would be my preferred option.
  • If a brand does not offer their preferred size, shape, colour, or price point, they will find another that does.
    		•
    fate /feɪt/ (n) số phận (the things, especially bad things, that will happen or have happened to somebody/something)
  • The court will decide our fate/fates.
  • He will learn his fate in court tomorrow.
  • No one knows what the fate of the hostages will be.
  • The meeting will decide the fate of the factory.
  • He did not intend to meet the same fate as his companion.
    		•
    well brought up /ˌwel brɑːt ˈʌp/ (adj) ngoan ngoãn, lịch sự, có giáo dục (được nuôi dạy tốt)
    (people, especially children, who are well brought up are polite and act in a quiet and pleasant way, because they have been taught this behaviour at home)
  • She is well brought up.
  • (phrasal verb) bring up: nuôi dạy, nuôi nấng, dạy dỗ (to take care of a child until he or she becomes an adult)
  • He was brought up by my mother when he was a baby.
  • Her grandparents brought her up.
    		•
    shamelessly /ˈʃeɪm.ləs.li/ (adv) không biết xấu hổ, trơ trẽn (in a way that shows that you do not feel ashamed of something you have done, although other people think you should)
  • She's shamelessly having an affair with her friend's husband.
  • You've copied my idea shamelessly.
    		•
    defiant /dɪˈfaɪ.ənt/ (adj) chống đối, ngang ngạnh (openly refusing to obey somebody/something, sometimes in an aggressive way)
  • a defiant
  • The terrorists sent a defiant message to the government.
  • He suddenly took a defiant attitude toward the police officer.
  • Because Gina is a defiant teenager, she is often called to the principal’s office.
  • (v) defy: chống đối, không tuân theo
  • They defied an evacuation order and stayed in town during the hurricane.
  • It is rare to see children openly defying their teachers.
  • (collocation) defy the odds: (to not happen according to the principles you would expect; to accomplish something that most would consider impossible)
  • a 16-week premature baby who defied the odds and survived
    		•
    figure-hugging /ˈfɪɡ.əˌhʌɡ.ɪŋ/ (adj) ôm dáng, ôm sát
  • used to describe clothes that fit closely to your body
  • (of a piece of clothing) tight in an attractive way that shows the shape of a woman's body

    		•
  • a figure-hugging dress
    		•
    feign /feɪn/ (v) giả vờ, làm bộ (to pretend that you have a particular feeling or that you are ill/sick, tired, etc.)
  • He feigned sickness so he wouldn’t have to go to school.
  • ‘A present for me?’ she asked with feigned surprise.
  • The kids always feign sleep when I go in to check on them.
  • I feign sleep when Mom checks up on me after bedtime, then I go back to playing video games when she leaves.
    		•
