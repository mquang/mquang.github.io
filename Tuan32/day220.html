Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
utter /ˈʌt̬ər/ (adj) hoàn toàn, thực sự (nhấn mạnh)
(used to emphasize how complete something is)
(v) nói (to say something)		 (adj)
  • What an utter fool I was!
  • This company treats its employees with utter contempt.
  • That’s utter nonsense!
  • She is now an utter stranger to me.

    • (v)
  • Tom didn't utter a single word.
  • She couldn't utter a word.
  • She sat through the whole meeting without uttering a word.
  • (n) utterance: lời nói (something you say)
  • Politicians are judged by their public utterances.
  • In his public utterances he has suggested support for the bill, but in private he speaks against it.
    		•
    blow up (phrasal verb) nổi nóng (to suddenly become very angry)
    blow - blew - blown
  • My dad blew up (at me) when he saw the bill.
  • Jenny’s father blew up when she didn’t come home last night.
    		•
    outrageous /ˌaʊtˈreɪ.dʒəs/ (adj) không thể chấp nhận được (very shocking and unacceptable)
  • outrageous prices
  • The woman slapped the man who asked her an outrageous question.
  • (n, v) outrage:
    (n) sự phẫn nộ     (a feeling of anger and shock)

    (v) phẫn nộ (to cause someone to feel very angry, shocked, or upset)
    • (n)
  • Her speech caused outrage among the gay community.
  • These murders have provoked outrage across the country.

    • (v)
  • Customers were outraged by the price increases.
  • Local people were outraged at the bombing.
    		•
    therapist /ˈθerə.pɪst/ (n) bác sĩ chuyên khoa (về một phép chữa bệnh nào đó)
    someone whose job is to treat a particular type of mental or physical illness)
  • a speech therapist
  • I'm seeing my therapist on Friday morning.
  • Therapists helped her regain the use of her fingers.
  • He's been seeing a therapist to try to control his anger.
  • (n) therapy: liệu pháp trị liệu (the treatment of an illness or injury over a fairly long period of time)
  • He is undergoing cancer therapy.
    		•
    consolation /ˌkɑːn.səˈleɪ.ʃən/ (n) sự an ủi, sự giải khuây (a thing or person that makes you feel better when you are unhappy or disappointed)
  • The children were a great consolation to him when his wife died.
  • He went to seek consolation in the local pub.
  • (v) console: an ủi, giải khuây (to make someone feel better when they are feeling sad or disappointed)
  • I tried to console her with a box of chocolates.
  • She consoled herself with the fact that no one else had done well in the exam either.
    		•
    mull over /mʌl/ (phrasal verb) suy nghĩ (to think carefully about something for a long time)
  • I need a few days to mull things over before I decide if I'm taking the job.
  • I need some time to mull it over before making a decision.
  • I'll mull over your suggestions and reply to you next week.
  • That's an interesting idea, but I'll have to mull it over.
    		•
    conclusion /kənˈkluː.ʒən/ (n) kết luận
  • It took the jury some time to reach the conclusion that she was guilty.
  • In conclusion (= finally), I would like to thank…
  • It is still too early to reach a conclusion on this point.
  • All the evidence pointed to the conclusion that he was guilty.
  • (v) conclude:
    1. kết thúc     (to come to an end; to bring something to an end)

    2. kết luận (to decide that something is true after considering all the information you have)

    3. hoàn thành việc kí kết hợp đồng, thỏa thuận (to finish arranging an agreement etc successfully)
    • 1.
  • I’d like to conclude with a song by Tim Buckley.
  • He concluded the discussion by thanking everyone for attending.

    • 2.
  • It is too early for us to conclude anything.

    • 3.
  • A trade agreement was concluded between the two countries.
  • An agreement was concluded under which Thailand would help to develop tourism in Laos.
    		•
    play hardball (idiom) chơi "rắn" (đạt được mục tiêu bằng mọi cách, bằng mọi giá)
    (If someone plays hardball, they will do anything that is necessary to achieve or get what they want, even if this involves being harsh or unfair.)
  • If he wants to play hardball with us, we can play that way, too.
  • It's only a month before the election, and I'm sure they'll start to play hardball.
  • We're prepared to play hardball with the company if it means we'll be paid the appropriate amount for our work.
    		•
    guinea pig /ˈɡɪn.i ˌpɪɡ/ (n)
    1. chuột lang (a small animal covered in fur with rounded ears, short legs, and no tail, often kept as a pet by children)


    2. chuột bạch, vật thí nghiệm (someone who is used in a scientific test to see how successful or safe a new product, system etc is)    		 2.
  • He used me as a guinea pig.
  • We are guinea pigs.
  • This is the first time I've cooked this dish so you are my guinea pigs.
    		•
    trip /trɪp/ (v) vấp (chân)
    (to lose your balance after knocking your foot against something when you are walking or running)
  • I tripped as I got off the bus.
  • He tripped and fell.
  • Clary tripped over a cable and broke his foot.
