Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
revision /rɪˈvɪ.ʒn/ (n) sự sửa đổi, sự xem lại, sự duyệt lại
+ a change or set of changes to something
+ the act of changing something, or of examining something with the intention of changing it
  • They made revisions to the book.
  • He made some minor revisions to the report before printing it.
  • The teacher gave me some suggestions for revision.
  • My boss did not like the revision, so I switched back to the original.
  • (v) revise: duyệt lại, xét lại, sửa đổi
  • The timetable has been revised.
  • The revised timetable will go into effect on the 5th of this month.
    		•
    division /dɪˈvɪ.ʒn/ (n) 1. sự chia, phép chia
    2. sư đoàn (quân sự); phân khu, khu vực (hành chính)     (a separate part of an army or large organization)
    3. phe (a group of teams that play against each other in a particular sport)
  • The children haven't studied division yet.
  • After learning multiplication, the students were taught division.
  • What division of the company do you work in?
  • A major had to take command of the division.
  • (v) divide: chia
  • She divided the pie into eight pieces.
  • 10 divided by 5 2.
  • Shall we divide the project into six or seven small tasks?
    		•
    decision /dɪˈsɪ.ʒn/ (n) 1. sự quyết định
    2. sự giải quyết, phán quyết (tòa án)     (a choice or judgement that you make after thinking and talking about what is the best thing to do)
  • The final decision is yours.
  • Marriage is a big decision.
  • This is my final decision.
  • I don't want to influence your decision.
  • (v) decide: quyết định
  • In the end, we decided to go to the theatre.
  • Have you decided where you're going for your holiday this year?
  • You have to decide between the two candidates.
    		•
    distinguish /dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪʃ/ (v) phân biệt (to notice or understand the difference between two things, or to make one person or thing seem different from another)
  • They look so similar it's often difficult to distinguish one from the other.
  • Dogs can't distinguish between colors.
  • Can you distinguish her from her sister?
  • I can't distinguish a frog from a toad.
  • Can you distinguish silver from tin?
  • I can't distinguish him from his brother.
  • (adj) distinguished:
    + đặc biệt, khác biệt, đáng chú ý
    + ưu tú, xuất sắc, lỗi lạc
    (used to describe a respected and admired person, or their work)
  • Tom is distinguished.
  • a distinguished writer/director/politician
    		•
    extinguish /ɪkˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪʃ/ (v) dập tắt (lửa, ánh sáng) (to make a fire stop burning or a light stop shining)
  • to extinguish a cigarette
  • It took the firefighters several hours to extinguish the flames.
  • All lights had been extinguished.
    		•
