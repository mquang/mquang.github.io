excite /ɪkˈsaɪt/ (v) kích thích, làm hào hứng (to make someone have strong feelings of happiness and enthusiasm)
  • Nothing about my life excites me at present.
  • The movie was okay, but it didn't excite me that much.
  • (adj) exited: (mình/ai đó cảm thấy) kích thích, hào hứng, háo hức
  • (adj) exiting: (mô tả câu chuyện, sự vật, sự việc, trải nghiệm) thú vị, làm mình/ai đó thấy hào hứng
  • How exciting!
  • I'm excited.
  • I felt excited.
  • Is it exciting?
  • That movie is exciting.
    - excited.

    irritate /ˈɪrəˌteɪt/ (v)
    1. kích thích, chọc tức, làm phát cáu (to make someone angry or annoyed)

    2. kích ứng (da) (to make your skin or a part of your body sore or painful)
  • His incompetence began to irritate everyone.
  • The way he looked at me irritated me very much.
  • The soap irritated my eyes.
  • (adj) irritating: (mô tả sự vật, sự việc, ai đó) làm mình thấy khó chịu (making you feel annoyed)
  • (adj) irritated: (mình/ai đó cảm thấy/có cảm giác) khó chịu (annoyed)
  • My eyes feel irritated.
  • She can be really irritating but I try to ignore her.
  • He has the irritating habit of smoking during meals.
  • He's the most irritating man I've ever met.
  • Tom is irritating because he always has to have the last word in any argument.
    		•
    stimulate /ˈstɪ.myəˌleɪt/ (v) kích thích
    + to encourage something to grow, develop, or become active
    + to make someone excited and interested about something
    + kích thích sự phát triển, sự hoạt động của một bộ phận trên cơ thể
  • Read books to stimulate your child’s imagination.
  • A raise in employee wages might stimulate production.
  • The worried mom didn’t know what it would take to stimulate her son’s interest in school and get him away from video games.
  • Good teachers should ask questions that stimulate students to think.
  • Moderate exercise stimulates the circulation of the blood.
  • The women were given fertility drugs to stimulate the ovaries.


  • (n) stimulant: tác nhân gây kích thích, chất kích thích
    + something that makes or causes something else to grow or develop
    + a substance, such as a drug, that makes the mind or body more active
  • Caffeine, which is found in coffee and tea, is a mild stimulant.
  • Tourism has acted as a stimulant to the country's economy.
    		•
    provoke /prəˈvoʊk/ (v) khiêu khích, chọc tức, khích
  • The animal will not attack unless it is provoked.
  • Don't provoke me.
  • It seems like you're trying to provoke a fight.
  • (adj) provocative:
    1. khiêu khích     (causing an angry reaction, usually intentionally)
    2. kích thích, khêu gợi (tình dục) (If behaviour or clothing is provocative, it is intended to cause sexual desire)

    )
  • Mary is wearing provocative clothing.
