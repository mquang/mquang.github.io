Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
earnings /ˈɜːr.nɪŋz/ (n)
1. tiền lương (the amount of money that someone is paid for working)
2. tiền lãi, lợi nhuận (the profit that a company makes)
  • Average weekly earnings rose by 1.5% in July.
  • His earnings come to ￡5,000 a year.
  • Average earnings for skilled workers are rising.
  • Company ABC releases information that earnings for the third quarter have risen from $10,000,000 to $20,000,000.
  • (v) earn: kiếm được
  • I earn $80,000 a year.
  • How much do you earn, if you don't mind me asking?
    		•
    salary /ˈsæ.lə.ri/ (n) tiền lương (cố định - theo hợp đồng, thỏa thuận) (a fixed amount of money agreed every year as pay for an employee, usually paid directly into his or her bank account every month)
  • Your salary will be paid on the third week of each calendar month.
  • I wouldn't work for them if they paid me twice my current salary.
  • He's learning French so he can get a better salary.
  • Tom is perfectly satisfied with his current salary.
  • He earns my salary.
    		•
    wage /weɪ.dʒ/ (n) tiền lương (trả theo giờ - từ việc làm thêm ...) (an amount of money that is paid to an employee, especially for each hour worked)
  • Wages are paid on Fridays.
  • His 15 stores employ 200 workers at an average wage of $7.40 an hour.
  • The workers receive their wages every Friday.
  • The minimum wage in Okinawa is 642 yen per hour.


    		•
    income /ˈɪn.kəm/ (n) thu nhập (the money that a person, a region, a country, etc. earns from work, from investing money, from business, etc.)
  • Tourism is a major source of income for the area.
  • Farming is his main source of income.
  • He earns a good income as a consultant.
  • Taxation is based on income.
  • His income is three times larger than mine.


  • (n) gross income: tổng thu nhập (the total amount of income received before taking off tax, expenses etc)
  • (n) net income: lương thực lĩnh (số tiền nhận được sau khi trừ thuế, khấu hao vật liệu, quỹ ban đời sống...) (the amount of income left after all tax and other costs have been paid)
    • Earnings and net income are commonly used as synonyms.
    revenue /ˈre.və.njuː/ (n)
    1. tổng thu nhập (tổng số tiền - chưa tính thuế, tiền khấu hao...)
    2. money that a government receives from taxes
  • Taxes provide most of the government's revenue.
  • Other taxes will have to be raised to compensate for the lost revenue.
    • Phân biệt revenue và income:
    - Revenue is the total amount of cash received from the sale of goods or services for a company.
    - Income refers to the net profit that remains after all expenses have been accounted for
    Let's use an example:
        Company ABC sells computers for $1000 each. In one year they sell 50,000 computers, bringing in revenue of $50,000,000. In the course of doing business that year, they have operating expenses of $35,000,000. These operating expenses could include payroll, rents, materials and equipment depreciation. Therefore, the net income for Company ABC is $15,000,000.
