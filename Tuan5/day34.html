Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
allowance /əˈlaʊ.əns/ (n)
1. tiền phụ cấp, trợ cấp (ăn trưa, xăng xe đi lại...) (an amount of money that is given to somebody regularly or for a particular purpose)

2. lượng cho phép (an amount of something that you are allowed)
  • When I was young, I got allowance from my parents.
  • I couldn't have managed at college if I hadn't had an allowance from my parents.
  • Since there is no food allowance, meeting attendees are responsible for paying for their own meals.

    • 2.
  • The allowance for most flights is 20 kilos.
  • (v) allow:
    1. cho phép     (to give permission for someone to do something)
    2. thừa nhận (to admit or agree that something is true)
  • You're not allowed to talk during the exam.
  • Smoking is not allowed in this restaurant.
  • Am I allowed to enter?


  • She allowed that she might have been too suspicious.
    		•
    pension /ˈpen.ʃn/ (n) tiền lương hưu, trợ cấp (an amount of money paid regularly by a government or company to somebody who is considered to be too old or too ill/sick to work)
  • My grandfather is living on a pension.
  • He draws his pension at the post office.
  • She went to the company to draw her pension.
  • She lives on her pension and her savings.
  • He won't be able to his pension until he's 65.
    		•
    commission /kəˈmɪ.ʃn/ (n) 1. tiền hoa hồng

    2. hội đồng, ủy ban     (an official group of people who have been given responsibility to control something, or to find out about something, usually for the government)
    (v)
    1. to ask someone do a particular piece of work for you
    2. ủy nhiệm, bổ nhiệm     (to give someone the official authority to be an officer in the armed forces)    		 (n1)
  • She gets a 15 percent commission she sells.
  • In this job you work on commission (= are paid according to the amount you sell).

    • (n2)
  • The government has set up/established a commission to investigate the problem of inner city violence.
  • Both states set up commissions to examine their public schools.
  • The government has set up a commission of inquiry into the disturbances at the prison.

    • (v1)
  • She's commissioned an artist to paint her portrait.
  • She has been commissioned to write a new national anthem.

    • (v2)
  • Grandfather was commissioned as Group Captain in the RAF just before the war.
    		•
    currency /ˈkə.rən.si/ (n) 1. tiền tệ (the system of money that a country uses)
    2. sự lưu hành (the fact that something is used or accepted by a lot of people)
  • They were paid in U.S. currency.
  • Where can I exchange foreign currency?
  • The currency of the United States is the dollar.

    • 2.
  • I'm not sure about the accuracy and currency of their information.
  • The term “post-industrial” now has wide currency.
    		•
    withdraw /wɪðˈdrɔː/ (v) rút (tiền, rút lui, rút lại lời nói, rút tay khỏi túi ...)
    withdraw - withdrew - withdrawn
  • This credit card allows you to withdraw up to £200 a day from ATMs.
  • She had to withdraw from the competition because of a leg injury.
  • She withdrew her hand from his.
  • Tom withdrew $300 from his account.
  • How much money you would like to withdraw?
  • When was the last time you withdrew money?
  • (n) withdrawal: sự rút tiền, rút ra, rút khỏi
  • Her sudden withdrawal from the competition surprised everyone.
  • He had made several large withdrawals from his bank account (= He had taken out a lot of money).
  • The last withdrawal from the account was on 10 August.
    		•
    deposit /dɪˈpɑː.zɪt/ (v)
    1. gửi tiền

    2. để, đặt, thả     (to put or lay somebody/something down in a particular place - LEAVE)
    (n1) tiền đặt cọc, trả trước

    (n2) tiền gửi ngân hàng
    		 (v1)
  • You can withdraw and deposit money in any of our branches.
  • I deposited £500 in my account this morning.

    • (v2)
  • She deposited a pile of books on my desk.
  • I deposited my luggage in a locker at the station.
  • The taxi deposited us at the train station.
  • Please deposit your things in your room and return to the hotel lobby.

    • (n1)
  • The shop assistant says if I leave £10 as a deposit, they'll keep the dress for me.
  • Will you get your deposit back if you cancel the trip?
  • We paid/put a deposit of £5,000 on the house, and paid the balance four weeks later.

    • (n2)
  • I’d like to make a deposit into my savings account.
  • (n) fixed deposit: tiền gửi có kì hạn với lãi suất cố định (an arrangement with a bank in which you leave your money in an account for a particular length of time, in order to get a higher rate of interest that is fixed)
  • She invested the money on fixed deposit for six months.
  • What are the interest rates for fixed deposit accounts?
  • I’d like to open a fixed deposit account.
    		•
    exchange /ɪksˈtʃeɪn.dʒ/ (n) sự trao đổi (tiền, hàng hóa, thông tin ...) (the act of giving something to someone and them giving you something else)
    (v) trao đổi
  • I buy you lunch and you fix my computer. Is that a fair exchange?
  • Are you an exchange student?
  • Tom is dating an exchange student from China.
  • Is there a bank where I can exchange yen for dollars?
  • I exchanged seats with her.
    		•
