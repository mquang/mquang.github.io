    characteristic /ˌker.ək.təˈrɪs.tɪk/ (n) đặc tính, đặc điểm, đặc trưng, đặc thù (a quality or feature of something or someone that is typical of them and easy to recognize)
  • physical characteristics such as your height and weight
  • Unfortunately a big nose is a family characteristic.
  • The male bird displays (= has) several characteristics which distinguish him from the female.
  • He has all the characteristics of a great husband.
  • The group shared one characteristic - they were all under 25.
  • (n) character:
    1. tính cách, cá tính     (all the qualities and features that make up someone’s personality)

    2. đặc điểm, tính chất
    + a set of qualities that make a place or thing different from other places or things
    + the qualities or characteristics that make something interesting or special

    3. nhân vật (a person in a book, play, film etc)

    4. chữ, ký tự (a letter, number, or symbol that is written, printed, or used in computer programs)
    • 1.
  • Stinginess with money was one aspect of Steve’s character that I didn’t like.
  • I can’t understand why Simon refused to help; it seems so out of character.
  • She's a woman of strong character.

    • 2.
  • the wine's distinctive character
  • Each of his books has its own distinctive character.
  • the unique character of the town/city/region
  • The room lacks character. [=there's nothing special about the room]

    • 3.
  • Who plays the main character?
  • In the story, the main character has left his girlfriend and baby.
  • The story revolves around three main characters.

    • 4.
  • The password may be up to 12 characters long.
  • We studied more than 800 Chinese characters.
