US: /ˈfrædʒəl/		 (adj)
1. mong manh, dễ vỡ (easily damaged or broken)


2. thể trạng yếu, mệt (a weak physical condition because of illness)

3. thanh nhã, thanh tú (thin and delicate)		 1.
  • Be careful with that vase - it's very fragile.
  • Eggs are fragile, so you must store them carefully so that they're not vulnerable to breakage.

    • 2.
  • His health has always been fragile.
  • I’m feeling a bit fragile this morning.
  • I felt rather fragile (= weak) for a few days after the operation.
  • No breakfast for me, thanks - I'm feeling a little fragile (= ill, upset, or tired) after last night's party.

    • 3.
  • fragile beauty
  • The woman's fragile face broke into a smile.
