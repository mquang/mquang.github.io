participate /pɑrˈtɪ.səˌpeɪt/ (v) tham dự, tham gia, dơ tay - góp phần vào (bài giảng)
  • She didn't participate in the discussion.
  • Did you participate in this discussion?
  • They expected him to participate in the ceremony.
    • participate in = take part in
  • All the children took part in the Thanksgiving play.
  • Everyone is asked to take part in the celebration.
  • (n) participant: người tham dự, người tham gia
  • Each participant will be given a name tag to show that they paid to come to the conference.
  • What can participants do at the end of the event?
    		•
    in advance (phrase) trước
  • book + phrase
  • thank you + phrase
  • please pay + phrase
  • happy birthday + phrase

  • You must pay for the ticket in advance.
  • Tickets must be purchased two weeks in advance.

  • What is the difference between "sooner" and "earlier"? Thanks in advance.
  • This game is new version. Do you play at old version in advance?

    patron /ˈpeɪ.trən/ (n)
  • người bảo trợ, ông bầu

  • khách hàng quen (a regular customer of a store, restaurant, hotel, etc.)
    		•
  • The Princess Royal is a well-known patron of several charities.
  • He's been a patron of this store for many years.
  • (v) patronize:
    1. bảo trợ, đỡ đầu
    2. thể hiện mình hiểu biết, ta đây hơn người (người đối diện sẽ cảm thấy: nó nghĩ mỗi mình nó biết còn người khác không biết không bằng)
    3. đến, lui tới (cửa hàng)
    • 1.
  • She patronizes many contemporary British artists.

    • 2.
  • Don’t patronize me!
  • He felt patronized by his supervisor because she explained the situation as if he was a child.

    • 3.
  • I'll never patronize that store again.
  • The restaurant was patronized by many artists and writers during the 1920s.
  • I patronize my favorite restaurant at least twice a week.
  • (n) patronage: sự ủng hộ (bằng việc mua hàng và sử dụng sản phẩm, dịch vụ)
  • Thank you for your patronage.
  • At checkout, the hotel clerk never fails to thank guests for their patronage.
    		•
    consumer /kənˈsuːmər/ (n) người tiêu dùng
  • Many consumer reviews on Amazon are fake.
  • As a consumer, I try and buy inexpensive products.
    • collocation:
    + consumer goods/products: hàng (sản phẩm) tiêu dùng
    + consumer demand: nhu cầu tiêu dùng
    + consumer price: giá tiêu dùng
    + consumer spending: chi tiêu của người tiêu dùng
    + consumer market: thị trường tiêu dùng
  • (v) consume:
    1. dùng, tiêu thụ
    2. đốt cháy hết, tàn phá hết
  • We recommend that this wine should be consumed within six months.
  • He consumes huge amounts of bread with every meal.
  • The hotel was quickly consumed by fire.
    • Phân biệt consumer và customer:
    + customer: người mua hàng, có thể dùng hoặc không dùng sản phẩm đó
    + consumer: người tiêu thụ sản phẩm, không nhất thiết phải mua sản phẩm, có thể dùng thông qua customer
  • (n) consumption: sự tiêu dùng, tiêu thụ
  • Water consumption decreased during the winter.
    		•
    priority /praɪˈɔːrə.t̬i/ (n) sự ưu tiên
  • Being a parent is her first priority.
  • List the tasks in order of priority.
  • Emergency admissions to hospital are given top priority.
  • My first/top priority is to find somewhere to live.
  • Customer satisfaction is our number one priority.
  • (v, US) prioritize: ưu tiên (việc nào trước)
    • (v, UK) prioritise
    mutually /ˈmjuːtʃuə.li/ (adv)
    1. lẫn nhau, qua lại (ảnh hưởng, tác động đến cả 2 phía)


    2. đồng, chung (quan điểm, ý kiến, cảm xúc...)
  • a mutually beneficial relationship
  • The two systems are mutually incompatible.
  • Can we find a mutually convenient time to meet?
  • After years of unhappiness, the couple mutually agreed to a divorce.
  • After several arguments about what city they should live in, the husband and wife mutually agreed on a Charlotte suburb.
  • (xác suất, toán) mutually exclusive events: sự kiện (biến cố) xung khắc (không xảy ra đồng thời, không cùng xảy ra, chỉ xảy ra 1 trong 2)
  • Tung đồng xu (coin-flipping) coi sự kiện A là mặt ngửa, sự kiện B là mặt sấp A và B được gọi là Mutually Exclusive Events.
  • A lot of people still think brains and beauty are mutually exclusive (= cannot exist together at the same time).
  • The two possible outcomes of a coin flip are mutually exclusive; when you flip a coin, it cannot land both heads and tails simultaneously.
  • (adj) mutual:
    1. đồng, chung (quan điểm, ý kiến, cảm xúc...)
    (of two or more people or groups) feeling the same emotion, or doing the same thing to or for each other
    2. lẫn nhau
    • 1.
  • mutual friend

  • We have a mutual dislike of dogs.
  • We need to work together to find a solution to our mutual problem.
  • Find mutual interests, and you will get along with each other.

    • 2.
  • Friendship consists of mutual understanding.
    		•
    mentally /ˈmen.tə.li/ (adv) liên quan đến tinh thần, thần kinh
    mentally deficient: kém thông minh.
  • Michael is a mentally deficient child
    • mentally ill: bị bệnh tâm thần
  • My brother was mentally ill
    • mentally alert: tỉnh táo
  • He was as mentally alert as a man half his age.
  • (adj) mental:
    1. (thuộc) tinh thần, tâm lý, thần kinh
    2. (từ lóng - UK) điên
  • (n) mentalism: tâm thần luận (các trò đọc suy nghĩ, dự đoán sự kiện, điều khiển tâm trí...)
  • (n) mentality: cách nghĩ, cách tư duy (a person’s or group’s way of thinking about things)
  • I can't understand the mentality of people who hurt animals.
  • I don't understand the mentality of these teenagers.
  • (n) mentalist: nhà tâm thần luận (có khả năng biểu diễn những trò mình đã đề cập ở mentalism)
    		•
