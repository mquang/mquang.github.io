Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
hastily /ˈheɪ.stɪ.li/ (adv) vội vàng, hấp tấp
  • He hastily stuffed a few clothes into a bag.
  • Perhaps I spoke too hastily.
  • Because she felt that the tourism video appeared to have been hastily made, Ms.Peppin recommended that it be filmed again.
  • (n) haste: sự vội vàng, hấp tấp
    • (idiom) make haste slowly: dục tốc bất đạt (đừng làm cái gì quá vội vàng)
  • I know you're late for work, but make haste slowly so you don't end up in an accident.
  • (adj) hasty: vội vàng, hấp tấp
  • My friend arrived while I was having a hasty breakfast.

    		•
    provided that /prəˈvaɪd/ (conjunction) miễn là với điều kiện
  • = as long as
    		•
  • (synonym, phrase) as long as: miễn là với điều kiện
    • provided that/as long as + S + V
  • We'll be there at about 7.30, provided that there's a suitable train.
  • Provided that the boat leaves on time, we should reach France by morning.
  • The machines will run well provided that they are greased in time.
  • You can play in the living room as long as you don’t make a mess.
    		•
    go into effect /ɪˈfekt/
    /əˈfekt/    		 (phrase) có hiệu lực
  • = come into effect
    		•
  • When does this new law go into effect?
  • The new ticket prices will go into effect April 12, the Giants' Opening Day.
    		•
    On the contrary /ˈkɑːn.tre.ri/ (idiom) trái lại (thành ngữ được đặt đầu câu để nhấn mạnh sự trái ngược về mặt ý nghĩa của câu đó với câu đằng trước)
  • (adj, n) contrary: trái ngược
  • It doesn't seem ugly to me. On the contrary, I think it's rather beautiful.
  • It wasn't a good thing. On the contrary it was a huge mistake.
  • Some people say that older people are wiser. On the contrary, I've met many foolish grandparents, and a number of very sensible young adults.
    		•
    On the whole /hoʊl/ (phrase) nhìn chung, nói chung, đại khái
  • = In general
    		•
  • On the whole, this was a very good day.
  • On the whole, I prefer classical music.
  • The weather was quite nice on the whole, except for one really rainy day in the middle of the trip.
    		•
    convey /kənˈveɪ/ (v) truyền tải, truyền đạt
  • Please convey my apologies to your wife.
  • Colours like red convey a sense of energy and strength.
  • I can't convey my feelings in words.
  • Could you convey a message to Mr Merrick for me, please?
  • (n) conveyor: băng chuyền

    • - conveyor belt: băng chuyền
  • (n) conveyance: sự chuyên chở, sự vận chuyển
  • The conveyance of freshly picked fruit from the fields to the farmer’s market takes place before the sun rises.
    • sincere /sɪnˈsɪr/ (adj) chân thành, thành thật
  • My sincere thanks to you for being my child's teacher.
  • My sincere congratulations on your happy marriage and my best wishes to you two for a lifetime of happiness.
  • a sincere apology
    • YouTube: shin lim
  • (n) sincerity: sự chân thành, lòng chân thành, thành thật
  • If you question his sincerity, do not ask for his help.
  • Nancy seems to lack sincerity.
  • Tom doubts Mary's sincerity.
  • (adv) sincerely: thành thật (từ này hay xuất hiện ở cuối thư trân trọng)
  • We sincerely apologize for our error.
  • I sincerely hope you'll give me this second chance.
    		•
    accommodate /əˈkɑːmə.deɪt/ (v)
    1. chứa

    2. cung cấp
  • The hotel can accommodate up to 500 guests.
  • The car park can accommodate about 200 cars.
    • accommodate someone with sth: cung cấp/cấp cho ai cái gì
  • She will accommodate me with the use of her old car.
  • He will accommodate me with the use of his house, while he is abroad.
  • The chef would be happy to accommodate your special requests.
    		•
    would /wʊd/ (modal verb)
    1. quá khứ của will (sẽ)

    2. + like:(muốn)

    3. sự việc trái với sự thật xảy ra ở hiện tại (câu điều kiện loại 2)

    4. sự việc trái với sự thật đã xảy ra ở quá khứ (câu điều kiện loại 3)

    5. + : thích cái gì hơn là cái gì

    6. nói về những điều thường xảy ra trong quá khứ    		 1.
  • He said he would see his brother tomorrow.

    • 2.




    3.
    if + past simple, ...would + infinitive
  • If I had his number, I would call him. (I don't have his number now, so it's impossible for me to call him).
  • If I were you, I would explain what happened.
  • If I won the lottery, I would buy a big house.

    • 4.
    if + past perfect, ...would + have + past participle
  • If I had been more careful, I wouldn't have made such a big mistake.
  • If she had studied, she would have passed the exam.

  • I’d rather stay at home than go out tonight.
  • I don’t really want to go back to France again this year. I’d sooner go to Spain.
  • Thanks for the invitation, but, if you don’t mind, we’d just as soon stay at home and watch it all on TV.
