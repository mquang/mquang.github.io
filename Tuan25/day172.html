decent /ˈdiː.sənt/ (adj)
1. ổn
  • acceptable
  • of a good enough standard or quality


    • 2. treating people in an honest, fair, and nice way    		 1.
  • a decent
  • We get good benefits, and the pay is decent.
  • Nearby is a village with a decent pub.
  • Tom said there were no decent restaurants in his neighborhood.
  • Tom couldn't find a decent job in Boston, so he moved to Chicago.
  • I need a decent night's sleep.
  • Tom hasn't eaten a decent meal in a long time.

    • 2.
  • It was decent of David to offer to pay.
  • It was very decent (= kind) of you to help.
    		•

  • to provide a good reason for something

    on one's own /oʊn/ (phrase) tự thực hiện công việc (If you do something on your own, you do it without any help from other people.)
  • Bridget learned to tie her shoes on her own when she was three.
  • I don't need anyone's pity or help, I can make it all on my own, thank you very much.
    • YouTube: FitnessBlender
  • (determiner, pronoun; v) own:
    (determiner, pronoun) của chính mình, của riêng mình; tự mình     (belonging to or done by a particular person or thing)

    (v) có, sở hữu, là chủ sở hữu của (to have something that belongs to you)
    • (determiner, pronoun)
  • He wanted an apartment of his own.
  • I'd like to have my very own apartment.
  • I saw it with my own eyes (= I didn't hear about it from somebody else).

    • (v)
  • Do you own your house or do you rent it?
  • Does anyone own this coat? It was left in a classroom.
  • (v) disown: từ bỏ, chối bỏ, cắt đứt, không còn quan hệ (to state that you no longer have any connection with someone that you used to be closely connected with)
  • Her family disowned her for marrying a foreigner.
  • Farrah’s father has threatened to disown her, cutting off all connections if she does not go through with the marriage he has arranged.
    		•
    destiny /ˈdes.tɪ.ni/ (n) định mệnh, vận mệnh, số mệnh (the things that will happen to someone in the future, especially those that cannot be changed or controlled)
    (syn) fate
  • Our destiny is shaped by our actions and decisions, not by chance.
  • He wanted to take control of his own destiny.
  • Nancy wondered whether it was her destiny to live in England and marry Melvyn.
  • (adj) destined: số phận đã định trước (having a future that has been decided or planned at an earlier time, especially by fate)
  • She seemed destined to become a very rich woman.
  • He was destined to become a simultaneous interpreter.
    • YouTube: SSSniperWolf
    determine /dɪˈtɝː.mɪn/ (v) xác định, quyết định
  • Your health is determined in part by what you eat.
  • Eye colour is genetically determined.
  • People want to determine their own destinies.
  • The tests will help the doctors determine what treatment to use.
  • The date of the court case has not yet been determined.


  • (n) determinant: yếu tố quyết định (something that controls or decides how something else will develop or what result it will have)
  • The definition of a determinant is something that determines an outcome or result of something.
  • When your boss' opinion is ultimately the only opinion that matters in making a decision in your office, this is an example of a determinant.
  • If you have a specific gene that is dominant and that always determines what a person's eye color will be, this is an example of a determinant.
    		•

  • She read the report ahead of time to prepare for the meeting.
