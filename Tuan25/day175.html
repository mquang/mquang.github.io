immortalize /ɪˈmɔːr.t̬əl.aɪz/ (v) to prevent somebody/something from being forgotten in the future, especially by mentioning them in literature, making films/movies about them, painting them, etc.
- Immortalize is usually passive.

- If someone or something is immortalized in a story, film, or work of art, they appear in it, and will be remembered for it.
  • Marlene Dietrich was immortalized through her roles in films like "The Blue Angel".
  • The Choptank is the river immortalized in James Michener's novel "Chesapeake."
  • Dickens’ father was immortalized as Mr Micawber in ‘David Copperfield’.



    • YouTube: Stories
  • (adj) immortal: bất tử, bất diệt; bất hủ
    + living or lasting for ever
    + very special and famous and therefore likely to be remembered for a long time
