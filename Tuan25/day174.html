observe /əbˈzɜːv/ (v)
1. phát hiện, nhận thấy (to see or notice somebody/something)

2. quan sát, theo dõi (to watch somebody/something carefully, especially to learn more about them)
 1.
  • Have you observed any changes lately?
  • Officers observed him driving at 90 miles per hour.
  • Scientists have been observing variations in the thickness of the ozone layer.
  • Did you observe anything unusual about his appearance?

    • 2.
  • The patients were observed over a period of several months.
  • I felt he was observing everything I did.
  • We observed 120 women interacting with their babies and compared them with 120 men.
  • (n) observation: sự quan sát, theo dõi (the act of observing something or someone)
  • She was admitted to hospital for observation (= so that doctors could watch her and see if anything was wrong with her).
  • He spent two nights under close observation in hospital.
    • an observation : đài quan sát, chòi quan sát, tòa quan sát
  • The army built an observation tower on the top of the building.
    • YouTube: Stefi & Emi Family
  • (adj) observant: tinh ý, tinh mắt (good or quick at noticing things)
  • "That's a new dress, isn't it?" "Yes, you are observant!"
  • An observant reader has pointed out an error on page 26.
    		•
    disobey /ˌdɪsəˈbeɪ/ (v) không nghe lời, không tuân theo mệnh lệnh, quy tắc (to refuse to do what a person, a law, an order, etc. tells you to do)
  • How dare you disobey me!
  • He was punished for disobeying orders.
  • a naughty boy who often disobeyed his mother and father.
  • The soldier disobeyed the general's orders.
  • (v) obey: nghe lời, tuân theo (mệnh lệnh, quy tắc)
  • ‘Sit!’ he said, and the dog obeyed him instantly.
  • Soldiers are expected to obey orders without questioning them.
  • (adj) obedient: ngoan ngoãn, biết nghe lời, vâng lời
  • Our sons are very obedient.
    		•
    take/plead the fifth /pliːd/ chọn quyền im lặng
    - something that you say in order to tell someone you are not going to answer a question
    - to refuse to answer questions in a court of law because the answers might be harmful to one or might show that one has committed a crime

  • "So, who do you like best, Jenny or Kim?"
    "Sorry, I take the Fifth on that."

    hereby /ˌhɪəˈbaɪ/ (adv) used for stating that something said or written in a statement or document is now official or for emphasizing what you are saying
  • I hereby agree to the conditions of this contract.
  • I hereby submit my resignation, effective immediately.
  • I hereby give you the authority to sign all or any contracts on my behalf.
    		•
    sentence /ˈsen.təns/ (v) tuyên án (to decide and say officially what a punishment will be)
  • The defendant was sentenced to death.
  • He was sentenced to life imprisonment.
    		•
    give birth to somebody (phrase) đẻ ra, sinh ra
  • At 9:40 Claudia gave birth to a nine-pound baby boy.
  • Angela gave birth to a beautiful baby girl last night.

    disclaimer /dɪˈskleɪmər/ (n) sự khước từ trách nhiệm với những gì ngoài điều khoản (a statement that you are not legally responsible for or involved with something, or that you do not know about it - used especially in advertising or legal agreements)
  • They protect themselves by issuing various disclaimers.
  • Prescription drug commercials are mandated to issue a disclaimer, describing the drug's side effects.
  • The website's disclaimer states that shipments are only guaranteed when sent to residents in the UK.
  • (v) disclaim: từ chối trách nhiệm, không biết, không liên quan (to state, especially officially, that you are not responsible for something, that you do not know about it, or that you are not involved with it)
    • (synonym) deny
  • We disclaim all responsibility for this disaster.
  • He disclaimed all responsibility for the accident.
  • Although the suspect tried to disclaim responsibility for the murder, the police were sure that he was the culprit.
  • Martin disclaimed any responsibility for his son’s actions.
