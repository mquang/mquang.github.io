trailer /ˈtreɪ.lər/ (n) toa moóc
- a box on wheels that is pulled by a car and is used for taking things from one place to another


- a wheeled vehicle for living or travelling in, especially for holidays, that contains beds and cooking equipment and can be pulled by a car
  • The car was pulling a trailer with a motorcycle on it.
  • Everyone is inside Tom's trailer.

    escape /ɪˈskeɪp/ (v) bỏ trốn, thoát khỏi
  • to get away from a place where you have been kept as a prisoner or not allowed to leave
  • to get away from an unpleasant or dangerous situation

    		•
  • Two prisoners have escaped.
  • He escaped from prison this morning.
  • She managed to escape from the burning car.
  • The pilot escaped death by seconds.

    assume /əˈsuːm/ (v)
    1. giả định; nghĩ là, cho là (to accept something to be true without question or proof)

    2. đảm nhận, đảm đương (to start to have control, responsibility etc or to start in a particular position or job)    		 1.
  • I assumed it was free.
  • I assume you agree with me.
  • I assumed you were Canadian.
  • I assumed (that) nobody was home because the car wasn’t in the driveway.
  • She automatically assumes that I will help her.
  • Assuming that all goes well, how long is the project expected to take?

    • 2.
  • Jim Paton will assume the role of managing director.
  • He formally assumes the presidency next week.
  • The new president assumes office in January.
  • (n) assumption: giả định; những gì người khác nghĩ, cho rằng mình thế này thế kia (mặc dù không biết rõ chính xác về mình) (something that you think is true although you have no definite proof)
  • My calculations were based on the assumption that house prices would remain steady.
  • Lots of evidence supports this assumption.
  • People make a lot of assumptions about me.
