Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
bottom out (ph.v) chạm đáy (không thể tụt giảm hơn được nữa, từ đó có khả năng sẽ tăng trưởng trở lại)
  • Property prices are still falling, and show no signs of bottoming out.
  • The market has bottomed out and share prices are rising again.
    		•
    break into (ph.v)
    1. đột nhập (to enter a building or car by using force, in order to steal something)

    2. tham gia vào thị trường, lĩnh vực nào đó và gặt hái được thành công (to become involved in a new job or business activity; to be successful when you get involved in something)
    		 1.
  • We had our car broken into last week.
  • The alarm went off as soon as they tried to break into the house.

    • 2.
  • It is hard for new companies to break into the industry.
  • She is well aware that breaking into journalism will not be easy.
  • The US market is huge and we need to break into it.
  • We see the deal with China as a way of breaking into markets in the Far East.
    		•
    dry up (ph.v) cạn kiệt
  • (of rivers, lakes, etc.) to become completely dry
  • if a supply of something dries up, there is gradually less of it until there is none left

    		•  1.
  • During the drought the river dried up.
  • The land had dried up and no crops would grow.

    • 2.
  • What will happen to the project when the money dries up?
  • As she got older, offers of modelling work began to dry up.
  • Funds have completely dried up.
    		•
    hammer out (ph.v) bàn bạc, thỏa hiệp (to discuss a plan, an idea, etc. until everyone agrees or a decision is made)
  • A new contract is being hammered out between the two sides.
  • They finally reached an agreement but it had taken a long time to hammer it out.
    		•
    go under (ph.v) phá sản (to become bankrupt)
  • The firm will go under unless business improves.
  • During the recession, more than 10000 firms went under.
    		•
    level off (ph.v) chững lại, không giảm không tăng
  • to stop rising or falling and remain level
  • to stay at a steady level of development or progress after a period of sharp rises or falls

    		•
  • The plane levelled off at 1 500 feet.
  • Sales have levelled off after a period of rapid growth.
    		•
    mark up (ph.v) đội giá, tăng giá, đẩy giá lên (to increase the price of something, especially something that you bought for a lower price)
  • Foreign cars are often marked up by 40 per cent for the British market.
  • You'd better buy the shoes now before they mark them up.
  • Movie theaters mark up the price of popcorn and candy really high because those are their main moneymakers.
  • (ph.v) mark down: giảm giá, hạ giá (to reduce the price of something)
  • All goods have been marked down by 15 per cent.
  • Those bananas have all been marked down because they're starting to turn brown.
  • They’ve marked down the shoes to £20 for this week only.
    		•
    run up (ph.v) tích lũy nợ, để nợ chồng nợ (if you run up debts, you get into debt and allow the amount to increase)
  • The company has run up massive debts and now owes a staggering €850 million.
  • How had he managed to run up so many debts?
    		•
    run down (ph.v)
    1. (ô tô) đâm/tông khiến người/vật bị tông ngã/đổ xuống (to collide with and knock down someone or something)

    2. (dùng/làm) cạn kiệt, hết pin, năng lượng (to lose power or stop working)

    3. (công ty, doanh nghiệp) giảm dần quy mô, biên chế, số lượng nhân công (​to gradually stop functioning or become smaller in size or number)
    		 1.
  • She got run down outside school.
  • Mary ran down a stop sign.
  • The drunken driver ran three pedestrians down.

    • 2.
  • The clock finally ran down.
  • Switch your headlights off, or you’ll run the battery down.

    • 3.
  • The company is running down its sales force.
    		•
    write off (ph.v) coi như bỏ, coi như mất trắng
  • to decide that someone or something is useless, unimportant, or a failure
  • to officially say that a debt no longer has to be paid, or officially accept that you cannot get back money you have spent or lost

    		•
  • We knew we'd never get the money back so we just wrote the off.
  • After six months of work, we eventually wrote the project off as a non-starter.
    		•
