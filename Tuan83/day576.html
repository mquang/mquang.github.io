Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
crack down (ph.v) trừng trị, trừng phạt thẳng tay, dùng biện pháp mạnh (to start dealing with someone or something much more strictly; to take strong action to stop something)
  • The teacher started to crack down on student tardiness by making them write 100 sentences for each minute they are late.
  • Teachers must crack down on bullying as soon as they become aware of it.
  • The library is cracking down on people who lose their books.
  • After years of leniency, the school is finally cracking down on plagiarism.
    		•
    do over (ph.v) làm lại, thực hiện lại (to do something again, often in an attempt to improve)
  • The actors had to do a scene over because the camera man forgot to press record.
  • Please do over this report and try to make it more concise.
    		•
    drop off (ph.v)
    1. sụt giảm (to decline significantly)

    2. đưa/vận chuyển đến nơi và thả ở đó (to leave someone or something at the destination to which one has transported it)
    		 1.
  • The demand for the Blackberry smartphone dropped off after the iPhone entered the market.
  • Traffic in the town has dropped off since the bypass opened.

    • 2.
  • Pete’s mom drops him off at school every morning.
  • I'll come to your house tonight and drop off your cake pan.
    		•
    wrap up (ph.v) kết thúc (to complete work on something; to bring something to an end)
    (syn) draw to a close/an end
  • We ought to wrap up this meeting and get back to work.
  • OK, everyone, let's wrap it up for tonight.
    		•
    knock off (ph.v)
    1. nghỉ, tan làm (to stop working for the day)

    2. dừng lại việc (used for telling someone to stop doing something)
    		 1.
  • Do you want to knock off early tonight?
  • We knocked off early on Friday to avoid the rush hour queues.
  • I'm going to knock off around six. You want to meet me for dinner?

    • 2.
  • Knock it off, you two! I don't want to see any more fighting.
  • I'm going to go tell those kids to knock off all the shouting.
    		•
    bear with (ph.v) vui lòng kiên nhẫn đợi (used as a polite way of asking someone to be patient while you do or finish something else)
  • A: Hi, I have an interview at 10 with Hanna Baker.
    B: Okay, bear with me a sec please.
  • Bear with me for just a couple of minutes while I .
    		•
    check off (ph.v) đánh dấu lại là đã kiểm tra, đã xử lý hoặc đã đúng (to write a mark next to an item on a list, showing that you have dealt with it or it is correct)
  • Check people’s names off as they arrive.
  • Please check off these parcels before they are posted.
    		•
    check out (ph.v)
    1. làm thủ tục trả phòng và rời khách sạn/bệnh viện

    2. thử ghé xem, trải nghiệm thử (to look at or examine someone or something to see whether you like them)
    		 1.
  • Joan had already checked out of the hotel.
  • Please remember to leave your room keys at reception when you check out.

    • 2.
  • Have you checked out the local sports centre yet?
  • "Check this out. It's that website I was telling you about."
    "Yeah? Let's see."
  • Check out the prices at our new store!
    		•
    pull out (ph.v)
    1. tiến, đánh lái ra đường nơi các phương tiện đang lưu thông

    2. rút khỏi (​to move away from something or stop being involved in it)
    		 1.
  • Don't pull out! There's a car coming.
  • She just pulled out in front of me without indicating!
  • A car suddenly pulled out in front of me.
  • The traffic was so bad that it took me ages to pull out.

    • 2.
  • The firm is pulling out of the personal computer business.
  • They are pulling their troops out of the war zone.
