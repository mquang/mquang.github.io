recognize /ˈrekəɡ.naɪz/ (v)
1. nhận ra (to know someone or something because you have seen or heard him or her or experienced it before)


2. công nhận, thừa nhận, chấp nhận
UK: recognise
1.
  • I hadn't seen her for 20 years, but I recognized her immediately.
  • Do you recognize this song?
  • He'd completely changed - I didn't recognize him.
  • We soon recognized each other, although we had not met for years.
  • I recognized him as soon as he came in the room.
    • YouTube: America's Got Talent
    2.
  • I hadn't recognized the importance of this document until you told me about it.
  • The school has not yet been recognized as an official training centre.
  • The RAF recognized him as an outstandingly able engineer.
  • (n) recognition:
    - sự công nhận, sự thừa nhận
    - nhận dạng (chữ viết, giọng nói...)
    • 1.
  • She gained recognition as an expert in energy conservation.
  • We have poor brand recognition in the Asian market.
  • She recognition worldwide as an outstanding artist.

    • 2.
  • The cell phone has the capability of recognition.

    acknowledge /əkˈnɑː.lɪdʒ/ (v)
    1. công nhận, thừa nhận, nhận

    2. báo cho biết đã nhận được (thư) (to tell someone, usually in a letter or email, that you have received something they sent you)

    3. nhận ra (to show that you have noticed somebody/something by smiling, waving, etc.)
    		 1.
  • They acknowledged that the decision was a mistake.
  • Do you acknowledge that you caused this mess?
  • She won't acknowledge responsibility for her actions.
  • He acknowledged his fault.
  • Tom seems to be unwilling to acknowledge that Mary is a better swimmer than he is.

    • 2.
  • Please acknowledge receipt of this letter (= tell us when you receive it).
  • Did you acknowledge his letter?
  • All applications will be acknowledged.

    • 3.
  • He didn't even acknowledge my presence (= show that he had seen me).
  • He acknowledged me by raising his hat.
  • She walked away without acknowledging me.
  • (n) acknowledgments: lời cảm ơn (a short text at the beginning or end of a book where the writer names people or other works that have helped in writing the book)
    		•
    affect /əˈfekt/ (v) ảnh hưởng, tác động (to have an influence on someone or something, or to cause a change in someone or something)
  • Both buildings were badly affected by the fire.
  • Your opinion will not affect my decision.
  • What you spend time doing in your childhood affects the rest of your life.
  • Smoking affects our health.
  • The damp weather affects his health.
  • Your attendance will affect your final grade.
    		•
    effect /ɪˈfekt/
    or
    /əˈfekt/    		 (n)
    1. ảnh hưởng, tác động

    2. hiệu ứng     (the lighting, sound, or scenery used in a play, movie, or broadcast.)
    		 1.
  • I tried taking tablets for the headache but they didn't have any effect.
  • Modern farming methods can have an adverse effect on the environment.
  • Smoking has a harmful effect on your health.
  • The aspirin took effect quickly.







    • go into effect/come into effect: có hiệu lực (ngày 4)
    FB: Learn English
  • (adj) effective:
    1. hiệu quả, hữu dụng
    - successful or achieving the results that you want
    - producing the result that is wanted or intended; producing a successful result

    2. bắt đầu có hiệu lực
    (of laws and rules) coming into use
    • 1.
  • Aspirin is a simple but highly effective treatment.
  • Tom is an effective salesman.
  • Tom gave Mary advice on effective ways to study French.
  • His method is much more effective than ours.

    • 2.
  • The law will be effective from the 1st of April.
  • The new laws will become effective next month.
  • The new speed limit on this road is effective June 1.
    • (n) effectiveness: tính hữu hiệu, tính hiệu quả (the ability to be successful and produce the intended results)
    effort /ˈefərt/ (n) sự cố gắng, nỗ lực (physical or mental activity needed to achieve something, or an attempt to do something)
  • Your success depends on your efforts.
  • No one succeeds in the world without effort.
  • His effort contributed to my company's growth.
  • Learning to speak another language takes effort.
  • (adj) effortless: dễ dàng, không đòi hỏi nhiều cố gắng (needing little or no effort, so that it seems easy)
  • She dances with effortless grace.
  • The way she dances makes it seem so effortless.
