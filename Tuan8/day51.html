dusty /ˈdʌs.ti/ (adj) bụi bặm, đầy bụi
  • The road to Bangalore was hot and dusty.
  • The room was dark and dusty.
  • The shelves are really dusty.
  • Samandari lives in a small dusty village on the edge of the desert.
  • (n, v) dust:
    (n) bụi
    (v)
    1. lau bụi, phủi bụi


    2. phủ     (to cover something with a light powder)
    • (n)
  • The furniture was covered in dust and cobwebs.
  • I blew the dust off the books.

    • (v1)
  • I was dusting in the bedroom when the phone rang.


  • Rachel dusted the books and the bookshelves.

    • (v2)
  • Dust the top of the cake with icing sugar.
  • Dust the top of the cake with cinnamon.

    dirty /ˈdɝː.t̬i/ (adj)
    1. bẩn (not clean)


    2. khiếm nhã, tục tĩu (used to describe something that is connected with sex)    		 1.
  • How did you get so dirty?
  • All my socks are dirty.
  • I can't breathe this dirty city air.

    • 2.
  • You have a really dirty mind!
  • She looked at me as if I had said a dirty word.

    		•
    chief /tʃiːf/ (adj)
    1. đứng đầu, trưởng (highest in rank)
    2. chính, chủ yếu (most important or main)
    (n) trưởng (cảnh sát trưởng, giám đốc...)
    (a person with a high rank or the highest rank in a company or an organization)
    		 (adj1)
  • the chief financial officer of the company
  • the chief fire officer
  • Chief Executive Officer

    • (adj2)
  • the chief
  • One of the chief causes of crime today is drugs.
  • Safety is our chief concern.
  • Coffee and rice are the country's chief exports.

    • (n)
  • a chief
    		•
    chef /ʃef/ (n) đầu bếp
  • She is head chef at the Waldorf Astoria.
  • A couple of the kitchen staff are on holiday so I'm having to be chef tonight.
  • Sagin is a 31-year-old French chef living and working in Montreal.

    		•
    praise /preɪz/ (v) khen ngợi, tán dương, biểu dương
    (n) sự khen ngợi
    synonym:
    (v) commend
    (n) commendation (ngày 6)
    (n, v) compliment (ngày 11)
    (v)
  • My parents always praised me when I did well at school.
  • Jane was praised by her teacher.
  • Fire chiefs praised a 10-year-old girl who saved her brother's life yesterday.
  • He was highly praised for his research on heart disease.

    • (n)
  • His first novel received high praise.
  • They deserve praise for all their hard work.
  • “Good job” is high praise coming from her. She rarely compliments anyone's work.
    		•
    acclaim /əˈkleɪm/ (n, v) khen ngợi (praise for a person or their achievements) (n)
  • She deserves acclaim for all her charitable works.
  • She has won critical acclaim for her excellent performance.

    • (v)
  • His work was acclaimed by art critics.
  • He was acclaimed as England's greatest modern painter.
    		•
