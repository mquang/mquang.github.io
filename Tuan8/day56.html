Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
clarify /ˈklærɪ.faɪ/ (v) làm rõ (to make something clear or easier to understand by giving more details or a simpler explanation)
  • Could you clarify the first point please? I don't understand it completely.
  • I must clarify that I never intended to offend anyone with my words.
  • Illustrations are provided to help clarify the written instructions.
  • I'm sorry, but can you clarify what it is you're asking about?
  • Just to clarify, you said that you're definitely coming, right?
    		•
    demonstrate /ˈdemən.streɪt/ (v) làm rõ, giải thích
    - to show or make something clear
    - to show something and explain how it works
  • The teacher demonstrated how to use the equipment.
  • He got a job demonstrating kitchen equipment in a department store.

  • The salesman demonstrated how to use .




  • (n) demonstration:
    1. sự hướng dẫn, chỉ dẫn, dạy     (the act of showing someone how to do something, or how something works)
    2. cuộc biểu tình
    • 1.
  • Let me give you a demonstration of how the camera works.

    • 2.
  • There were a number of demonstrations against the new tax.
  • There were demonstrations against the government by Japanese university students in the 1960's.
  • Dozens of young people attended the demonstration.
    		•
    interpret /ɪnˈtɜː.prɪt/ (v)
    1. giải thích (to explain the meaning of something)
    2. phiên dịch, thông dịch (to translate one language into another as you hear it)


    3. hiểu (theo một cách nào đó)
    (to decide that something has a particular meaning and to understand it in this way)
    		 1.
  • The students were asked to interpret the poem.

    • 2.
  • We had to ask our guide to interpret for us.
  • She couldn't speak much English so her children had to interpret for her.

    • 3
  • I didn't know whether to interpret her silence as acceptance or refusal.
  • How do you interpret this passage of the poem?
  • I interpreted silence as consent.
  • She interpreted his remarks as a threat.
  • (n) interpreter: thông dịch viên (someone whose job is to change what someone else is saying into another language)
  • She works as an interpreter in Brussels.
  • Speaking through an interpreter, the President said that the talks were going well.
    		•
    elaborate /iˈlæbə.reɪt/ (v) chi tiết hơn (to add more information to or explain something that you have said)
    (adj) tỉ mỉ, kỹ lưỡng, trau chuốt, phức tạp, tinh vi (very complicated and detailed; carefully prepared and organized)
    		 (v)
  • Can you elaborate?
    • FB: The IELTS Workshop
    (adj)
  • Tom's explanation is very elaborate.
  • She had prepared a very elaborate meal.
  • an elaborate computer system
  • elaborate designs
  • a very elaborate telecommunications network
    		•
    evaluate /ɪˈvælju.eɪt/ (v) đánh giá, định giá
    synonym: assess (ngày 47)
  • It can be difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of different treatments.
  • This activity encourages students to evaluate different points of view and present their own conclusions.
  • Our research attempts to evaluate the effectiveness of the different drugs.
  • You should be able to evaluate your own work.
  • Your work will be evaluated by members of the management team.
  • The magazine article evaluated ten different digital cameras and ranked them according to price and ease of use.
  • The teacher had to evaluate all the students.
  • Scott's job is to evaluate the assets of companies.
  • There are many things which we cannot evaluate in terms of money.
  • (n) evaluation: sự đánh giá
  • Evaluation of this new treatment cannot take place until all the data has been collected.
  • There are many people who think that job evaluation is unnecessary.
