Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
criticize /ˈkrɪt̬ɪ.saɪz/ (v) phê bình, phê phán, bình phẩm, chỉ trích
- to express disapproval of someone or something


- to give an opinion or judgment about a book, film, etc.
  • They criticized me for coming late.
  • Tom criticized Mary in front of everyone.
  • I don't mind being criticized when I am wrong.
  • We were taught how to criticize poems.
  • (n) critic: nhà phê bình
  • The film was praised by the critics when it first came out.
  • She's a film critic for the Irish Times.
  • (n) criticism: sự (lời) phê bình, phê phán, bình phẩm, chỉ trích
  • If you have any constructive (= helpful) criticism of the project, I'd be glad to hear it.
  • Her lack of interest in the project has provoked sharp criticism from colleagues.
  • (n, v) critique:
    (n) bài phê bình     (a piece of written criticism of a set of ideas, a work of art, etc.)

    (v) phê bình, bình phẩm, đánh giá (to give an opinion or judgment about a piece or work, book, film, etc)
  • Students take turns critiquing each others' work.
  • (adj) critical:
    1. phê bình, phê phán, chê bai     (expressing disapproval of somebody/something and saying what you think is bad about them)

    2. biết phê bình, đánh giá (involving making fair, careful judgements about the good and bad qualities of somebody/something)

    3. rất quan trọng (extremely important because a future situation will be affected by it)

    4. nguy hiểm, nguy kịch (extremely serious or dangerous)
    • 1.
  • a critical report/comment

    • 2.
  • Students are encouraged to develop critical thinking instead of accepting opinions without questioning them.

    • 3.
  • Your decision is critical to our future.
  • The president's support is critical (to this project).

    • 4.
  • Both drivers are in a critical condition (= so badly hurt that they might die) after the 120 mph crash.
  • The three gunshot victims are in critical condition.
  • The bullet penetrated his chest, leaving him in critical condition.

    offend /əˈfend/ (v) xúc phạm
  • I really didn't mean to offend her - I just said it unthinkingly.
  • Forgive me if I have offended you.
  • His remarks deeply offended many Thanh Hoa people.
  • (adj) offended: bực mình, cảm thấy bị xúc phạm (upset and angry, often because someone has been rude)
  • I felt a little offended by their lack of respect.
  • She was deeply offended by his remarks.
  • She'll be offended if you don't say thank you for her help.
  • I get very offended when he talks to me like that.
  • (adj, n) offensive:
    (adj) xúc phạm, khinh miệt
    (n) cuộc tấn công     (a planned military attack)
    • (adj)
  • offensive remarks
  • This programme contains language that some viewers might find offensive.
  • The word "nigger" is an offensive term.

    • (n)
  • The plans for the offensive were secret.
  • Several soldiers were injured in the offensive.
  • (n) offence/offense:
    1. vi phạm pháp luật, hành vi bất hợp pháp     (an illegal act - syn: CRIME)
    2. bực mình, cảm giác bị xúc phạm (upset and hurt or annoyed feelings, often because someone has been rude or shown no respect)
    • 1.
  • Driving without a licence is an offence.
  • (idiom) no offence: không có ý xúc phạm, không có ý làm mất lòng, không có ý động chạm (used to say that you do not mean to upset or insult somebody by something you say or do)
  • No offence, but I find your sister a little rude.
  • No offence, but I'd really like to be on my own.
    		•
    insult (n): /ˈɪn.sʌlt/
    (v): /ɪnˈsʌlt/    		 (n) lời lăng mạ, sỉ nhục, sự xúc phạm (an offensive remark or action)
    (v) lăng mạ, sỉ nhục, xúc phạm (to say or do something to someone that is rude or offensive)    		 (n)
  • She made several insults about my appearance.
  • Offering me so little money was an insult.
  • I meant it as a bit of constructive advice, but he took it as a personal insult.

    • (v)
  • She felt they had insulted her by repeatedly ignoring her questions.
  • First he drank all my wine and then he insulted all my friends.
    		•
    humiliate /hjuːˈmɪli.eɪt/ (v) làm nhục, làm bẽ mặt (trước đám đông) (to make somebody feel ashamed or stupid and lose the respect of other people)
  • I didn't want to humiliate her in front of her colleagues.
  • Mary humiliated Tom in front of everyone.
  • The teacher attempted to humiliate me in class by sharing my low test grade with my peers.
