Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
workplace /ˈwɝːk.pleɪs/ (n) cơ quan, nơi làm việc (the office, factory, building, etc. where people work)
  • safety standards in the workplace
    		•
    workspace /ˈwɜːkspeɪs/ (n) văn phòng, bàn làm việc (the office, desk, etc. where someone works)
  • A workplace is a location where you turn up for work five days a week. On the other hand, a workspace is the space where you work.
  • The workplace is where you go to work; the workspace is where you do your work.
  • It is important that your workspace is light and comfortable.
  • We are very informal here, so we want our workspace to look as much like a coffee shop as possible.
    		•
    workforce /ˈwɜːk.fɔːs/ (n) lực lượng lao động, số lượng công nhân, nhân viên
  • Two thirds of the workforce is/are women.
  • The company is cutting its workforce.
  • As CEO, she is responsible for a workforce of 29,000 people.
    		•
    workbench /ˈwɜːk.bentʃ/ (n) bàn làm việc (a strong, solid table on which you can work with tools, wood, small machines, etc.)
    worksheet /ˈwɜːk.ʃiːt/ (n) tờ (trang) câu hỏi, bài tập (a piece of paper or an electronic document on which there is a series of questions and exercises to be done by a student)
    correlate /ˈkɔːr.ə.leɪt/ (v) tương quan, liên quan đến nhau, có mối quan hệ nào đó với nhau
  • Studies show intelligence is unique to each person and does not correlate to a specific race or gender.
  • In economics, the supply and the demand normally correlate because one factor determines the other element.
  • Your doctor will tell you that obesity and a lack of exercise correlate with health problems.
  • In a SQL database query, a correlated subquery is a subquery (a query nested inside another query) that uses values from the outer query.
    		•
    extreme /ɪkˈstriːm/ (adj) rất (very great in degree)
  • People here are living in conditions of extreme poverty.
  • We are working under extreme pressure at the moment.
  • (adv) extremely: rất
  • He works extremely hard.
  • Their new tablet is selling extremely well.
  • She found it extremely difficult to get a job.
    		•
    wire /waɪər/ (n) dây đồng, dây điện
  • a coil of copper wire
  • (n) wiring: hệ thống dây điện (the system of wires that carry electricity in a building)
  • We'd better get an electrician to check the wiring before we start decorating.
  • The wiring was concealed behind a false panel.
    		•
    oversight /ˈoʊ.vɚ.saɪt/ (n) lỗi (a mistake caused by a failure to notice or do something)
  • I didn't mean to leave her name off the list; it was an oversight.
  • Due to an administrative oversight, several members of staff did not receive pay checks this month.
  • Because of a bank oversight, the money had not been credited to my account.
    		•
