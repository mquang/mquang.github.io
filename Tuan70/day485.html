exempt /ɪɡˈzempt/ (v) miễn (to give somebody official permission not to do something or not to pay something they would normally have to do or pay)
  • Pregnant women are exempt from dental charges under the current health system.
  • His bad eyesight exempted him from military service.
  • Charities are exempted from paying the tax.

  • I bought some perfume in the duty-free shop.
    		•
    tariff /ˈtærɪf/ (n) thuế quan - thuế do hải quan của một nước thu đối với hàng hoá xuất khẩu hoặc nhập khẩu khi vận chuyển qua cửa khẩu nước đó (a tax on goods coming into or going out of a country)
  • The US has imposed new tariffs on chocolate from Brazil.
  • An agreement was reached to eliminate tariffs on information technology products.
    		•
    youth /juːθ/ (n)
    1. thời trẻ; sự trẻ, sức trẻ (the period of your life when you are young, or the state of being young)

    2. những người trẻ, những bạn trẻ tuổi (young people considered as a group)
    		 1.
  • I was a fairly good football player in my youth.
  • She regrets that she spent her youth travelling and not studying.
  • His youth gives him an advantage over the other runners.

    • 2.
  • It’s quite a job, training the youth of the country to be mature, responsible citizens.
  • The website targets unemployed youth.
    		•
    contaminate /kənˈtæm.ə.neɪt/ (v) làm bẩn, làm ô uế, ô nhiễm, phun thuốc, tẩm hóa chất, làm nhiễm độc...
  • to make a substance or place dirty or no longer pure by adding a substance that is dangerous or carries disease
  • to influence people’s ideas or attitudes in a bad way

    		•
  • The meat might be contaminated.
  • Industrial sewage continues to contaminate our beaches.
  • They were accused of contaminating the minds of our young people.
  • (n) contaminant: chất bẩn độc hại (a substance that makes something less pure or makes it poisonous)
  • Filters do not remove all contaminants from water.
  • High levels of contaminants have been found in the groundwater, causing concerns about possible health risks to nearby neighborhoods.
