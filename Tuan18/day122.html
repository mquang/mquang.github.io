tendency /ˈten.dən.si/ (n) có khả năng; thường (if someone or something has a tendency to do or become a particular thing, they are likely to do or become it)
  • There is a tendency for unemployment to rise in the summer.
  • The drug is effective but has a tendency to cause headaches.
  • She has a tendency to work late.
  • I have a tendency to talk too much when I'm nervous.
  • (v) tend to do something: thường (to be likely to do something or to happen in a particular way because this is what often or usually happens)
  • When I'm tired, I tend to make mistakes.
  • Things tend to get lost when you move house.
  • We tend to eat at home.

    - to follow an animal or person as closely as possible without being seen or heard, usually in order to catch or kill them
    - to illegally follow and watch someone over a period of time
