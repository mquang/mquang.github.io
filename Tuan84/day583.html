  • something such as a swimming pool or shopping area that is intended to make life more pleasant or convenient for people in a town, hotel, or other place

    or
    /ɪnˈhɪə.rənt/    		 (adj) vốn có (existing as a natural, basic or permanent part of something and that cannot be removed)

    impartial /ɪmˈpɑːr.ʃəl/ (adj) trung lập, công bằng (not supporting any of the sides involved in an argument)
    (syn) neutral
  • A trial must be fair and impartial.
  • The jury has to give an impartial verdict after listening to all of the evidence.

    bias /ˈbaɪ.əs/ (n)
    1. hành động, quyết định thiên vị, không công bằng, có thành kiến
  • the action of supporting or opposing a particular person or thing in an unfair way, because of allowing personal opinions to influence your judgment
  • an attitude that you have that makes you treat someone in a way that is unfair or different from the way you treat other people

    • 2. thiên về (the fact of preferring a particular subject or thing)
    		 1.
  • Employers must consider all candidates impartially and without bias.
  • After a long court battle, the firm was found guilty of showing bias against females in its promotion practices.
  • Do you feel the reporter showed bias against males when she only interviewed female nurses?
  • There was a clear racial bias in the selection of candidates.
  • The villagers had a bias against any newcomer.

    • 2.
  • She showed a scientific bias at an early age.
  • The course has a strong practical bias.
  • (adj) unbiased: công bằng, khách quan, không thiên vị, không thành kiến
  • We'd like an unbiased opinion, if possible.
