Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
preface /ˈpref.ɪs/ (n) lời nói đầu (được viết bởi tác giả cuốn sách) (an introduction at the beginning of a book explaining its purpose, thanking people who helped the author, etc.)
foreword /ˈfɔː.wɜːd/ (n) lời tựa (được viết bởi một người khác hơn là tác giả của cuốn sách) (a short introduction at the beginning of a book, usually by a person other than the author)
  • A foreword is written by someone other than the author and tells the readers why they should read the book. A preface is written by the author and tells readers how and why the book came into being.
    		•
    context /ˈkɒn.tekst/ (n) văn cảnh, ngữ cảnh, bối cảnh (the situation within which something exists or happens, and that helps you to understand it)
  • I think it's important to put this into context.
  • These events are meaningless outside their historical context.
  • Because my teacher did not know the context of the situation, she sent my friend and I to the principal’s office when she heard us talking about our favorite guns in a fictional video game.
    		•
    title /ˈtaɪ.t̬əl/ (n)
    1. tên tác phẩm (the name of a book, poem, painting, piece of music, etc.)

    2. tên chức danh, chức vụ, học hàm, học vị (a word in front of a person’s name to show their rank or profession, whether or not they are married, etc.)
    		 1.
  • What's title of her new book?

    • 2.
  • What's her title - is she Professor or Doctor?
  • What's your job title now - are you managing director?
  • Give your name and title (= Mr, Miss, Ms, Dr, etc.).
    		•
    headline /ˈhed.laɪn/ (n)
    1. tiêu đề (the title of a news article printed in large letters, especially at the top of the front page on a newspaper or the home page on a news website)

    2. (ns) tiêu điểm, tin chính (a short summary of the most important items of news, read at the beginning of a news programme on the radio or television)
    		 1.
  • The scandal was in the headlines for several days.
  • The story of his arrest appeared beneath the headline “Caught!”.

    • 2.
  • The fuel crisis continues to dominate the headlines.
    		•
    chapter /ˈtʃæp.tər/ (n) chương (a separate section of a book, usually with a number or title)
  • I read the first few chapters and then got bored.
  • I've just finished Chapter 3.
    		•
    table of contents (n) mục lục (a list of divisions (chapters or articles) and the pages on which they start)
    document /ˈdɑː.kjə.mənt/ (n) tài liệu
  • a piece of paper or a set of papers containing official information
  • a computer file that you can write in
    • (v) ghi lại (to record something in the form of a written document, photograph, film, etc.)
    		 (n)
  • I've lost a file containing a lot of important documents.
  • Please sign one copy of the document and return it to us as soon as possible.
  • A selective glossary of terms relating to credit finance documents appears in Appendix 2.
  • Remember to save your documents at regular intervals when making changes.

    • (v)
  • Students are encouraged to document their experiences.
  • Her report documents the effects of climate change.
  • (adj) undocumented: không có tài liệu, bằng chứng nào ghi chép về việc này (not supported by written proof)
  • People should not stop eating fish because of some undocumented risk of cancer.
  • The company cannot reimburse you for undocumented travel expenses.
    		•
    bookmark /ˈbʊk.mɑːrk/ (n) thanh đánh dấu trang
  • a piece of thick paper, leather, or plastic that you put between the pages of a book so that you can find a page again quickly
  • the address of a web page that is kept on your computer so that you can find it again easily
    • (v) đánh dấu trang (to add a website to a list of websites you use a lot, so you can click on it to go straight to that website)    		 (v)
  • You'll want to bookmark this page for regular reference.
    		•
    dust jacket /ˈdʌst ˌdʒæk.ɪt/ (n) bìa bọc ngoài sách (a sheet of stiff paper, often in color, wrapped around the cover of a book to protect and advertise it)
    directory /daɪˈrek.tər.i/ (n)
    1. sổ, danh bạ (a book or electronic resource containing lists of information, usually in alphabetical order, for example people’s phone numbers or the names and addresses of businesses in a particular area)

    2. thư mục (a file containing a group of other files or programs in a computer)    		 1.
  • I couldn’t find your number in the telephone directory.
  • I found your address in the directory of university graduates.
  • I found the company's name in a trade directory.

    • 2.
    (syn) folder
  • I suggest you create a separate directory to keep these files in.
  • "Folders" is a common naming convention when referring to directories in the context of graphical user interfaces. "Directories" is a more common term used with console based interfaces.
    		•
