Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
central /ˈsen.trəl/ (adj)
1. tại trung tâm (in the middle of an area or an object)
    1.1 used for saying that something is in the middle of an area, so it is very convenient for using shops, theatres etc

2. trọng tâm, quan trọng nhất (most important)
 1.
  • He lives in central London.
  • My flat is very central.

    • 2.
  • The central issue is that of widespread racism.
  • Owen played a central role in the negotiations.
  • Community involvement is central to our plan.
  • (v) centralize: tập trung/trung tâm hóa, quy về 1 chỗ (to remove authority in a system, company, country, etc. from local places to one central place so that the whole system, etc. is under central control)
  • Payment of bills is now centralized (= organized at one place instead of several).
  • (v) decentralize: phi tập trung hóa, chia nhỏ, phân tản quyền lực (to move the control of an organization or government from a single place to several smaller ones)
  • We decentralized our operations last year and opened several regional offices.
  • If we decentralized, the provinces will have more autonomy.
  • Decentralized finance, or 'DeFi', is an emerging digital financial infrastructure that theoretically eliminates the need for a central bank or government agency to approve financial transactions.
    		•
    market /ˈmɑːr.kɪt/ (n)
    1. thị trường

    2. chợ, hội chợ (a place or event at which people meet in order to buy and sell things)
    		 1.
  • the market
  • The domestic market makes up about 75% of their sales.

    • 2.
  • He runs a fruit and vegetable stall in the market.
  • There’s a good antiques market here on Sundays.
    		•
    marketplace /ˈmɑːr.kɪt.pleɪs/ (n) thương trường, thị trường cạnh tranh (the system of buying and selling in competitive conditions)
  • Analysts question whether the company can survive in a highly competitive marketplace.
  • Some retailers worry that new regulations will hurt their ability to compete in the marketplace.
    		•
    utilize /ˈjuːt̬əlaɪz/ (v) sử dụng, tận dụng
    (syn) make use of
  • The offices have a heating system that utilizes solar energy.
  • If you utilize your time wisely, you will be able to finish all of your chores by lunch.
  • The disorganized woman didn’t know how to utilize her space, so her house stayed cluttered.
    		•
    facilitate /fəˈsɪl.ɪ.teɪt/ (v) giúp cho việc ... trở nên dễ dàng (to make something possible or easier)
  • With the extra funding, the school is going to install elevators to facilitate mobility for disabled students.
  • If you choose the gold package, a tour guide will personally facilitate your trip by travelling with you during your mountain hike.
  • The creators of XRP developed it to be a desirable bridge currency that can facilitate trades in any other currency.
    		•
    hassle /ˈhæs.əl/ (n) khó khăn (a situation that is annoying because it involves doing something difficult or complicated that needs a lot of effort)
  • Liz is getting a lot of hassle about the claim from her insurance company.
  • The shirt didn’t fit so I had to go through the hassle of taking it back to the shop.
    		•
    short-handed /ˌʃɔːrtˈhæn.dɪd/ (adj) thiếu nhân công, thiếu nhân lực
  • Some hospitals are so short-handed that doctors have to work 20-hour shifts.
  • I’m sorry about the delay—we’re rather short-handed today.
  • The company is short-handed these days. I have to wear two hats .
    		•
    heist /haɪst/ (n) vụ cướp
    (syn) robbery
  • Police have no suspects and few clues in the heist.
    		•
    trend /trend/ (n) xu hướng (a general tendency in the way a situation is changing or developing)
  • the general trend towards the centralization of political power
  • The growing trend is for single mothers to bring up children by themselves.
  • (v) uptrend: xu hướng tăng (a gradual increase in the price or value of something, in the prices on a financial market, etc.)
  • Gold prices have been on an uptrend since 2000.
  • (v) downtrend: xu hướng giảm (a gradual reduction in the price or value of something or in the activity of a financial market)
