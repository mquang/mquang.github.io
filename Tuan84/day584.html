festive /ˈfes.tɪv/ (adj) vui vẻ, hồ hởi, lung linh như trong không khí mùa lễ hội (looking or feeling bright and cheerful in a way that seems suitable for celebrating something)
  • My neighborhood is notorious for never being festive, made obvious by the lack of Halloween decorations anywhere throughout all of October.
  • The Christmas lights gave the house a festive appearance.
  • It was Christmas and everyone was in festive mood.
  • (n) festival: lễ hội, đại (nhạc) hội
    + a day or period of the year when people stop working to celebrate a special event, often a religious one
    + a series of performances of films, plays, music, or dancing that is usually organized in the same place at the same time each year
  • It is one of the biggest summer music festivals.
  • My school is getting ready for the campus music festival.
  • This year, the countdown festival Tiger Remix 2020 – Waking the Spring will be held at three biggest cities of Vietnam including Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Danang. The Tiger Remix will feature the high-quality sound and light system as well as professional stage decoration and huge LED screens. Besides, there will be a competition for music talents during the party, creating an amazing playground for music-lovers across the country.

    hardly /ˈhɑːrd.li/ (adv)
    1. gần như không (almost no; almost not; almost none)

    2. gần như là không (thể) (used especially after ‘can’ or ‘could’ and before the main verb, to emphasize that it is difficult to do something)

    3. chỉ vừa mới (used to say that something has just begun, happened, etc.)
    (syn) scarcely, barely
    1.
  • Hardly anyone turned up for the party.
  • I hardly watch any television, apart from news and current affairs.

    • 2.
  • I can hardly keep my eyes open (= I'm almost falling asleep).
  • Something is wrong with the phone – I can hardly hear you.
  • It's so airless in here - I can hardly breathe.

    • 3.
  • We can't stop for coffee now, we've hardly started.

    course /kɔːs/ (n)
    1. khóa học (a set of classes or a plan of study on a particular subject, usually leading to an exam or qualification)

    2. liệu trình (a fixed number of regular medical treatments)

    3. món (any of the separate parts of a meal)
    		 1.
  • I enrolled in a ten-week online course.

    • 2.
  • She received probation upon completing a drug rehab course.
  • She needed a six-month course of physiotherapy after she broke her leg.

    • 3.
  • a -course
  • I've only eaten one course and I'm already full.
  • The main course was roast duck.

    approach /əˈproʊtʃ/ (v)
    1. đến gần

    2. tiếp cận để hỏi, đặt vấn đề, đưa ra lời mời, đề nghị (to ask someone for something, or ask them to do something, especially when you are asking them for the first time)
    (n) cách tiếp cận; sự tiếp cận, tiến đến lại gần
    		 (v1)
  • We could just see the train approaching in the distance.
  • is approaching.

    • (v2)
  • The charity approached several stores about giving food aid.
  • I have already been approached by several other companies. (=offered a job, work etc).
  • At first, they refused to sign the agreement, but they caved in when they heard another firm was being approached.

    • (n)
  • a new approach to
  • This book takes an unorthodox approach to art criticism.
  • Our approach frightened the birds.

    1. người lạ (someone you do not know)

    2. người lần đầu tới đây, không thông thuộc đường xá (a person who is in a place that they have not been in before)

    • 2.

    1. kì lạ     (unusual and unexpected, or difficult to understand)

    2. xa lạ
