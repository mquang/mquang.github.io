surreal /səˈriː.əl/ (adj) very strange; more like a dream than reality
In the art world, “surreal” refers to art that mixes real life and fantasy together.
grab /ɡræb/ (v) tóm lấy, bắt lấy, giật lấy
  • to take hold of something or someone suddenly and roughly)
  • to take an opportunity, accept an invitation etc immediately

    		•
  • She grabbed the opportunity to go to America.
  • A mugger grabbed her handbag as she was walking across the park.
  • If you don't grab this opportunity, you might not get another one.
  • I managed to grab the gun from Bowen.
  • She grabbed the child's hand and ran.
    		•
    outrun /ˌaʊtˈrʌn/ (v)
  • to run faster or further than someone else
  • to develop more quickly than something else

    		•
    outrun - outran - outrun
  • His motorcycle could outrun any car on the road.
  • Demand for the new model is outrunning supply.
    		•
    flick /flɪk/ (v) búng, phẩy (to make something move away by hitting or pushing it suddenly or quickly, especially with your thumb and finger)
  • Papa flicked the ash from his cigar.
  • She flicked the dust off her collar.
    		•
    articulate /ɑːrˈtɪk.jə.lət/ (v) nói, diễn đạt một cách rõ ràng, lưu loát (to express or explain your thoughts or feelings clearly in words)
  • After my grandfather had a stroke, he found it difficult to articulate his needs.
  • When I get nervous, it is very hard for me to articulate my thoughts.
  • Even though English was not Maria’s native language, she was still able to articulate her statement to the court in that language.
    		•
    vividly /ˈvɪvɪd.li/ (adv) in a way that is very clear, powerful, and detailed in your mind
  • I can vividly remember the day we met.
  • I vividly remember my first day at school.
    • YT: Speak English With Vanessa
    Come to think of it (phrase) nói đến/nhắc đến mới để ý/nhận ra/nhớ ra là (used for adding something that you have just remembered about a subject that you are talking about)
  • I love getting her letters. Come to think of it, I haven’t had one for a while.

  • A: If you hear of anyone that's hiring, let me know.
    B: OK, sure... Actually, come to think of it, I have a friend who's looking for some people.
  • I haven't been to Spain in a couple of years. You know, come to think of it, I haven't gone anywhere in the last few years.
    		•
    threaten /ˈθretən/ (v) đe dọa, hăm dọa
  • He threatened to take them to court.
  • They threatened to kill him unless he did as they asked.
  • Are you threatening me?
  • Tom threatened to .
  • Tom threatened me. He said if I didn't give him the money, he'd cut off one of my fingers.
  • (n) threat:
    1. sự đe dọa, hăm dọa     (a statement in which you tell somebody that you will punish or harm them, especially if they do not do what you want)

    2. mối đe dọa, hiểm họa (the possibility that something unwanted will happen, or a person or thing that is likely to cause something unwanted to happen)
    • 1.
  • Your threats don’t scare me.

    • 2.
  • He could be a real threat to the Spanish player in the final.
  • Drugs pose a major threat to our society.
