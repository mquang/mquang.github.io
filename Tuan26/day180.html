homesick /ˈhoʊm.sɪk/ (adj) nhớ nhà (sad because you are away from home and you miss your family and friends)
  • As I read my mother's letter, I began to feel more and more homesick.
  • Alone in the big city, I began to get homesick.
    		•
    turn out (phrasal verb)
    1. hóa ra là (to be known or discovered finally and surprisingly)

    2. (to happen in a particular way or to have a particular result, especially an unexpected one)    		 1.
  • It turned out that she was a friend of my sister.
  • It turned out that I was .
  • Turns out I never lost the ticket—it was in my pocket the whole time!

    • 2.
  • She assured him that everything would turn out all right.
  • It was a difficult time, but eventually things turned out all right.
  • Sometimes things don't turn out the way we think they're going to.
  • The party turned out to be a huge disappointment.
    		•
    slice /slaɪs/ (n) miếng, lát
    (a flat, often thin, piece of food that has been cut from a larger piece)
    (v) thái, cắt thành từng miếng, từng lát (mỏng) (to cut something into thin, flat pieces)
  • a slice of bread/cake
  • cucumber/lemon slices
  • Cut the tomatoes into slices.
  • a thin slice of ham


    • (v)
  • Slice the mushrooms thinly.
  • Thickly slice the potatoes.
    		•
    hook up (phrasal verb)
  • to help someone get something that they need or want
  • to meet with someone
  • to connect two things
  • to set up an electronic device or system
  • to arrange a date for two people
  • to kiss
  • to have a casual sexual encounter
    		•
  • Do you think you can hook me up with some tickets for tonight?
  • Give me a call if you'd like to hook up for lunch sometime.
  • I can hook up to the library from my computer.
  • I can't believe you hooked up with my ex-boyfriend!


    		•
    screw someone /skruː/ (spoken) đụ má ... (fuck someone, a rude and angry expression of disgust or dissatisfaction with someone or something)
  • Screw you.
  • She treated you like garbage, and now she expects you to help her with the business? Screw her!
  • (v) screw:
    1. an offensive word meaning to have sex with someone

    2. đóng (đinh)     (to attach one thing to another using a screw)
    • 1.
  • Wanna screw?
  • They say he's screwing the boss's wife.

    • 2.
  • The shelves were screwed to the wall.
    		•
    somehow /ˈsʌm.haʊ/ (adv)
    1. bằng cách nào đó (in a way that is not known or certain)

    2. vì lí do nào đó (for a reason that is not clear)    		 1.
  • We must stop him from seeing her somehow.
  • It won't be easy, but we'll get across the river somehow.

    • 2.
  • Somehow, I just don’t think it’ll work.
  • Somehow, I don't feel I can trust him.
    		•
    funeral /ˈfjuː.nərəl/ (n) đám tang, tang lễ
  • The funeral will be held next Friday.
  • Over two hundred people attended the funeral.
  • The funeral was held in St Mary’s Church.
  • Hundreds of mourners attended the funeral of the two boys.
    		•
    last /læst/ (v)
    1. kéo dài (to continue for a particular period of time)

    2. tồn tại (to survive something or manage to stay in the same situation, despite difficulties)
    (determiner) qua, vừa rồi, ở mốc thời gian trước mà gần đây nhất (most recent)    		 (v1)
  • The meeting lasted two hours.
  • The drought lasted for several months.
  • They say the snow will last until the end of next week.
  • Each lesson lasts an hour.

    • (v2)
  • If you go into the job with that attitude, you won’t last long.
  • Doctors say that she probably won't last out the night (= she will probably die before the morning).

    • (determiner)
  • last
  • The last time I saw him was in May.
  • This last point is crucial.
    		•
    underpaid /ˌʌn.dəˈpeɪd/ (adj) bị trả lương bèo bọt, không tương xứng với công sức bỏ ra (earning less money than you deserve for your work)
    illuminate /ɪˈluː.mə.neɪt/ (v) chiếu sáng (to light something and make it brighter)
  • The room was illuminated by candles.
  • The streets were illuminated with strings of coloured lights.
  • The earth is illuminated by the sun.
  • Floodlights illuminated the stadium.
    		•
    initiative /ɪˈnɪʃə.t̬ɪv/ (n) sự chủ động khi gặp phải một vấn đề, tự túc, tự mình đưa ra quyết định (the ability to make decisions and take action without waiting for someone to tell you what to do)
  • Don’t keep asking me for advice. Use your initiative.
  • She did it on her own initiative.
  • Employees are encouraged to use their initiative if faced with a problem.
