Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
intimate /ˈɪn.tɪ.mət/ (adj) thân mật
  • private and friendly so that you feel comfortable
  • having an extremely close friendship

    		•
  • The restaurant has a very intimate atmosphere.
  • an intimate meal for two
  • an intimate relationship
    		•
    vulnerable /ˈvʌl.nərə.bəl/ (adj) (weak and easily hurt physically or emotionally)
  • I felt very vulnerable, standing there without any clothes on.
  • Older people are especially vulnerable to cold temperatures even inside their homes.
  • The wound is vulnerable to infection.
  • While the base is vulnerable, we'll attack.
    		•
    intrigued /ɪnˈtriːɡd/ (adj) tò mò, hiếu kì muốn biết (very interested in something because it seems strange or mysterious)
    intrigued to know/learn etc
  • She was intrigued to know what he planned to do next.
  • His classmates were all intrigued to learn about and see his ducks.
  • I'm intrigued to know what you thought of the movie.
    • YT: EF International Language Campuses
    snuggle /ˈsnʌɡəl/ (v) rúc, xích gần, ôm ấp (to move yourself into a warm and comfortable position, especially close to somebody)
  • The children snuggled up to their mother to get warm.
    		•
    interpersonal /ˌɪn.təˈpɜː.sən.əl/ (adj) giữa các cá nhân với nhau (connected with relationships between people)
  • interpersonal skills
  • A car salesman must have excellent interpersonal skills in order to persuade customers to purchase a car.
  • Some colleges teach interpersonal skills where students learn to interact with their peers.
    		•
    playful /ˈpleɪ.fəl/ (adj) vui vẻ, đùa
  • full of fun; wanting to play
  • made or done in fun; not serious

    		•
  • My mother was in a playful mood that day.
  • He gave her a playful punch on the arm.
  • She gave him a playful push.
    		•
    affectionate /əˈfek.ʃən.ət/ (adj) trìu mến; tình cảm (showing in a gentle way that you love someone and care about them)
  • an affectionate
  • Tom is affectionate.
  • My mother wasn't very affectionate with us when I was young, so I'm not used to hugging and stuff.
  • (n) affection: tình cảm, sự quan tâm, yêu thương, yêu mến (a feeling of liking or love and caring)
  • He didn't show his wife any affection.
  • She felt no affection for the child.
  • He had a deep affection for his aunt.
  • Their father never showed them much affection.
    		•
    mushy /ˈmʌʃi/ (infomal)
    - When someone is thinking clearly they are said to be "sharp"
    - My brain is mushy = I'm unable to think clearly or well (bối rối, rối bời)
    thrive /θraɪv/ (adj) phát triển (to grow, develop, or be successful)
    thrive - throve - thriven
  • Industries such as water, telecoms, and insurance have been thriving.
    		•
    yearn /jɜːn/ (v) mong mỏi, khao khát (to want something very much, especially when it is very difficult to get)
  • She yearned for children of her own.
  • She yearned to escape from her office job.
  • Hannah yearned for a child.
    		•
