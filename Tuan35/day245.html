Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
elapse /iˈlæps/ (v) (quãng thời gian) trôi qua (if a period of time elapses, it passes)
  • Four years had elapsed since she last saw him.
    		•
    wander /ˈwɑːn.dɚ/ (v) đi lang thang, đi loanh quanh (to walk around slowly in a relaxed way or without any clear purpose or direction)
    (syn) range, roam
  • I was just wandering around the house.
  • They wandered down the street.
  • I was bored, so I wandered around town.
    		•
    on second thoughts /θɔːt/ (phrase) (used when you want to change something that you have just said, often to say the opposite)
  • We don’t need an umbrella. On second thoughts, maybe we do.
  • Can I have a cup of coffee, please? - actually, on second thoughts, I'll have a beer.
  • I’ll call her tomorrow – no, on second thought, I’ll try now.
    		•
    conventional /kənˈven.ʃən.əl/ (adj) theo cách truyền thống, thông thường (traditional and ordinary)
  • a conventional wedding
  • conventional
  • To win the marketing job, we’ll need to let go of our conventional ideas and think outside the box.
  • A microwave cooks food much faster than a conventional oven.
    		•
    accelerate /əkˈselə.reɪt/ (v) tăng tốc (when a vehicle or its driver accelerates, the speed of the vehicle increases)
  • The car can accelerate from 0 to 60 miles per hour in just 6 seconds.
  • The van driver accelerated every time I tried to overtake him.
  • I accelerated to overtake the bus.
  • (v, opp) decelerate: giảm tốc
  • The speeding man tried to decelerate when he saw the police officer parked beside the road.
    		•
    discriminate /dɪˈskrɪm.ə.neɪt/ (v)
    1. phân biệt (to recognize that there is a difference between people or things; to show a difference between people or things)

    2. phân biệt đối xử (to treat one person or group worse/better than another in an unfair way)
    		 1.
    (syn) distinguish
  • The computer program was unable to discriminate between letters and numbers.

    • 2.
  • It was found that the company still discriminated on the basis of race in promotions.
  • Don't discriminate against people based on nationality, gender, or occupation.
  • My employer is a fair man who does not discriminate and treats everyone as his equal.
    		•
    moderate (adj):
    /ˈmɒdərət/
    (v):
    /ˈmɑː.də.reɪt/    		 (adj) vừa phải (neither very nor very )
    (v) giảm bớt, hạ bớt
    		 (adj)
  • Cook the sauce over a moderate heat.
  • The hotel was a moderate price.
  • Moderate exercise stimulates the circulation of blood.

    • (v)
  • We agreed to moderate our original demands.
  • She apologized immediately and moderated her voice.
    		•
    conductor /kənˈdʌk·tər/ (n)
    1. (US) nhân viên xe lửa (a person who is in charge of a train and travels with it, but does not drive it)

    2. (UK) phụ xe (a person whose job is to collect money from passengers on a bus or train or check their tickets)
