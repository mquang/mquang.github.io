Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
take place /pleɪs/ (v) diễn ra, xảy ra (to happen)
  • The concert takes place next Thursday.
  • The story takes place in the 18th century.
  • The film festival takes place in October.
    • Một số câu mình trích trong Part 3, 4 của đề thi:
  • What change has taken place at Pacific Trends?
  • Where does the conversation most likely take place?
  • What will take place on Friday?
    		•
    compete /kəmˈpit/ (v) cạnh tranh (to try to be more successful or better than someone else who is trying to do the same as you)
  • Several companies are competing for the contract.
  • The two athletes are competing for the gold medal.
    • Chú ý: Cẩn thận nhầm với dạng động từ của complete (hoàn thành):

    (v-ing) competing - completing
    (v-ed) competed - completed
  • (n) competition: sự cạnh tranh, ganh đua, cuộc thi (thể dục thể thao)
  • He usually wins the running competition.
  • The competition takes place in Athens.
  • The competition is open to all players.
  • (adj) competitive: hiếu thắng
    (wanting very much to win or be more successful than other people)
  • She is very competitive.
  • She's fiercely competitive and gets very upset if she loses at anything.
  • (n) competitor: đối thủ, đối thủ cạnh tranh
  • Their prices are better than any of their competitors.
  • How many competitors took part in the race?
  • We produce cheaper goods than our competitors.
    		•
    competent /ˈkɑm.pə.t̮ənt/ (adj) đủ khả năng, đủ trình độ (having enough skill or knowledge to do something well or to the necessary standard)
  • You must prove you are a competent driver before you can get your driver’s license.
  • I don't feel competent to comment.
  • Each person can learn to be competent in almost anything.
  • (adj) incompetent: không đủ khả năng, không đủ năng lực, kém cỏi

  • (n) competence: năng lực, khả năng
  • Students can prove their competence by performing well in the exam.
    		•
    experience /ɪkˈspɪri.əns/ (n) kinh nghiệm, trải nghiệm
    (v) trải qua
  • My lack of practical experience was a disadvantage.
  • Do you have any previous experience with this type of work?
  • In my experience, people generally smile back if you smile at them.
  • Do you have any experience working with children?
  • He has little experience using this program.
  • She gained experience through volunteer work.
  • Customers have experienced problems in finding parking places at the mall.
  • I had a lot of interesting experiences during my year in Africa.
    		•
    experiment /ɪkˈspɛ.rə.mənt/ (n) sự thí nghiệm, thực nghiệm, cuộc thử
    (v) thí nghiệm, thử nghiệm
  • We can find the best solution by experiment.
  • The school is experimenting with new teaching methods.
  • I wish the government would stop experimenting with new teaching methods for our kids every couple of years.
  • Scientists have experimented with liquids and gels in which plants can grow artificially.
