Verb + full infinitive or -ing form with a change in meaning
Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
remember /rɪˈmem.bər/ (v)
+ full infinitive: nhớ làm việc gì theo như dự định (do something you are/were planning to do)

+ verb-ing: nhớ về một việc/sự kiện đã xảy ra trong quá khứ (think of a past event)		 + full infinitive
  • I'm glad I remembered to do my homework
  • Remember to take your P.E. clothes to school.

    • + verb-ing
  • I remember teachers at my school hitting children when they were naughty!
  • I don't remember signing a contract.
  • I remember meeting her at a party once.
  • I remember my father bringing home a huge Christmas tree.
    		•
    forget /fəˈɡet/ (v)
    + full infinitive: quên mất không làm việc gì theo như dự định (not do something you are/were planning to do)

    + verb-ing: quên một việc/sự kiện đã xảy ra trong quá khứ (not able to remember a past event)
    forget - forgot - forgotten
    + full infinitive
  • I forgot to turn up for my driving test yesterday. How silly!

    • + verb-ing
  • I'll never forget taking my driving test for the first time. It was awful!
  • She would never forget seeing the Himalayas for the first time.
    		•
    try /traɪ/ (v)
    + full infinitive: cố gắng (make an effort to achieve something)

    + verb-ing: thử (do something as an experiment to solve a problem)    		 + full infinitive
  • I tried to open the window.
  • We must try to spend less money.
  • She can be really irritating but I try to ignore her.

    • + verb-ing
  • If you don't know what that word means, try looking it up in a dictionary.
  • Maybe you should try getting up earlier.
  • Try using a different shampoo.
    		•
    mean /miːn/ (v)
    + full infinitive: định, có ý định (to intend)

    + verb-ing: đồng nghĩa với việc
    (adj)
    1. hà tiện, bủn xỉn (not willing to give or share things, especially money)

    2. bẩn tính, xấu tính, đối xử không ra gì
    (of people or their behaviour) crual or unkind
    mean - meant - meant
    + full infinitive
  • Do you think she meant to say 9 a.m. instead of 9 p.m.?
  • I didn’t mean to make you cry.
  • He meant to invite Monica, but he clean forgot.

    • + verb-ing
  • Being at university often means learning to live on your own.
  • Working in London means leaving home at 6.30.
  • If we want to catch the 7.30 train, that will mean leaving the house at 6.00.

    • (adj1)
  • She was too mean to put the heating on.
  • He's a mean man who never gives presents to anyone.

    • (adj2)
  • He's so mean to his mother!
  • Don’t be so mean to her!
  • It was mean of them not to invite her to the party.
    		•
    go on (v)
    + full infinitive: chuyển qua (to move to the next thing or stage, to start another)

    + verb-ing: tiếp tục làm việc gì (to continue)    		 + full infinitive
  • Whichever page you're on, go on to read the next chapter.
  • She admitted her company's responsibility for the disaster and went on to explain how compensation would be paid to the victims.
  • Jared attended Chichester Comprehensive and then went on to study philosophy at Cambridge.

    • + verb-ing
  • I won’t go on working in this job forever.
  • We really can't go on living like this - we'll have to find a bigger house.
  • The kids went on laughing even after the teacher had told them to stop.
    		•
    stop /stɑːp/ (v)
    + full infinitive: tạm dừng để làm việc gì (interrupt an action to do something else)

    + verb-ing: dừng việc gì lại (stop an action)    		 + full infinitive
  • I stopped to pick up a letter that I'd dropped.
  • I was busy writing an essay but I had to stop to answer your phone.
  • We stopped to drink a cup of coffee.

    • + verb-ing
  • Stop shouting - you're giving me a headache!
  • I couldn't stop laughing.
