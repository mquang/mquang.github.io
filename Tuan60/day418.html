depreciate /dɪˈpriː.ʃi.eɪt/ (v) mất giá (to cause something to lose value, especially over time)
  • Our car depreciated (by) $1,500 in the first year we owned it.
  • Shares continued to depreciate on the stock markets today.
  • The dollar’s depreciation will lead to higher inflation and interest rates, hurting the economy.
    		•
    be deprecated /ˈdep.rə.keɪt/ (từ vựng công nghệ thông tin) (cảnh báo về hàm, phương thức, tính năng) sẽ bị thay thế/loại bỏ, không còn trong các phiên bản trong tương lai; khuyên lập trình viên nên ngừng việc sử dụng hàm/phương thức/tính năng cũ này mà hãy sử dụng lựa chọn thay thế mới hơn
    (of a software feature) to be considered outdated and best avoided, even though you can still use it, usually because it has been replaced with a newer feature or it will be removed in the future
  • This function is deprecated.
  • A number of features have been deprecated in the latest version of the software.
  • Deprecated in general means "don't use it".
    		•
    duty roster /ˈrɑː.stɚ/ (n) phân công công việc (a list of people's names with their work schedules)
  • There is a weekly duty roster.
    		•
    subsequent /ˈsʌb.sɪ.kwənt/ (adj) sau, tiếp theo (happening or coming after something else)
  • These skills were passed on to subsequent generations.
  • The first meeting will be in the City Hall, but all subsequent meetings will be held in the school.
  • Developments on this issue will be dealt with in a subsequent report.
  • This will be discussed in subsequent chapters.
    		•
    pending /ˈpen·dɪŋ/ (preposition, adj) đang chờ
  • He is being held in jail pending trial.
  • His application for citizenship was still pending.
  • More employees are likely to lose jobs in the pending merger.
    		•
    ornament /ˈɔːr.nə.mənt/ (n) vật trang trí, đồ trang trí, làm cảnh (an object that you keep in your house because it is beautiful rather than useful)
    (v) trang trí
    		 (n)
  • garden ornaments such as statues and fountains
  • The 3-year-old child hung the blue sparkling globe on the Christmas tree because it was his favorite ornament.

    • (v)
    (syn) decorate
  • She ornamented her letters with little drawings in the margin.
  • (adj) ornamental: để trang trí (used as decoration rather than for a practical purpose)
  • The buttons on the sleeves are just ornamental.
    		•
    trophy /ˈtroʊ.fi/ (n) chiếc cúp (vô định)
  • the NCAA championship trophy
  • The club haven't won a major trophy for the past three seasons.
  • Portugal will be defending the trophy they won last year.
    		•
    inscribe /ɪnˈskrɑɪb/ (v) viết lên trang giấy, sách; chạm khắc lên mặt đồng, đá, bạc... (to write words in a book or carve (= cut) them on an object)
  • name is inscribed on the base of the trophy.
  • (n) inscription: dòng chữ (được viết, chạm khắc) (a piece of writing inscribed on a stone, in the front of a book etc)
  • The monument carries the inscription: "To the fallen in two world wars".
