    pluck /plʌk/ (v) ngắt, giật, nhổ, vặt (lông gà)
    - to pull something, especially with a sudden movement, in order to remove it
    - to remove the feathers from a chicken or other bird so that it can be cooked and eaten
  • She plucked an apple off the tree.

  • She plucked her eyebrows.
  • pluck a feather from a bird

  • The truth turned out to be stranger than we had expected.

  • She thought she was pregnant, but it turned out to be a false alarm.
  • As it turned out (=used to say what happened in the end), he passed the exam quite easily.
    		•

    curl /kɝːl/ (v) làm xoăn, uốn xoăn, uốn quăn; cuộn lại, cuộn quanh (thân cây)
  • Mary was busy curling her hair.
  • Ivy curled round the tree.
    		•
    loath /loʊθ/ (adj) ngại, thực sự là không muốn, không thích (to be unwilling to do something, do not want to do something)
    be loath to do something
  • He was loath to admit his mistake.
  • I am loath to go to a party where I don’t know anyone.
  • The sleepy child is loath to wake up for school.
  • The atheist was loath to go to church with his family.
  • I was loath to leave the party because it was so much fun.
