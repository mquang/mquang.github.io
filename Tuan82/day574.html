Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
put up (ph.v)
1. treo, đóng, dựng, xây dựng

2. tăng giá

3. cho ai đó ở nhờ (to let someone stay in your house)

4. bỏ ra, chi ra
 1.
  • to put up a
  • I put a few posters up to make the room look less bare.
  • They’re planning to put a hotel up where the museum used to be.

    • 2.
  • Several of the banks have decided to put up their interest rates.
  • They've put up the rent by £20 a month.

    • 3.
  • Could you put me up for the night when I come to London?

    • 4.
  • Dad put $1000 up to help me buy a car.
  • The family has put up £15,000 towards the cost of the child’s medical treatment.
    		•
    jot down (ph.v) ghi chép lại nhanh (to write something quickly on a piece of paper so that you remember it)
  • Let me just grab a pen so I can jot that number down.
  • This is important. Please jot this down.
  • Could you quickly jot down the directions so we won't get lost driving to the vineyard?
  • Could someone please jot some notes down at the meeting for me?
    		•
    sort out (ph.v)
    1. dọn dẹp, sắp xếp lại

    2. giải quyết
    		 1.
  • The cupboards need sorting out.
  • I need to sort out the mess on my desk.

    • 2.
  • This matter could be sorted out if they would just sit down and talk.
  • I've spent nearly an hour on this math problem, but I still can't sort it out.
    		•
    use up (ph.v) dùng hết, sử dụng hết
  • I’ve used up all my holiday entitlement, and it’s only August.
  • I can't believe you used up all of my shampoo and just left the empty bottle in the shower!
  • Don't use up all the milk - we need some for breakfast.
  • Andy's just had a bath and used up all the hot water.
    		•
    break down (ph.v)
    1. chia nhỏ

    2. hỏng

    3. phá
    		 1.
  • The amount doesn’t seem quite so bad when you break it down into monthly payments.
  • I know it can be confusing, but once I break it down for you, I think you'll start to get it.
  • It’s easier to handle the job if you break it down into several specific assignments.

    • 2.
  • She didn't come to the party because her car broke down on the way here.
  • The telephone system has broken down.

    • 3.
  • Firefighters had to break down the door to get into the flat.
  • Firefighters had to break the door down to reach the people trapped inside.
    		•
    come apart (ph.v) bung, bục, toác, lìa rời; (có thể) tách rời
  • to be separated into ; break apart
  • to be capable of being separated into parts

    		•
  • My boots are coming apart at the seams.
  • I picked up the book and it came apart in my hands.
  • The bed comes apart so you can store it when you don’t need it.
  • The windows come apart for easy cleaning.
    		•
    count on somebody/ something (ph.v) trông chờ, trông cậy vào (to depend on someone to do what you want or expect them to do for you)
  • I'm counting on you to help me.
  • A lot of people count on these welfare payments to get by each month.
  • I was counting on the money from the insurance to buy a new house, after my house burned down.
    		•
    fall out (ph.v)
    1. (răng/tóc) rụng

    2. cạch mặt nhau (to stop being friendly with someone because you have had a disagreement with them)
    		 1.
  • A side effect of the treatment is that your hair starts to fall out.
  • My daughter is so excited that her two front teeth have fallen out.

    • 2.
  • He left home after falling out with his parents.
  • The two fell out over coaching tactics a long time ago.
    		•
