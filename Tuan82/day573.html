Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
fond of (phrase) thích, yêu thích, ưa thích, có tình cảm gắn bó
  • liking and caring about someone very much, especially as a result of knowing them well or for a long time
  • getting enjoyment and satisfaction from something, especially often or over a long time

    		•
  • Over the years, I have grown quite fond of her.
  • We had grown fond of the house and didn't want to leave.
  • Sheila's very fond of telling other people what to do.
    		•
    chip in (ph.v) đóng góp (to give some money, esp. when several people are giving money to pay for something together)
  • I coundn't go to the party, but I still wanted to chip in for a gift.
  • If everyone chips in we'll be able to buy her a really nice present.
  • They each chipped in $50 to take their parents out to dinner.
    		•
    come down with (ph.v) bị ốm (to start to suffer from an illness, especially one that is not serious)
  • He wanted to join us, but unfortunately he came down with the flu yesterday.
    		•
    keep on (ph.v) tiếp tục duy trì, tiếp diễn (to continue doing something)
  • If you keep on practicing, you'll get better!
  • My sister kept on asking me question after question.
  • The rain kept on all night.
  • Keep on until you get to the church.
  • I'm going to call the cops on our neighbors if they keep on making such a racket.
  • (ph.v, syn) keep going/ keep on going: tiếp tục duy trì, tiếp diễn
  • If things keep going like this, we'll have to close the business.
  • The hikers intended to keep on going until nightfall.
    		•
    fill in (ph.v) bù lấp khoảng trống ()
  • to add information such as your name or address in the empty spaces on an official document
  • to give someone details about something
  • to do someone’s job for them while they are away

    		•  1.
  • Please fill in your name and address in the space provided.
  • I spent over two hours filling in the application form.

    • 2.
  • Did Sam fill you in about the new project?
  • My secretary will fill you in on the details.

    • 3.
  • I'm not her regular secretary - I'm just filling in.
  • The gym teacher was sick today, so a substitute filled in for her.
    		•
    call for (ph.v) yêu cầu (to need, require, or demand something or the services of someone)
    		 1.
  • The opposition has called for an investigation into the misuse of public funds.
  • Protesters were calling for a ban on the production of GM foods.
  • He called for the results of the survey to be made public.

    • 2.
  • The job calls for excellent computer skills.
    		•
    factor in (ph.v) tính đến, suy xét đến (to include or consider a particular fact when you are planning or calculating something)
  • Her salary is not that high when childcare costs and medical insurance are factored in.
  • The boss factored in overtime when giving out bonuses.
    		•
    to die for (informal) quá hấp dẫn, làm mình mong muốn có chết đi được, có chết để có được nó cũng được (so good that you want to have it very much; desirable enough to die for; worth dying for)
  • The desserts in this restaurant are to die for!
  • The cabin was built above the lake, and it has a view to die for.
  • She was wearing a dress to die for.
    		•
