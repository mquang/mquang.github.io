deceive /dɪˈsiːv/ (v)
1. lừa, lừa dối (to trick someone by behaving in a dishonest way)

2. làm ai đó lầm tưởng (to make somebody have a wrong idea about somebody/something)
 1.
(syn) cheat, trick
  • Her husband had been deceiving her for years.
  • Don’t be deceived – she’s not as nice as she seems.
  • The company deceived customers by selling old computers as new ones.

    • 2.
    (syn) mislead
  • Unless my eyes deceive me, that's his .
  • Don’t be deceived by the new cover – this is a rehash of old hits.
  • The sound of the door closing deceived me into thinking they had gone out.
  • (adj) deceptive: lừa dối, lừa đảo, làm lầm tưởng (making you believe something that is not true)
    • (syn) misleading
  • Online fraud involves using the internet to trick someone into giving away their money or data. It takes many forms, from deceptive emails which trick us into paying money to the wrong bank account, to the theft of credit card details.
  • The packaging was deliberately deceptive to convince consumers that the product was worth the cost.
  • (adj) deceitful: lươn lẹo, dối trá (behaving in a dishonest way by telling lies and making people believe things that are not true)
  • She is trying to decide whether to give her deceitful husband a second chance.
