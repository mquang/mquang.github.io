religion /rɪˈlɪ.dʒən/ (n) tôn giáo, tín ngưỡng
  • The book explores the relationship between religion and civilization.
  • What do you think is the role of religion in the modern world?
  • I don't believe in religion.
  • He has a good knowledge of Japanese religion.
    		•
    region /ˈriːdʒən/ (n) vùng, khu vực, miền
  • a mountainous region
  • Do you think the various craft remind people of a specific region?
  • The two regions differ in religion and culture.
  • Tibet is the highest region in the world, with an average altitude of 4,875 meters.
  • Mosquitoes have been found in the coldest regions of Siberia.

  • - Where are you from?
    - I grew up in Bedfordshire, a region near London!
  • (idiom) (somewhere) in the region of: khoảng, xấp xỉ (= approximately)
  • He earns somewhere in the region of €50 000.
  • They estimate that the temperature yesterday was (somewhere) in the region of -30°C.
  • The cost of the plan would be in the region of $40 to $60 billion.
  • We're going to have to spend in the region of $20,000 for this renovation.
    		•
    accommodation /əˌkɑːməˈdeɪ.ʃn/ (n) chỗ trọ, chỗ ở
  • They paid for his flights and hotel accommodations.
  • The cost of student accommodations is rising steadily.
  • The cost, including flights, accommodation, and meals, was €100,000.
  • (v) accommodate: chứa (ngày 4)
    		•
    accompany /əˈkʌm.pə.ni/ (v) đi theo, đi cùng, đi kèm
  • His wife accompanied him on the trip.
  • Visitors to the Sydney Orgo Lab facilities must be accompanied by an employee at all times.
  • The course books are accompanied by four CDs.
  • Would you like me to accompany you to your room?
  • I have two tickets for the theatre on Saturday evening - would you like to accompany me?
    • Would like là 1 cách nói trịnh trọng, polite hơn want:
    - Would you like ...
    - Would you mind ... (bạn có phiền nếu ...)
    - Do you want ...

    I would love to (rất muốn) mang sắc thái mạnh hơn I would like to (muốn)
    accumulate /əˈkjuːmjə.leɪt/ (v) tích lũy, tích lại, gom góp lại
  • I seem to have accumulated a lot of books.
  • After a long career in the antiques trade, Harry had accumulated many rare treasures.
  • (n) accumulator: ắc quy
    		•
    accurate /ˈækjə.rət/ (adj) đúng, chính xác

  • My watch is not very accurate.
  • Accurate measurement is very important in science.
  • Her novel is an accurate reflection of life in Spain.
  • (n) accuracy: độ chính xác
  • She says she can type 85 words per minute with 90% accuracy.
