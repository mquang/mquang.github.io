excellent /ˈek.sə.lənt/ (adj) xuất sắc, ưu tú, tuyệt vời
  • The food was excellent.
  • Tom is an excellent lawyer.
  • The restaurant is well known for its friendly atmosphere and excellent service.
  • He has excellent despite his age.
  • She is an excellent .
  • (n) excellence: sự xuất sắc, ưu tú, trội
  • This college has a long tradition of athletic excellence.
  • His excellence in baseball earned him a scholarship
  • (v) excel + in/at: trội về, xuất sắc về (môn gì)
  • She has always excelled in foreign languages.
  • As a child he excelled at music and art.
  • I have nothing I feel I can excel at.

    complement /ˈkɑm.pləˌmɛnt/ (n)
  • phần bù
  • phần bổ sung
  • bổ ngữ
    • (v) bổ sung cho nhau/đi kèm lẫn nhau tạo nên sự hoàn hảo
  • The team needs players who complement each other.
  • The music complements her voice perfectly.
  • This wine is a nice complement to grilled dishes.
    • Rượu này mà uống kèm với mấy món nướng thì tuyệt vời.
  • They complement one another in unexpected ways, creating a system that thrives on its own success.
  • 2's complement
    • Chú ý:
    2 góc are complementary là 2 góc phụ nhau (tổng = 90°), chứ không phải bù nhau
    compliment /ˈkɑm.plə.mənt/ (n) lời khen ngợi
    (v) khen ngợi, ca tụng
  • I was embarrassed by their compliment.
  • Are you fishing for (= trying to get) compliments?
  • I don't like the way Tom is always fishing for a compliment.
  • I've gotten a lot of compliments today.
  • (adj) complimentary:
    1. (được) khen ngợi

    2. miễn phí     (given free to people)
    • 1.
  • The reviews of his latest film have been highly complimentary.
  • Everything I've heard about your work has been highly complimentary.
  • Our guests said some very complimentary things about the meal I'd cooked.

    • 2.
  • There was a complimentary bottle of champagne in the hotel room.
  • I’ve got some complimentary tickets for the theatre tonight.
    • (synonym)
    + (v) commend
    + (n, v)praise
    quantity /ˈkwɑn.tə.t̮i/ (n) số lượng
    (n-s) quantities
  • The police found a quantity of drugs at his home.
  • We consumed vast quantities of food and drink that night.
  • A large quantity of beer was sold.
  • (v) quantify: định lượng, xác định số lượng, lượng tử hóa
  • It's difficult to quantify how many people will be affected by the change.
  • (n, grammar) quantifiers: định lượng từ, từ chỉ số lượng (many, much, some, a few, a lot of, lost of, plenty of...)
    		•
    quality /ˈkwɑ.lə.t̮i/ (n)
    1. chất lượng

    2. đức tính, phẩm chất
  • I only buy good-quality wine.
  • The quality of the picture on our television isn't very good.
  • I don't think he has the right qualities to be a teacher.
  • We’re looking for someone who loves children and has the qualities of a good teacher.
  • (v) qualify: đủ điều kiện, đủ tư cách, đủ khả năng, đủ tiêu chuẩn (FINISH TRAINING)
  • This training course will qualify you for a better job.
  • He qualified as a vet last year.
  • This course qualifies you to teach in any secondary school.
  • She hopes to qualify (as a lawyer) at the end of the year.
  • It takes three years to qualify as a social worker.
  • (n) qualification:
    1. chứng chỉ, bằng cấp

    2. kĩ năng, kinh nghiệm
    • 1.
  • You'll never get a good job if you don't have any qualifications.
  • We are looking for graduates with qualifications in maths or science.

    • 2.
  • Some nursing experience is a necessary qualification for this job.
