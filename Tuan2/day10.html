Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
instead of /ɪnˈsted/ (phrasal preposition) thay vì, thay thế cho ai, cái gì hoặc điều gì
instead of + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • You can go instead of me, if you want.
  • You waste a lot of water by having a bath instead of a shower.
  • Instead of complaining, why don't we try to change things?
  • The government wants more people to use public transport instead of private cars.
  • Instead of sleeping, I'd rather study English.
    		•
    in spite of /spaɪt/ (phrasal preposition) mặc dù
    in spite of + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • He got the job in spite of his prison record.
  • He was very fast in spite of being overweight.
  • They fell in love in spite of the language barrier.
  • We went out in spite of the rain.
    • synonym:
    + despite cũng + N/V-ing (có thể dùng thay thế cho in spite of
    + though
    + although+ mệnh đề
    + even though
    though mang nghĩa thân mật hơn although, xem phim mình hay nghe nó nói though ở cuối câu, lúc này though mang nghĩa giống however/nevertheless
  • He liked the hat. He decided not to buy it, though.
  • My friend is very nice. She can be a little bossy at times, though.





    • Khi though không nằm ở cuối câu, though, although, even though có thể thay thế cho nhau (interchangeable).
    Even though nhấn mạnh sự trái ngược hơn so với although.
  • She is very beautiful, even though she is fifty years old.
    		•
    in addition to /əˈdɪʃən/ (phrasal preposition) ngoài
    in addition to + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • In addition to the twins, Jason has another child by his first wife.
  • In addition to my weekly wage, I got a lot of tips.
  • In addition to his flat in London, he has a villa in Italy and a castle in Scotland.
  • In addition to teaching basic academic skills, we offer a large variety of activities for students.
  • In addition to his regular job here, he works in a store downtown three nights a week.
    		•
    Despite /dɪˈspaɪt/ (preposition) mặc dù
    despite + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • Despite the bad weather, we enjoyed ourselves.
  • Despite the adverse conditions, the road was finished in just eight months.
  • Despite his lack of experience, he got the job.
  • Despite his lack of stature, he became a successful footballer.
  • Despite eating McDonalds regularly Mary remained slim.
  • He remained poor despite being a hard worker.
    		•
    because /bɪˈkɑːz/ (conjunction) vì, bởi vì
    (clause +) because + clause
  • The journey was quite quick because the road was clear.
  • Shana rescheduled her meeting because there was a conflict in her calendar.
  • I did it because he told me to.
  • Because you've done such a good job, I'm giving everyone a 10% bonus.
    		•
    because of (adverbial prepositional phrase) vì, do, bởi
    (clause +) because of + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • The flight was delayed because of bad weather.
  • We're here because of you.
  • I was late because of the traffic.
  • He was absent because of illness.
  • Because of increases in airfares and hotels prices, most salespersons will exceed their travel allowance for this month.
  • The car accident happened because of a distracted driver.
  • Ivy succeeded because of her parents’ dedication and support.
  • The company went bankrupt because of poor financial management.
    		•
    due to /duː/ (adjectival prepositional phrase) vì, do, bởi
    (be +) due to + N/cụm N hoặc V-ing
  • His weight problem is due to overeating.
  • Due to a small fire, the office will be closed temporarily.
  • The car accident was due to a distracted driver.
  • Ivy’s success is due to her parents’ dedication and support.
  • The company’s bankruptcy was due to poor financial management.
    • Khi because of và due to đi kèm với the fact that, theo sau chúng sẽ là một mệnh đề
  • Due to the fact that the weather was hot, we went to the pool.
  • Because of the fact that John studied hard, he passed the exam easily.
    		•
    be used to/get used to (phrase) quen làm gì
    be used to/get used to + N/V-ing
  • She wasn't used to it.
  • She wasn't used to driving on the left.
  • She is used to driving on the left.
  • Diane has a new job. She has to get up much earlier now than before - at 6.30. She finds this difficult because she isn't used to getting up so early.
  • I am used to getting up early in the morning.
  • You will soon get used to the work.
    • Phân biệt be used to/get used to (quen làm gì) với used to + V (đã từng là/làm gì trong quá khứ và giờ không làm nữa)
  • I used to drive to work every day, but these days I usually go by bike.
  • We used to live in a small village, but now we live in London.
  • She used to be a teacher, but now she works in publishing.
  • He is singing about a girl who used to be his.
    		•
