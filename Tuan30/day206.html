  • The videotape would have been useful to establish alibis for the defendants.

    looking for a needle in a haystack /ˈniː.dəl/
    /ˈheɪ.stæk/    		 (idiom) mò kim đáy bể (something that is impossible or extremely difficult to find, especially because the area you have to search is too large)
  • I went into my younger brother's bedroom to get the CD I lent him. It was like looking for a needle in a haystack. His room is so untidy.
  • I tried to find my lost key on the sandy beach, but it was like trying to find a needle in a haystack.
  • Trying to find my contact lens on the floor was like looking for a needle in a haystack.
  • (n) needle:
    1. cây kim (kim khâu, kim đan)

    2. mũi kim (của kim tiêm)

    3. kim của cân, đồng hồ, la bàn
  • Here, your eyes are better than mine - could you thread (= put thread through) this needle for me?
  • Be careful with that needle - it has a very sharp point.

So I decided to try my luck and for the first time in my whole life, I was the first to start a conversation with a guy.

After a couple more messages, he asked me to add him as a friend on Facebook and suggested for both of us to delete our accounts from the dating site. That’s practically how I found my love.

He promised to arrange a place for me to stay, and pay all the expenses I’d incur there. Basically, all that I needed to do was to buy a ticket to get there – the rest was on Luke, as he promised.

Oh, he was even more handsome than I thought he would be and he was so glad to meet me. I must admit, it was a bit strange to actually meet the person you had been chatting with for months, but it was still very exciting and pleasant.

I couldn’t stop looking at him, since he seemed to be even more attractive than I thought he was, while looking through his photos on Facebook. But then Luke told me that I’d better get some rest after my flight, and that he had some stuff to do anyway, so he promised to call me a little bit later and left me there all alone.
