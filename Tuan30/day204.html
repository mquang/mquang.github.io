bell tower /ˈbel ˌtaʊ.ər/ (n) tháp chuông (a tall structure, often part of a church or large building, that contains one or more bells)
  • (n) bell: chuông (chuông bấm cửa, chuông xe đạp, quả chuông); tiếng chuông (vào lớp, kết thúc)...)
  • a bicycle bell
  • I stood at the front door and rang the bell several times.
  • The children arrived at school just as the bell was ringing.
    		•
    chime /tʃaɪm/ (v) đánh chuông, rung chuông
    (of bells) to make a clear ringing sound
    (n) tiếng vang, âm vang (a ringing sound)
    		 (n)
  • I was woken up by the chimes of the cathedral bells.
    		•
    awe /ɔː/ (n) a feeling of great respect, usually mixed with slight fear or surprise
  • I stared at the clouds, in awe of their beauty.
  • They experienced a tremendous sense of awe in the cathedral.
  • My little boy watched in awe as the fireworks display lit up the sky.
  • After I watched the action movie, I was left in awe by the spectacular special effects.
    		•
    prayer /preər/ (n)
    1. lời cầu nguyện, lời thỉnh cầu Chúa (words that someone says or thinks when they are praying to God)

    2. nghi lễ cầu nguyện, buổi cầu nguyện (a religious meeting that takes place regularly in which people say prayers)
    		 1.
  • Their prayers were answered and the child was found safe and well.
  • Does God answer our prayers?
  • I sent up a quick prayer and entered the interview room.

    • 2.
  • Students are required to attend prayers twice a week.
  • We had family prayers before breakfast.
    		•
    haphazard /ˌhæpˈhæzəd/ (adj) bừa bãi, lung tung, không có kế hoạch, bản vẽ, lịch trình cụ thể (with no particular order or plan)
  • The haphazard arrangement of different types of flowers created a surprisingly beautiful bouquet.
  • I need a closet organizer to help me fix my haphazard mess of clothing.
  • In the early years training was haphazard.
    		•
    admittedly /ədˈmɪt̬ɪd.li/ (adv) phải thừa nhận là, công nhận là (used when you are agreeing that something is true, especially unwillingly)
  • Admittedly, Venice wasn’t a very big place, but there was little chance of meeting her again accidentally.
  • Well, admittedly, I am no expert on art, but I think that is awful!
  • She is very clever, admittedly, but do you think she has the right personality for the job?
    		•
    demise /dɪˈmaɪz/ (n) dấu chấm hết (the end of something that was previously considered to be powerful, such as a business, industry, or system)
  • Online newspapers are causing the demise of the traditional newspaper.
  • The demise of the company was sudden and unexpected.
  • the demise of the typewriter in this computer age
    		•
    ultimately /ˈʌl.tə.mət.li/ (adv) cuối cùng (finally)
  • The campaign was ultimately successful.
  • You are ultimately responsible for your own actions.
  • He ultimately decided to go to college.
  • She ultimately agreed to join the group.
    		•
    fire marshal /ˈfaɪə ˌmɑː.ʃəl/ (n) the head of a department or office who is responsible for fire safety in an area, and who may also be a firefighter or police officer
    petition /pəˈtɪʃən/ (n) đơn kiến nghị
  • a document signed by a large number of people demanding or asking for some action from the government or another authority
  • a formal letter to a law court asking for a particular legal action
    (v) kiến nghị, thỉnh cầu (to make a formal request to somebody in authority)
    		•  (n)
  • More than 2000 people signed a petition to protect a wildlife area from development.
  • She's filing a petition for divorce.

    • (v)
  • They're petitioning for better facilities for disabled people.
  • She is petitioning for a re-trial.
  • I think we should petition the government to increase the grant for the project.
    		•
    repurpose /ˌriːˈpɜː.pəs/ (v) to use something for a different purpose to the one for which it was originally intended

        NARRATOR: This has to be the most epic tree house I have ever seen. Four stories high, surreal architecture, and this amazing tree swing. There's even a tree growing out of the third floor, and what looks like a penthouse suite on top. I have to know where this is.

        It was curiosity that drew this guy to the tree house.

        CHUCK SUTHERLAND: Hi, my name is Chuck Sutherland. I'm a geographer and a photographer. The minister's tree house is in Crossville, Tennessee and is arguably the world's largest tree house. It's 97 feet tall and is supported by six different trees.

        The tree house is well known to those who lived in the area, and I'd been hearing about it for some time, so I decided to see it first hand.

        NARRATOR: The tree that's poking out the third floor is actually the main support for the structure - an 80 foot tall white oak tree with a 12 foot diameter base.

        CHUCK SUTHERLAND: My first impression upon looking at the massive facade of this tree house was simply awe. It was obvious this took a lot of labor and a lot of time to make.

        NARRATOR: The tree house was named after the man who decided to build it - minister Horace Burgess. According to Horace, the idea for the tree house came to him in a vision while he was deep in prayer - a bit like Noah and his ark.

        It set him on a 14 year path to build the largest tree house in the world. But unfortunately the minister was no architect.

        CHUCK SUTHERLAND: It was also haphazard. There was no real plan to the construction. Admittedly though, that's what made it more fun. Doors wouldn't lead where you thought they would. Ladders and stairs were in unexpected places. There's a basketball goal in the central room, which also doubled as a church on Sundays.

        NARRATOR: All in all, the tree house has over 80 rooms, and that structure on the top is actually a bell tower equipped with repurposed oxygen tanks, which serve as bells that chime daily.

        It's quirky touches like this and its random architecture that made Minister's tree house such a popular destination. Sadly, it's also what led to its demise in 2012.

        CHUCK SUTHERLAND: It was absolutely a fire hazard and it was absolutely dangerous those are the things that made it fun and attracted people.

        It was the tree house's haphazard construction that ultimately led to it being close by the state of Tennessee's fire marshal.

        NARRATOR: To this day, the tree house remains closed. But over 2000 people have signed a petition to have Minister Burgess's labor of love reopened. But without the funds to fix it, it's possible no one will ever be allowed to set foot inside this forest mansion again.
