foundation /faʊnˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (n)
1. nền móng, nền tảng
  • a basic idea, principle, etc that something develops from
  • the solid layer of cement, bricks, stones etc that is put under a building to support it



    • 2. tổ chức, quỹ (an organization that is established to provide money for a particular purpose, for example for scientific research or charity)

    3. sự thành lập (the act of starting a new institution or organization)    		 1.
  • The course gives students a solid foundation in the basics of computing.
  • It took the builders three weeks to lay the foundations.

    • 2.
  • The money will go to the San Francisco AIDS Foundation.

    • 3.
    (syn) establishment
  • The organization has grown enormously since its foundation in 1955.

    margin /ˈmɑːr.dʒɪn/ (n)
    1. lề (the empty space at the side of a page, sometimes separated from the rest of the page by a vertical line)

    2. khoảng cách chênh lệch
    + số lượng (phiếu bầu/điểm) chênh lệch
    + khoảng thời gian chênh lệch
    (the difference in the number of votes, points etc that exists between the winners and the losers of a competition or election)

    3. lợi nhuận, khoản tiền chênh lệch giữa chi phí thuê/mua vào/sản xuất với khoản tiền bán ra/thu được (the difference between the cost of buying or producing something and the price that it is sold for)
    		 1.
  • If I have any comments to make, I'll write them in the margin.

    • 2.
  • The Senate approved the use of military force by a margin of 52 votes to 47.
  • She won the presidency by a wide margin.
  • He had an 18-second margin over his nearest rival.

    • 3.
  • Using cheap labour increases profit margin.

    1. khả năng hiểu (the ability to understand something)

    • 2.
