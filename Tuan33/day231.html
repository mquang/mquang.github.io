start over (phrasal verb) bắt đầu lại (to begin to do something again)
(UK) start afresh
/əˈfreʃ/
  • I’m getting a little bit too old to start over.
  • I'm looking forward to starting afresh in a new town with a new job.
  • I decided to abandon the first draft of the report and start over.
    		•
    ironic /aɪˈrɑː.nɪk/ (adj)
    1. trớ trêu (an ironic event or situation is interesting and sometimes humorous, because it is the opposite of what you would expect)

    2. mỉa, mỉa mai, châm biếm (using words that are the opposite of what you really mean, often in a joking way)    		 1.
  • It's ironic that she became a teacher—she used to hate school.
  • Your car was stolen at the police station! How ironic!
  • It’s ironic that her husband smoked for thirty years, and yet she’s the one who died of lung cancer.

    • 2.
  • an ironic comment/reply
  • (n) irony: sự mỉa mai, châm biếm, sự trớ trêu
  • The wit and irony of the original novel has been lost in the film version.
  • She tried to ignore the heavy irony in his voice.
    		•
    impact /ˈɪm.pækt/ (n, v) ảnh hưởng, tác động
  • (n) the effect or influence that an event, situation etc has on someone or something
  • (v) to have an on something

    		•  (n)
  • Higher mortgage rates have already had a major impact on spending.
  • an international meeting to consider the environmental impacts of global warming
  • Winning this competition could have a big impact on my life.
  • Social networks are making a huge impact on sales.

    • (v)
  • Her father's death impacted greatly on her childhood years.
    		•
    stubborn /ˈstʌb.ən/ (adj) ương ngạnh, cứng đầu, ngoan cố, cố chấp (refusing to change your mind, even when people think you are wrong or are being unreasonable)
  • He was too stubborn to admit that he was wrong.
  • He's very stubborn and it drives me mad sometimes.
  • He’s sick, but he’s too stubborn to see a doctor.
    		•
    wealthy /ˈwel.θi/ (adj) giàu có (rich)
  • He's a very wealthy man.
  • She comes from a very wealthy family.
  • (n) wealth: tài sản, gia tài (a large amount of money or valuable possessions that someone has)
  • During a successful business career, she accumulated a great amount of wealth.
  • His personal wealth is estimated at around $100 million.
    		•
    deteriorate /dɪˈtɪə.ri.ə.reɪt/ (v) xấu đi (to become worse)
  • Her health deteriorated rapidly and she died shortly afterwards.
  • She was taken into hospital last week when her condition suddenly deteriorated.
    		•
    glance /ɡlæns/ (v) liếc nhìn, nhìn thoáng qua (to give a quick short look)
    (n) cái nhìn thoáng qua, liếc qua (a quick short look)
    		 (v)
  • I glanced at my watch for the 20th time that morning.
  • He glanced at the letter and then tossed it into the bin.

    • (n)
  • At first glance the contract seemed to be fine.
  • I had a quick glance at the article, but I haven’t read it yet.
