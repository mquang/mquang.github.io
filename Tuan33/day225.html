Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
in jeopardy /ˈdʒep.ə.di/ (idiom) có nguy cơ gặp nguy hiểm hoặc bị phá hỏng (in danger of being lost or harmed)
  • The lives of thousands of birds are in jeopardy as a result of the oil spill.
  • The civil war has put thousands of lives in jeopardy.
  • Thousands of jobs could be in jeopardy.
  • (v) jeopardize: gây nguy hiểm, rủi ro, hủy hoại
  • He would never do anything to jeopardize his career.
  • A scandal like this might jeopardize his political career.
    • YouTube: Learn English With TV Series
    outskirts /ˈaʊt.skɝːts/ (n) vùng ngoại ô (khu vực ven đô, rìa nội thành của một thành phố)
    (the areas that form the edge of a town or city)
  • The factory is in/on the outskirts of New Delhi.
  • They live on the outskirts of Milan.
    		•
    intrusive /ɪnˈtruː.sɪv/ (adj) phiền phức, quấy rầy (affecting someone’s private life or interrupting them in an unwanted and annoying way)
  • intrusive
  • intrusive press photographers
    		•
    reluctant /rɪˈlʌk.tənt/ (adj) lưỡng lự (hesitating before doing something because you do not want to do it or because you are not sure that it is the right thing to do)
  • She persuaded her reluctant husband to take a trip to Florida with her.
  • I'm a bit reluctant to get involved.
  • Maddox was reluctant to talk about it.
  • Students may feel reluctant to ask questions.
    		•
    aura /ˈɔːrə/ (n) khí chất, hào quang (a feeling or particular quality that is very noticeable and seems to surround a person or place)
  • The building retains an aura of mystery.
  • The woods have an aura of mystery.
  • The picture seemed to take on the aura of an ancient work of art.
  • The sunlight created an aura of beauty around her.
    		•
    content (adj)
    /kənˈtent/
    (n)
    /ˈkɑːn.tent/    		 (adj) hài lòng (pleased with your situation and not hoping for change or improvement)
    (ns) nội dung, những thứ bên trong
  • the things that are written in a letter, book etc
  • the things that are inside a box, bag, room etc

    • (n) hàm lượng (the amount of a substance that is contained in something else, especially food or drink)    		 (adj)
  • He seems fairly content with (his) life.
  • I'd be content with a modest income.
  • I am content with my job, my home, and my family.

    • (ns)
  • She kept the contents of the letter a secret.
  • Most of the gallery’s contents were damaged in the fire.
  • Fire has caused severe damage to the contents of the building.
  • It’s easy to update the content of the Web site.
  • If you want to make money from online advertising, you need content.

    • (n)
  • food with a high fat content
  • the fat content of cheese
    		•
    illusion /ɪˈluː.ʒən/ (n) ảo tưởng, ảo giác
  • a false idea or belief, especially about somebody or about a situation
  • (ảo giác về thị giác) something that seems to be different from the way it really is

    		•
  • She's under the illusion that (= believes wrongly that) she'll get the job.
  • The mirrors in the room gave an illusion of greater space.
  • The illusion of the house was designed so that people would think that the inside of the house was on the outside.
    		•
