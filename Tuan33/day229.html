Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
pass up (phrasal verb) bỏ qua (cơ hội)
(to not use the chance to do or have something when it is offered)
  • I can't believe she passed up the chance to go to South America.
  • He's never one to pass up a free meal.
  • Am I silly to pass this offer up?
  • I don’t think you should pass up the opportunity to go to university.
    • YT: English Lessons with Adam - Learn English [engVid]
    succumb /səˈkʌm/ (v) chịu thua, đầu hàng (sự cám dỗ, bệnh tật...)
    (to lose the determination to oppose something; to accept defeat)
  • I'm afraid I succumbed to temptation and had a piece of cheesecake.
  • He finally succumbed to Lucy's charms and agreed to her request.
  • The management finally succumbed to the demand of the workers and gave them a raise.
  • His career was cut short when he succumbed to cancer.
    		•
    get (somebody) off my back (spoken) dùng khi bạn cảm thấy ai đó đang quấy rầy và bạn muốn người đó ngừng làm phiền và để bạn được yên (to make someone stop annoying you with a lot of questions, criticisms etc)
  • I'll do it in a minute. Just get off my back!
  • Maybe the only way to get him off my back is to tell him the truth.
  • Get off my back, and don’t tell me to go out again. I have to study English.
    		•
    creep /kriːp/ (n) a person that you dislike very much and find very unpleasant
    (v) bò, trườn, đi rón rén, lẻn (vào phòng) (to move slowly, quietly, and carefully, usually in order to avoid being noticed)
    		 (n)
  • Leave me alone, creep!
  • He was such a creep - he was always staring at me in the canteen.


    • (v)
    creep - crept - crept
  • The spider crept up the wall.
  • I crept up the stairs, trying not to wake my parents.
  • The cat crept toward the bird.
    		•
    let someone down (phrasal verb) làm ai đó buồn, thất vọng (to disappoint someone by failing to do what you agreed to do or were expected to do)
  • You’ll be there tomorrow – you won’t let me down, will you?
  • I'm relying on your help - please don't let me down.
  • I promised Sophie I would meet her and I can’t let her down.
    		•
    resort to something /rɪˈzɔːrt/ (phrasal verb) đành phải (sử dụng biện pháp cuối cùng) (to do something that you do not want to do because you cannot find any other way of achieving something)
  • I had to resort to violence to get my money.
  • We may have to resort to using untrained staff.
    • YouTube: Jaiden Animations
    ledge /ledʒ/ (n) gờ, rìa (tường, cửa sổ...); mỏm đá (a narrow, flat area like a shelf that sticks out from a building, cliff, or other vertical surface)
  • While holding onto the ledge, James prayed someone would save him before he tumbled into the canyon.
  • From the window ledge, the bird peered down on the sidewalk.
  • For safety reasons, the fireman wore a harness when he stepped onto the ledge.
    		•
    ultimatum /ˌʌl.təˈmeɪ.t̬əm/ (n) tối hậu thư - lời đe dọa/cảnh báo đưa ra những điều kiện buộc đối phương phải chấp nhận, thi hành theo, nếu không sẽ dùng những biện pháp quyết liệt
    (a threat in which a person or group of people are warned that if they do not do a particular thing, something unpleasant will happen to them)
  • My boss gave me an ultimatum: get better results or find another job.
  • The workers got an ultimatum – go back to work or face dismissal.
  • Obviously our son was not concerned about the ultimatum we gave him because he sill came home two hours late.
    		•
    in a second =in a very short period of time
  • I'll be there in a second.
  • The doctor will be with you in just a second.
    		•
    gasp /ɡæsp/ (v) há hốc miệng vì kinh ngạc (to take a quick deep breath with your mouth open, especially because you are surprised or in pain)
  • He opened the box and gasped to see all the treasure inside.
  • Most people gasp when they hear how much money Patsy makes.
  • When they announced that Chris had won first prize, he gasped in astonishment.
  • When he collapsed on stage, the audience gasped.
    		•
    persuade /pəˈsweɪd/ (v) thuyết phục (to make someone decide to do something, especially by giving them reasons why they should do it, or asking them many times to do it)
  • If she doesn't want to go, nothing you can say will persuade her.
  • He is trying to persuade local and foreign businesses to invest in the project.
  • Try to persuade him to come.

  • (adj) persuasive: thuyết phục (able to make other people believe something or do what you ask)
  • a persuasive speech
  • Your arguments are very persuasive
  • The evidence was not really persuasive enough.
    		•
