cellular /ˈseljə.lɚ/ (adj) di động (connected with a phone system that works by radio instead of wires)
  • a cellular
  • (n) cell tower: tháp di động, trạm di động (a structure with devices for sending and receiving mobile phone signals)
    		•
    latency /ˈleɪ.tən.si/ (n) độ trễ
    (computing) the delay before data begins to move after it has been sent an instruction to do so
    autonomous /ɑːˈtɑː.nə.məs/ (adj) tự trị, tự quản, tự điều khiển (tự lái)
  • an autonomous organization, country, or region is independent and has the freedom to govern itself
  • (of a vehicle) that has the technology to drive itself without a person in control

    		•
  • an autonomous
  • Galicia is an autonomous region of Spain.
    		•
    seamlessly /ˈsiːm.ləs.li/ (adv) liền mạch, mượt mà, trơn tru, không bị gián đoạn (without any sudden changes, interruptions, or problems)
  • It's a children's film that seamlessly combines live action with computer-generated creatures.
  • (n) seam: đường may nối (a line where two things join, esp. where two pieces of cloth or leather have been sewn together)
  • The seam at the back of your skirt has split.
    		•
    synchronization /ˌsɪŋ.krə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (n) sự đồng bộ hóa
  • the fact of happening at the same time, or the act of making things happen at the same time
  • (computing) the act of linking data files between one computer or mobile device and another so that the information in the files on both machines is the same

    		•
  • (v) synchronize: đồng bộ
    • also sync
  • The sound track did not synchronize with the action.
  • The show was designed so that the lights synchronized with the music.
  • Let's synchronize our watches (= make them show exactly the same time).
  • How do I sync email across all my devices?
    		•
    translate /trænsˈleɪt/ (v)
    1. dịch (to change written or spoken words into another language)

    2. (to change something into a different form; to lead to a particular result)
    		 1.
  • She works for the UN, translating from English into French.
  • Her books have been translated into 24 languages.

    • 2.
  • It's time to translate words into action.
  • I hope all the hard work will translate into profits.
    		•
