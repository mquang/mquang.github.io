Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
go for (ph.v)
1. thích (like)

2. chọn (to choose a particular thing)

3. (to try to have or achieve something)
 1.
  • I don’t really go for horror films.
  • So what type of men do you go for?
  • He's not really my type—I don't usually go for guys with blonde hair.

    • 2.
  • I think I’ll go for the steak. What are you having?
    • YouTube: Speak English With Vanessa
    3.
  • Go for it, John! You know you can beat him.
  • It sounds a great idea. Go for it!

  • Sam: "I'm thinking of asking Dave out on a date, but what if he says no?"
    Mike: "Just go for it, dude! You'll never know unless you ask!"
    		•
    reflect on (ph.v) nhớ lại, ngẫm lại (to remember or think about someone or something)
  • Stuck in bed with a broken leg, all I could do was reflect on the accident that put me there.
    		•
    sign for (ph.v)
    1. đăng kí (if you sign for a sports club, you join it by signing a contract)

    2. kí nhận (to sign a document to show that you have received something)
    		 2.
  • I went to the door to sign for the package.
  • The courier asked me to sign for the parcel.
    		•
    get around to something (ph.v) làm một việc đã có dự định từ trước mà vẫn trì hoãn chưa làm (to do something that you have intended to do for a long time)
  • I still haven't got around to fixing that tap.
  • I intended to tidy the flat at the weekend, but I didn't get round to it.
    		•
    pay off (ph.v)
    1. thu lại quả ngọt, trái ngọt (to be successful and bring good results)

    2. trả hết (nợ)
    		 1.
  • All her hard work paid off in the end, and she finally passed the exam.
  • Though you are not getting the results you expected to, just hang in there, hard work always pays off.
    • YouTube: IELTS FACE-OFF
    2.
    (syn) retire
  • I've saved up enough to pay off my mortgage.
  • Only another six months and the house will be paid off.
  • We paid off our mortgage after five years.
    		•
    sleep in (ph.v) ngủ nướng (to continue sleeping after the time that you usually wake up)
  • The whole family sleeps in on Sundays.
  • I usually sleep in on Saturdays.
    		•
    catch up on (ph.v)
    1. (to find out about things that have happened, to learn the latest news about something)

    2. làm bù; tranh thủ làm cái gì đó mà chưa làm được, chưa có thời gian để làm (to spend extra time doing something because you have not done it earlier)
    		 1.
  • I need to catch up on all the gossip! I'm sure I missed a lot over spring break.
  • If you haven't been watching the news for the last few days, you have a lot to catch up on.

    • 2.
  • If you haven't done any biology homework in weeks, you better spend this weekend catching up on it—it's due on Tuesday!
    • YouTube: I Can Do English
    get along (ph.v)
    1. hòa thuận, có mối quan hệ tốt đẹp

    2. tiếp tục xử lý (công việc); tiến bộ, tiến triển (to manage to continue doing something or make progress)
    		 1.
  • Richard and his sister don’t get along.
  • I get along well with most of my colleagues.

    • 2.
  • Don’t worry, we’ll get along without you.
  • I couldn’t get along without you.
  • I got along much better in my new job.
