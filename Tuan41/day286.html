Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
measure up /ˈmeʒɚ/ (ph.v) đủ tốt (to be good enough; to meet a particular requirement, standard, or expectation)
  • She could never measure up to her mother's expectations.
  • I didn't get the job after all—I guess I just didn't measure up.
  • This meal doesn't measure up to my expectations.
    		•
    get down on sb/sth (ph.v) phàn nàn, chỉ trích (to keep criticizing someone or something; to have or express negative critical feelings toward someone or something)
  • Don’t get down on me. I didn’t do it!
  • She’s always getting down on the kids.
    		•
    give in (ph.v) nhượng bộ, khuất phục, chịu thua (to stop competing or arguing and accept that you cannot win)
  • You'll never guess the answer - do you give in?
  • She wouldn't give in until she received a full apology.
    		•
    grow apart (ph.v) xa cách dần (if people grow apart, their relationship gradually changes and they become less close)
  • Since moving to London, he’s grown apart from many of his friends.
    		•
    win (one) over (ph.v) có được sự ủng hộ, yêu thích từ ai (to earn one's support)
  • That candidate won me over with her performance in the debate.
  • He's not sure about the idea, but I'm sure we'll win him over in the end.
  • The politician won over the voters with her inspirational speech.

    fall for someone (ph.v) đổ, yêu (to be strongly attracted to somebody; to fall in love with somebody)
  • He fell for Rosie when he was in hospital and she was his nurse.
  • They fell for each other instantly.
    		•
    bring about (ph.v) gây ra, gây nên, tạo nên (to cause something to happen)
  • He brought about the collapse of the company by his reckless spending.
  • The arrival of electricity in rural areas brought about a huge change.
    		•
    sit around (ph.v) ngồi không, lãng phí thời gian (to spend time doing nothing very useful; to waste time sitting or relaxing idly)
  • Since Dad lost his job he just sits around the house all day.
  • I'm bored of just sitting around—let's go for a hike or something!
    		•
    come to one's senses (ph.v) biết nghĩ (to begin to think in a sensible or correct way after being foolish or wrong)
  • When will you come to your senses? Don't you realize that she's been lying to you?
  • I'm glad he finally came to his senses and went on to college.
    		•
    lie ahead (ph.v)
  • (tương lai) nằm ở phía trước
  • sẽ xảy đến, sẽ xảy ra (trong tương lai)

    • (if something lies ahead, it is going to happen in the future)
  • You are young and your whole life lies ahead of you.
  • If I get my university degree, I'm sure a good future lies ahead.
  • We need to be ready for whatever problems lie ahead.
    		•
    blow somebody away (ph.v) làm (ai đó) rất bất ngờ (to surprise someone very much)
  • Winning first prize and a full scholarship blew her away.
  • It just blows me away, the way everyone’s so friendly round here.
  • I was blown away by how good that movie was!
