Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
run over (ph.v) vượt quá thời gian (to a particular time limit)
  • Sorry I’m late, the meeting ran over.
  • My meeting ran over a bit, so I'm going to be a little late.
    		•
    work on (ph.v) cố gắng cải thiện (to spend time repairing or improving something)
  • Don’t worry about that, I’m working on it.
  • You need to work on your pronunciation a bit more.
  • His dancing technique is good, but he needs to work on his stamina.
    		•
    show up (ph.v) đến, tới, xuất hiện (to appear or arrive)
    (syn) turn up
  • Tom showed up at my door with flowers to apologize for his rude remarks.
  • How many people showed up to the meeting?
  • The virus does not show up in blood tests.
    		•
    burn out (ph.v)
    1. kiệt sức (to become too tired or unable to work, because of working too hard)

    2. cháy hết (if a fire burns out or burns itself out, it stops burning because there is no coal, wood etc left)

    3. cháy sạch (if a building or vehicle is burnt out, the inside of it is destroyed by fire)
    		 1.
  • If you keep working all nights and weekends, you're going to get burned out.

    • 2.
  • He left the fire to burn itself out.

    • 3.
  • The hotel was completely burnt out. Only the walls remained.
    		•
    set aside (ph.v) dành (​to save or keep something for a particular purpose)
  • Try to set aside some time each day for exercise.
  • a room that had been set aside for visitors
  • I will set a piece of cake aside for you.
    		•
    be into something (ph.v) thích, có hứng thú (to like and be interested in something)
  • I’m really into folk music.
    		•
    turn sb/sth down (ph.v) từ chối (lời mời, yêu cầu) (to refuse an offer or request)
  • He offered her a trip to Australia but she turned it/him down.
  • How could you turn down such a fantastic job?
  • I asked her out on a date, but she turned me down.
  • She turned down the opportunity to work in Paris.
    		•
    get down to something (ph.v) bắt tay vào việc (to start doing something seriously or with a lot of effort)
  • Let's get down to business.
  • After lunch we got down to discussing the issue of pay.
  • We need to get down to some serious talking.
    		•
