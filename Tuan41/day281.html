Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
ask after somebody (ph.v) hỏi thăm (to ask for news about someone)
  • Tony was asking after you and I told him you were fine.
  • Tell your father I was asking after him.
    		•
    bring forward (ph.v)
    1. thay đổi thời gian tổ chức (sớm hơn) (to change the date or time of an event so that it happens earlier)

    2. đưa ra (to introduce something so that it can be seen or discussed)
    		 1.
  • The meeting’s been brought forward to Thursday.
  • The elections were brought forward by three months.
  • They brought the date of the wedding forward so her cousins could attend.

    • 2.
  • The police brought forward some new evidence in the case.
  • The government has brought forward new proposals to tackle the problem of increasing crime.
  • Please bring the matter forward at the next meeting.
    		•
    bring in (ph.v)
    1. dùng đến (to use the skills of a particular group or person)

    2. mang lại (tiền bạc, doanh thu)
    		 1.
  • We need to bring in an expert to deal with this problem.
  • The police were brought in to investigate the matter.
  • Do you think we should we bring him in on this project?

    • 2.
  • Their chain of restaurants brings in millions of dollars a year.
  • The new product has been incredibly successful, bringing in about £1 million a month.
    		•
    bring out (ph.v) phát hành (to produce a new product and start to sell it)
  • The next year they brought out a low-priced car to compete with Ford.
  • He’s bringing out a new album next month.
    		•
    bring up (ph.v)
    1. nuôi nấng, chăm sóc (to look after a child until he or she becomes an adult)

    2. đề cập đến (to mention a subject or start to talk about it)
    (syn) raise
    1.
  • He was brought up by his grandmother.

    • 2.
  • I hate to bring this up but you still owe me £50.
  • Bring it up at the meeting.
    • YouTube: Speak English With Vanessa
    bring back (ph.v)
    1. sử dụng, áp dụng trở lại (to start to use something again that was used in the past)

    2. làm gợi nhớ lại cảm xúc, kỉ niệm (to cause ideas, feelings, or memories to be in your mind again)

    3. tiện đường mua/cầm theo cái gì/đưa ai đó về cùng trên đường về (to take something or someone with you when you come back from somewhere)
    		 1.
  • Few politicians are in favour of bringing back the higher rates of tax.

    • 2.
  • The photographs brought back many pleasant memories.

    • 3.
  • He always brings me back something nice when he goes abroad.
  • Don’t forget to bring something back for the kids.
  • I'm taking my niece to the zoo tomorrow afternoon and then bringing her back to her dad's house afterward.
    		•
    call off (ph.v) hủy (cancel)
  • I hope they don't call the concert off because of this rain.
  • Is it too late to call it off?
    		•
    come over (ph.v) nhà (chơi, bàn công việc...) (to go to someone else's house)
  • Why don’t you come over for dinner?
  • Come over to my place and we’ll discuss it.
    		•
    come on in (ph.v) mời vào, vào đi (to enter a place, such as a room, house, etc.)
    also come in
  • Come in! I'm in the kitchen!
    • YouTube: Rachel's English
