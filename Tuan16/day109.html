Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
matter /ˈmæt̬ɚ/ (n) vấn đề, chuyện
(v) quan trọng (to be important, or to affect what happens)		 (n)
  • What's the matter? Why are you crying?
  • I don't know what the matter is with the car, but it won't start.
  • What’s the matter with Derek these days? He’s acting so strangely.

    • (v)
    (thể phủ định) không quan trọng, không vấn đề gì, không có gì
    (trong câu hỏi - thể nghi vấn) có vấn đề gì không?
  • "What did you say?" "Oh, it doesn't matter."
  • Oh just put it anywhere - it doesn't matter where.
  • It doesn't matter what you wear - just as long as you come.
  • I know Charles doesn't think this project is important, but it matters to me.
    		•
    vanish /ˈvænɪʃ/ (v) biến mất (to disappear suddenly, especially in a way that cannot be easily explained)
  • My keys were here a minute ago but now they’ve vanished.
  • When she returned, her car had vanished.
  • The plane vanished from radar screens.
    • Mình vẫn nhớ từ này từ hồi đọc Harry Potter
    idiot /ˈɪdi.ət/ (n) đồ ngu, thằng ngu, đồ ngốc
  • Some idiot left the tap running in the bathroom and there's water everywhere.
  • It was all your fault, you idiot.


  • What an idiot!
    		•
    sensible /ˈsen.sə.bəl/ (adj) hợp lí, đúng đắn, biết nghĩ, khôn ngoan (reasonable, practical, and showing good judgment)
  • This was the first sensible suggestion she had heard from him.
  • Moving house seemed like the sensible thing to do.
  • He gave me some very sensible advice.
  • a sensible answer/compromise
  • I think the sensible thing to do is call and ask for directions.
    		•
    confidential /ˌkɑːn.fəˈden.ʃəl/ (adj) () kín, mật
    (secret, private, often in a formal, business, or military situation)
  • A person's medical records are confidential.
  • Always protect confidential files by locking them with a password.
  • Someone has leaked confidential government information to the press.
  • What I'm telling you is strictly confidential.
    		•
    make one's mind up (idiom) quyết định đi (to decide which of two or more choices you want, especially after thinking for a long time)
    make up your mind
    = make your mind up
    = make a decision
  • Karen couldn’t make up her mind whether to apply for membership or not.
  • Please make your mind up about Ralph. Will you pick him or not?
  • I don't have all day. Make up your mind.
    		•
    think on one's feet (idiom) đưa ra quyết định nhanh chóng, xử lý tình huống nhanh, nhanh trí (to make a quick decision or give an answer quickly)
