Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
imply /ɪmˈplaɪ/ (v) ngụ ý (to communicate an idea or feeling without saying it directly)
  • Are you implying (that) I'm fat?
  • What are you implying?
  • I'm not implying anything about your cooking, but could we eat out tonight?
  • Tom doesn't like to use the term "a person of color" because he thinks it implies that white people have no color.
  • Silence implies consent.
    • YouTube: Speak English With Vanessa
    involve /ɪnˈvɑːlv/ (v)
    1. bao gồm (những gì), đòi hỏi (những điều gì), chứa đựng (những gì)

    2. liên quan đến, dính líu, dính dáng đến
    - if a situation, an event, or an activity involves someone or something, they take part in it or are affected by it
    - to say or do something to show that someone took part in something, especially a crime

    3. MAKE INTERESTED (to make someone interested in taking part in something)    		 1.
  • Investment involves risks.
    (Việc đầu tư chứa đựng những rủi ro.)
  • What will the job involve?
  • The job involves me travelling all over the country.
  • Running your own business usually involves working long hours.
  • The test will involve answering questions about a photograph.

    • 2.
  • We want to involve as many people as possible in the celebrations.
  • Many of the crimes involved drugs.
  • How many vehicles were involved in the crash?
  • There was a serious incident involving a group of youths.
  • He admits being involved in the scandal.
  • I’m afraid your son’s been involved in an accident (=he is one of the people in an accident).
  • His confession involved a number of other politicians in the scandal.

    • 3.
  • Try to involve as many children as possible in the game.
  • A good teacher tries to involve children in activities where they interact with each other.
  • (n) involvement:
    1. sự tham gia, sự góp mặt     (the act or process of taking part in something; the state of being included in an activity)

    2. mối quan hệ tình cảm (a romantic or sexual relationship with somebody that you are not married to)
  • US involvement in European wars
  • He was accused of involvement in the robbery.
  • He spoke openly about his involvement with the singer.
  • (adj) involved:
    1. rắc rối, phức tạp     (not simple and therefore difficult to understand; complicated)
    2. mải mê, để hết tâm trí vào (việc gì) (giving a lot of time or attention to somebody/something)
    • 1.
  • The plot of the film was too involved - I couldn't understand it.
  • His story was so involved that I couldn’t follow it.

    • 2.
  • Maria was so involved in her work that she didn’t hear me come in.
  • I was so involved in my book I didn't hear you knock.
  • He's a very involved father (= he spends a lot of time with his children).
    		•
    implicate /ˈɪm.plɪ.keɪt/ (v) làm liên can, làm dính líu, kéo theo, khai ra (đồng phạm)
    (to show that someone is involved in something bad or criminal)
  • A lot of people were implicated in the scandal.
  • When my best friend tried to implicate me in the robbery in order to save herself, I knew we were no longer friends.
  • The arrested criminal did not hesitate to implicate his associates to gain immunity from prosecution.
  • (n) implication:
    1. sự liên can, dính líu (syn: involvement)
    2. điều ngụ ý, ẩn ý
    3. tác động, ảnh hưởng
    • 1.
  • He resigned after his implication in a sex scandal.

    • 2.
  • He smiled, but the implication was that he didn't believe me.
  • The implication in his article is that being a housewife is greatly inferior to every other occupation.

    • 3.
  • The low level of current investment has serious implications for future economic growth.
  • The company is cutting back its spending and I wonder what the implications will be for our department.
    		•
    indicate UK:
    /ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪt/
    US:
    /ˈɪndəˌkeɪt/    		 (v) biểu thị (to show, point, or make clear in another way)

    1. cho thấy (to show that something is true or exists)

    2. là dấu hiệu của (to be a sign of something; to show that something is possible or likely)    		 YouTube: JenniferESL
  • Research indicates that eating habits are changing fast.
  • The report indicates that the new car market has increased by 1.4%.
  • Her questions indicate that she understands the subject very well.
  • The strong wind indicates that a storm is coming.
  • Tone of voice can indicate feelings.
  • A red sky at night often indicates good weather the next day.
    • Dạng câu hỏi trong Part 7 có xuất hiện indicate:

    (Điều nào dưới đây biểu thị về bà Amy Fadden?)

    B. She has been a teacher for 23 years.
    C. She has written several books for children.
    dedicate UK:
    /ˈdedɪ.keɪt/
    US:
    /ˈdɛdəˌkeɪt/    		 (v)
    1. cống hiến, dâng hiến (to give all of your energy, time, etc.)

    2. dành cho (to say at the beginning of a book, a piece of music or a performance that you are doing it for somebody, as a way of thanking them or showing respect)    		 1.
  • He has dedicated his life to scientific research.
  • He dedicated his life to helping the poor.
    • YouTube: English Speeches
    2.
  • The record is dedicated to her mother, who died recently.
  • I dedicate this song to you.
  • I want to dedicate this song to him.


  • (adj) dedicated:
    1. tận tâm, tận tụy
    2. chuyên về     (designed to be used for one particular purpose)
    • 1.
    - a dedicated
  • She's completely dedicated to her work.

    • 2.
    - a dedicated
  • We have one TV channel completely dedicated to news, and another dedicated to sport.
