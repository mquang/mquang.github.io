refer to (phrasal verb)
1. nói đến, nhắc đến
2. xem, tham khảo		 1.
  • I was referring to Paris, Texas, not Paris, France.
  • The speaker did not refer to his notes during his talk.

    • 2.
  • Whenever I encounter a word that I don't know, I refer to a dictionary.
  • Which dictionary did you refer to?
  • Which dictionary are you referring to?
  • He referred to the dictionary for the correct spelling of the word.
  • (phrase) refer to ... as ...: xem ... như là ...
  • He formally adopted her in 1935, and she would always refer to him as her father.


  • He likes to be referred to as ‘Doctor Khee’.
  • Should I refer to you as Dr. Smith or Professor Smith?
  • Shanghai is referred to as the Paris of the East.
  • (n) reference:
    1. sự nói đến, nhắc đến
    2. sự tham chiếu, tham khảo
  • Avoid making any reference to his accident.
  • A dictionary is an excellent reference book.

    mention /ˈmen.ʃən/ (v) đề cập đến, nhắc đến
    - to speak about something quickly, giving little detail or using few words
    - to refer to something or someone

  • I'd rather you didn't mention it when we're in company.

  • Did she mention me in her letter?
  • Did I mention going to see Vicky on Sunday?
  • (idiom) don't mention it: không có gì, không có chi (used as a polite answer when somebody has thanked you for something)

    hint /hɪnt/ (n) lời gợi ý, lời nói bóng gió (something that you say or do that shows what you think or want, usually in a way that is not direct)

    Nó trốn ở kia kìa
  • Did she give you any hints about where she was going?
  • He hasn't announced his decision officially, but he's dropped several hints.
  • Should I drop a hint (= give a hint) to Matt?
  • You can give someone a hint to help them find the answer to something, but if you just give them the answer directly they won’t learn anything.
  • She hinted that she would like him to leave.
    • YouTube: Marie Forleo
    suggest /səˈdʒest/ (v)
    1. gợi ý, tiến cử (to tell somebody about a suitable person, thing, method, etc. for a particular job or purpose)

    2. đề nghị, đề xuất (to put forward an idea or a plan for other people to think about)

    3. cho thấy    		 1.
    syn: RECOMMEND
  • Where do you suggest we go?
  • What do you suggest we do?
  • I suggested an Italian restaurant near the station for the party.
  • Liz suggested (that) I try the shop on Mill Road.
  • Can you suggest where I could buy a dozen roses?


    • 2.
    syn: PROPOSE
  • Lori suggested that we should take a ten-minute coffee break.
  • I suggested to her that we go to the movies after he came home.
  • Tom's doctor suggested that he take a vacation and relax a bit.
  • I suggested going in my car.
    • Dạng câu hỏi Part 7:
    What is suggested about ...?
    (Ý nào dưới đây biểu thị về cái ... hoặc đoạn hội thoại cho chúng ta biết điều gì về cái ...)
    tương tự What is indicated about ...?, mình cần tìm thông tin tương ứng được diễn đạt hơi khác một chút ở đoạn văn và đối chiếu với đáp án

    3.
    (synonym) indicate
  • There's no absolute proof, but all the evidence suggests (that) he's guilty.
  • (n) suggestion: gợi ý, đề xuất (an idea or a plan that you mention for somebody else to think about)
  • I don't know what to wear tonight - do you have any suggestions?
  • She took a seat at his suggestion.
  • I went to the Park Street dentist's at Ann's suggestion and I was really impressed.
  • Don't underestimate the power of suggestion.
