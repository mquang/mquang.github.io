refuse /rɪˈfjuːz/ (v) từ chối (to say that you will not do or accept something)
  • Tom refused to let Mary go out and play in the rain.
  • She asked him some questions, but he refused to answer.
  • Tom didn't have the courage to refuse to do what Mary asked him to do.
  • What if I refuse?
  • He asked her to marry him, but she refused.
  • (n) refusal: sự từ chối, khước từ (the act of refusing to do or accept something)
    		•
    reject /rɪˈdʒekt/ (v) từ chối, không nhận
    - to refuse to accept somebody for a job, position, etc.
    - to not give someone the love and attention they want and are expecting from you
    - to refuse to accept or consider something
  • I've been rejected by all the universities I applied to.
  • The appeal was rejected by the court.
  • The college rejects hundreds of applicants each year.
  • She rejected him.
  • The chef will reject any produce that doesn’t look fresh.

    decline /dɪˈklaɪn/ (v)
    1. suy tàn, suy giảm, tụt (giá), trăng tàn, xế tàn (mặt trời) (to gradually become less, worse, or lower)

    2. từ chối (to refuse)

    3. dốc (xuống)    		 1.
  • His interest in the project declined after his wife died.
  • Industry in Britain has been in decline since the 1970s.
  • Today is the fifth day of continual stock price decline.
  • My grandmother's health has been declining since she broke her hip.


    • 2.

  • I invited him to the meeting but he declined.
  • He changed his mind and declined the company's offer.
  • They declined to tell me how they had obtained my address.

    deny /dɪˈnaɪ/ (v)
    1. từ chối (to refuse to admit or accept something)

    2. phủ nhận (to say that something is not true)

    1. deny (that) + mệnh đề
    2. deny + V-ing
    3. deny + N

    1.
  • The department denies responsibility for what occurred.
  • The girl denied saying bad things about her friend.
  • The goalkeeper denied him his third goal.
    • How I met your mother
    2.
  • Neil denies that he broke the window, but I'm sure he did.
  • Neil denies breaking the window.
  • The boy denied having stolen the bicycle.
  • He denies attempting to murder his wife.
  • (n) denial: sự phủ nhận
  • His denial of responsibility for the accident was unconvincing.
  • She issued a flat denial of the charges made against her.
