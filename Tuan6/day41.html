Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
clear /klɪər/ (adj)
1. rõ ràng, rành mạch, dễ hiểu (easy to understand, hear, read, or see)

2. trong, trong trẻo, không tì vết

3. thông thoáng, không có gì cản trở

4. chắc chắn (certain, having or feeling no doubt or confusion)
(v) dọn dẹp, làm trong sạch, khai thông, phát quang (REMOVE BLOCK)
 1.
  • clear instructions/directions
  • Is that clear?
  • It's written in clear, comprehensible English.
    • phim Extra
    2.
  • clear glass/ a clear blue sky
  • The water in the lake is so clear that you can see the bottom.
  • The sky is clear.
  • We could see hundreds of stars in the clear sky.
  • She has a beautifully clear skin/complexion (= with no marks or spots).

    • 3.
  • The road was clear.
  • We have a clear view of the ocean from our hotel window.
  • You won't get a clear view of the stage from here.
  • I always leave a clear desk at the end of the day.

    • 4.
  • I'm still not clear what the job involves.
  • My memory is not clear on that point.
  • Are you clear about the arrangements for tomorrow?

    • (v)
  • It took several hours to clear the road after the accident.
  • Tom cleared snow from the driveway.
  • It's your turn to clear the table.
  • I'll make the coffee if you'll clear the table.
  • Could you clear your things the sofa?
    		•
    obvious /ˈɑːb.vi.əs/ (adj) rõ ràng, rành mạch (easy to see or understand)
  • It's obvious (that) she doesn't like him.
  • There is no obvious connection between them.
  • It is obvious that he is right.
  • It's obvious that you're wrong.
  • Isn't obvious?
  • (adv) obviously: rõ ràng, thực sự (in a way that is easy to understand or see)
  • You obviously didn't read the instructions properly.
  • They were obviously exhausted after the game.
  • He was obviously drunk.
  • Diet and exercise are obviously important.
    		•
    distinct /dɪˈstɪŋkt/ (adj)
    1. rõ ràng, dễ thấy, dễ nhận biết (easily or clearly heard, seen, felt, etc.)

    2. riêng biệt, khác biệt (clearly different or of a different kind)
    		 1.
  • There was a distinct smell of gas.
  • There's a distinct smell of cigarettes in here.
  • There was the distinct smell of something burning.
  • She has a distinct English accent.
  • His voice was quiet but every word was distinct.

    • 2.
  • There's a distinct difference between them.
  • There are three distinct categories.
  • The phrase has three distinct meanings.
  • (n) distinction:
    1. sự khác biệt     (a difference between two similar things)

    2. xuất sắc (the quality of being excellent)
    		•
    apparent /əˈpæ.rənt/ (adj)
    1. rõ ràng, thấy rõ ra ngoài (easy to see or understand)

    2. có vẻ, bề ngoài (that seems to be real or true but may not be)
    syn: obvious
    1.
  • Her unhappiness was apparent to everyone.
  • Their devotion was apparent.
  • It was apparent from her face that she was really upset.
  • It's quite apparent that you don't want to do this for me.
  • He started yelling and throwing things for no apparent reason.

    • 2.

  • Their affluence is more apparent than real (= they are not as rich as they seem to be).
  • There are one or two apparent   discrepancies between the two reports.
  • The apparent cause of death was drowning, but further tests were needed.
  • (adv) apparently: có vẻ, hình như (based only on what you have heard, not on what you are certain is true)
  • Apparently it's going to rain today.
  • I thought she had retired, but apparently she hasn't.
  • Apparently, Tom is going to a university in Boston.
    		•
    evident /ˈevɪdənt/
    /ˈevədənt/    		 (adj) rõ ràng, rõ rệt (easily seen or understood)
    syn: obvious
  • Harry's courage during his illness was evident to everyone.
  • Her love for him was evident in all that she did.
    • (adv) evidently
  • Evidently, Tom didn't want to go.
  • Evidently he does not want to speak to me.
  • I thought she’d want to see me. Evidently, she doesn’t.
    		•
    evidence /ˈevɪdəns/
    /ˈevədəns/    		 (n)
    1. luận cứ, căn cứ (one or more reasons for believing that something is or is not true)



    2. chứng cớ, bằng chứng (the information that is used in court to try to prove something)
    		 1.
  • New evidence from a scientific study seems to support the idea that the Earth has been around for several billion years.
  • There is no scientific evidence to suggest that underwater births are dangerous.
  • Is there any scientific evidence that a person's character is reflected in their handwriting?
  • On the evidence of their recent matches, it is unlikely the Spanish team will win the cup.

    • 2.
  • Don't touch that. It's evidence.
  • What evidence do you have?
  • There's no evidence that Tom has done anything illegal.
  • I was asked to give evidence (= to say what I knew, describe what I had seen, etc.) at the trial.
    		•
