associate /əˈsoʊ.ʃi.eɪt/ (v) liên kết, cộng tác
(n) đối tác, cộng tác (a business partner, or someone that you work with)
(adj) (chức vụ) phó
 (v)
  • I have been associated with him for ten years.
  • I don't like you associating with those people.

    • (n)
  • He was my business associate.
  • Claire invited several business associates to dinner.
    • - a business associate

    (adj)
    - associate editor: Phó tổng biên tập
    - an associate director: phó giám đốc
  • She was only the associate producer of the movie.
  • (n) association:
    1. hội, hiệp hội     (a group of people who work together in a single organization for a particular purpose)
    2. mối quan hệ, mối liên kết, sự liên quan
    + a connection or relationship between people or organizations
    + a connection between two things where one is caused by the other
    • 1.
  • I applied for membership in the association.
  • student association
  • parent-teacher association
  • She set up an association to help blind people.

    • 2.
  • a close association between the two nations
  • Smoking has a close association with lung cancer.
  • (phrase) in association with:
    + với sự tài trợ, giúp đỡ     (with the help of a person or organization)

    + cùng với (cộng tác với) (made or done with another person, organization)
  • This event was organized in association with a local school.
  • This event was organized in association with the Sports Council.
  • The concert sponsored by the Arts Council in association with local businesses.

    		•
    affiliate /əˈfɪ.li.eɪt/ (n) chi nhánh, công ty con (a company, an organization, etc. that is connected with or controlled by another, larger one)
    (v) liên kết, tham gia
    - to link a group, a company or an organization very closely with another, larger one
    - to become part of or form a close relationship with a group or organization
    		 (n)
  • Our college is an affiliate of the university.
  • The World Chess Federation has affiliates in around 120 countries.

    • (v)
    affiliate sb/sth with/to sb/sth
  • I’m not affiliated with any political party.
  • All youth groups will have to affiliate to the National Youth Agency.
  • The Government will not allow the staff association to affiliate with outside unions.




    • YouTube: Amy Landino
  • (n) affiliation: mối liên kết (a connection with a political party or religion, or with a larger organization)
  • The group has affiliations with several organizations abroad.
  • Trade unions have a long history of affiliation to the Labour Party.
    		•
    branch UK:
    /brɑːntʃ/
    US:
    /bræntʃ/    		 (n) 1. nhánh (cây), nhánh (sông), ngả (đường), chi (của 1 dòng họ) ... (a part of something larger)
    2. chi nhánh (a local office or shop/store belonging to a large company or organization)
    		 1.
  • One branch of their family emigrated to Brazil.
  • The landscapers cut down many dead branches.
  • Algebra is a branch of mathematics.
  • Which branch of the armed forces were you in?

    • 2.
  • The bank has branches all over the country.
  • I used to work in the local branch of a large bank.
  • Our company has branches in many cities.
  • I heard that you became the manager of the Sydney branch.
  • The branch offices of the bank are located all over Japan.
  • (idiom) branch out: thử những điều mới (to start to do something different from what you usually do)

    subsidiary /səbˈsɪ.di.eri/ (n) chi nhánh (a company that is owned by a larger company)
    (adj) phụ, trợ, bổ sung (used to refer to something less important than something else with which it is connected)    		 (n)
  • She’s working for an overseas subsidiary of the company.
  • The British corporation purchased a subsidiary in China to handle its product distribution.

    • (adj)
  • a subsidiary role/factor
  • This question is subsidiary to the previous one.
