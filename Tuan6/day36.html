Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
brave /breɪv/ (adj) dũng cảm, gan dạ
synonym: (adj) courageous
  • It was a brave decision to quit her job and start her own business.
  • He was brave enough to go there alone.
  • He is brave enough to go there by himself.
  • Tom is one of the bravest men I've ever known.
  • How brave of him to jump into the water to save the little girl!
  • (n) bravery: sự dũng cảm, lòng dũng cảm
  • They were awarded medals for their bravery.
  • (n) bravado: ra vẻ can đảm
    - a show of bravery, especially when unnecessary and dangerous, to make people admire you
    - pretended courage or defiant confidence when one is really afraid
  • “Don't threaten me,” she whispered with false bravado.
    		•
    courage /ˈkə.rɪdʒ/ (n) sự dũng cảm, gan dạ
    synonym: (n) bravery
  • It took me several months to get up the courage to ask her to lunch.
  • After years of abuse from her husband, she eventually found the courage to leave him.
  • (adj) courageous: can đảm, dũng cảm
  • I hope people will be courageous enough to speak out against this injustice.
  • The courageous firefighters rushed into the burning building to save the trapped women and children.
    • encourage /ɪnˈkə.rɪdʒ/ (v) khuyến khích, cổ vũ, động viên (to talk or behave in a way that gives someone confidence to do something)
  • I encouraged Tom to .
  • He claims the new regulations will encourage investment.
  • The teacher encouraged me to study abroad.
  • (n) encouragement: sự khuyến khích, lời cổ vũ, động viên (words or behaviour that give someone confidence to do something)
  • Children need lots of encouragement from their parents.
  • I could never have achieved this without the encouragement of my husband and family.
  • (antonym) discourage: làm mất can đảm, giảm tự tin, gây chán nản, làm nản lòng ... (to make someone feel less confident, enthusiastic, and positive about something, or less willing to do something)
  • Don't let her comments discourage you.
    		•
    inspire /ɪnˈspaɪ.ər/ (v) truyền cảm hứng
  • The fire in her speech inspired everyone.
  • After her trip to Venezuela, she felt inspired to learn Spanish.
  • What an inspiring speaker!

  • (n) inspiration: nguồn cảm hứng, người (vật) truyền cho bạn cảm hứng (someone or something that gives you ideas for doing something)

  • What was your inspiration for the new designs?
  • The band confessed that their hit song had a real-life inspiration.
  • My inspiration comes from poets like Baudelaire and Jacques Prévert.
    • YouTube: Klossy
    motivate UK:
    /ˈməʊ.tɪ.veɪt/
    US:
    /ˈmoʊ.t̮əˌveɪt/    		 (v) thúc đẩy (to make someone want to do something, especially something that involves hard work and effort)
  • Teaching is all about motivating people to learn.
  • I don't want to be missing out. And that motivates me to get up and do something every day.
  • (n) motive: lý do, động cơ (a reason for doing something)
  • What was your motive?
  • What is the motive behind the bombing?
  • The motive for the murder is not yet known.

  • (n) motivation: động lực
  • What's your motivation?
  • Usually the only motivation for teenagers going to school is to socialize with their friends.

    incentive /ɪnˈsen.t̬ɪv/ (n) sự khuyến khích, sự khích lệ, thúc đẩy (something that encourages a person to do something)
    (syn): motivation
  • Awards provide an incentive for young people to improve their skills.
  • Good teachers provide their students with incentives to learn.
  • These kids have no incentive to learn.
  • When prices are so low, farmers have little incentive to increase production.
  • The rising cost of electricity provides a strong incentive to conserve energy.
  • (v) incentivize: to make someone want to do something
    • (UK): incentivise
