Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
tough /tʌf/ (adj)
1. khó khăn, khắc nghiệt (difficult to do or deal with)

2. cứng, dai
(food) difficult to cut or eat


3. cục súc (likely to behave in a violent way)
  • The company is going through a tough time at the moment.
  • My boss has given me a tough job/assignment.
  • Many homeless people are facing a tough winter.
  • Now I'm going to ask you a really tough question - are you ready?

    • 2.
  • This steak is very tough.
  • The meat was tough and hard to chew.

    • 3.
  • a tough neighbourhood
  • one of football’s most notorious tough guys
  • (v) toughen: làm cứng rắn, tôi luyện (to make something or someone tough, strong, or stronger)
  • His time in the army certainly toughened him up.
    		•
    generation /ˌdʒen.əˈreɪ.ʃən/ (n) thế hệ
  • all people of about the same age
  • a group of things that were developed from something else, or from which better things were developed
    		•
  • The younger generation smokes less than their parents did.
  • My generation have grown up without the experience of a world war.
  • There were three generations at the wedding – grandparents, parents, and children.
  • the generation
  • -generation computers
  • a new generation of
    • backfire /ˌbækˈfaɪər/ (v) phản tác dụng, đem lại kết quả ngược lại sự mong đợi (to have the opposite result from the one you intended)
  • Her plans to make him jealous backfired on her when he started dating her best friend.
  • The company’s new policy backfired when a number of employees threatened to quit.
  • The plan backfired on us and we lost a lot of money.
    		•
    downside /ˈdaʊn.saɪd/ (n) nhược điểm, điểm dở, điểm trừ (a disadvantage of a situation)
  • The downside of living here, of course, is that it is expensive.
  • The only downside of working here is that it is so far from my home.
  • (opp, n) upside: ưu điểm, điểm cộng, mặt lợi (the advantage of a situation)
  • The upside of the whole thing is that we got a free trip to Jamaica.
  • One upside to shopping online is that you can often get better deals than when shopping in store.
  • The upside to Facebook is that it helps keep you connected with family and friends who live far away.
    		•
    downsize /ˈdaʊn.saɪz/ (v) cắt giảm nhân sự (to make a company or organization smaller by reducing the number of people working for it)
  • The worsening situation forced the company to downsize from 39 employees to 7.
  • (opp, v) upsize: nâng cấp, mở rộng quy mô
    + to change to something larger or greater
    + to make a company or organization larger by increasing the number of people working for it
  • We need to upsize the entire computer system.
  • We are upsizing by 12% over the next two years.
    		•
    incident /ˈɪn.sɪ.dənt/ (n) sự cố, chuyện rắc rối (an event that is either unpleasant or unusual)
  • Am I at risk because of some incident in my sexual past?
  • The plane landed without incident.
  • We had an incident in the workplace that shut down business for a week.
  • (adj) incidental: phát sinh (happening in connection with something else, but not as important as it, or not intended)
  • Will I be reimbursed for incidental expenses at the conference?
    		•
    segue /ˈseɡ.weɪ/ (v) to move smoothly from one song, subject, place, etc. to another
    (n) a smooth movement from one song, subject, place, etc. to another
  • His performance of "Caroline" segued into a cover version of "My Tears".
    		•
    pocket /ˈpɒk.ɪt/ (n)
    1. túi

    2. túi tiền
    (v) đút túi
    - to put something into your pocket
    - to take something for yourself, especially dishonestly

    		 (n1)
  • a pocket
  • He took some coins his pocket.
  • The keys are in my trouser pocket.
  • Sarah put her maps in the outside pocket of her rucksack.

    • (n2)
  • There are eight hotels, with a price range to suit every pocket.
  • He had no intention of paying for the meal out of his own pocket.

    • (v)
  • Maggie locked the door and pocketed the keys.
  • She paid for the drink and pocketed the change without counting it.
  • I'll tell them I sold it for £20, not £25, then I can pocket the rest.
  • He regularly charges passengers more than the normal fare and pockets the difference.
    		•
    shift /ʃɪft/ (v) chuyển dịch, thay đổi
    (n) ca, kíp    		 (v)
  • Could you help me shift ?
  • Society's attitudes towards women have shifted enormously over the last century.
  • The wind shifted to the east.

    • (n)
  • Are you on the night shift or the day shift?
  • I’m working the day shift this month.
  • His shift starts at seven o'clock.
