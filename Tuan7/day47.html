access /ˈæk.ses/ (n)
1. quyền truy cập
2. sự tiếp cận, đến gần
(v) truy cập
 1.
  • You need a password to get access to the computer system.
  • You gave Howard access to the checking account.
  • He gave us access to a web site on which to submit our information.
  • Hackers complete access to the company files.

    • 2.
  • The police gained access through a broken window.
  • Journalists were denied access to the president.

    • (v)
  • People now can access information from the Internet as never before.
  • You can access your account online or by phone.
  • Many users now access the internet via their mobile phones.
    		•
    excess /ˈek.ses/
    /ɪkˈses/    		 (n, adj) (sự) vượt mức, quá mức, hơn (more than is necessary, reasonable or acceptable)
    		 (n)
  • They both eat to excess (= a lot more than they need).
  • He drinks to excess.
  • You shouldn't eat to excess.
  • The company’s losses are in excess of (= more than) $5 million.
  • The director earns an annual salary in excess of $100,000.
  • The car reached speeds in excess of 100 miles per hour.

    • (adj)
  • The airline charged me $75 for excess baggage.
  • On the return trip we were charged $80 excess baggage.
  • Excess vitamin D can cause kidney damage in young children.
  • Cut off the excess fat from the meat before cooking.
  • (v) exceed: vượt quá
  • His performance exceeded our expectations.
  • The book's success has exceeded all our expectations.
  • He was exceeding the speed limit by 15 miles an hour.
  • Working hours must not exceed 42 hours a week.
  • (adj) excessive: quá nhiều (too much, greater than what seems reasonable or appropriate)
  • Excessive exercise can sometimes cause health problems.
  • They complained about the excessive noise coming from the upstairs apartment.
    		•
    assess /əˈses/ (v) định giá, đánh giá (to judge or decide the amount, value, quality, or importance of something)
  • A college is going to assess a student’s ability based on grades.
  • The cost of repairs to the equipment was assessed at £2,000.
  • The value of the business was assessed at £1.25 million.
  • The total value of the paintings is assessed at $20 million.
  • He has written a guidebook that assesses the quality of Californian hotels.
  • (n) assessment: an opinion or a judgement about somebody/something
  • A teacher does a yearly assessment of each child’s progress.
  • The teachers worked together to create a math assessment for the 8th grade.
  • I don't agree with his assessment of the problem.
    		•
    asset /ˈæs.et/ (n)
    1. người (điều) có ích, có giá trị (a person or thing that is valuable or useful to somebody/something)
    2. tài sản (the things that a person or company owns, that can be sold to pay debts)
    		 1.
  • She'll be an asset to the team.
  • In his job, patience is an invaluable asset.
  • I think Rachel would be an asset to the department.
  • I'm sure you'll be a valuable asset to our company.
  • Thank you for your contribution - you are an asset to the company.
    • be an asset to sb/sth

    2.
  • Her assets include shares in the company and a house in France.
  • The company reported total assets worth $1.9 million.
  • a corporation with $9 billion in assets
  • He has over a million dollars in assets.
  • Shareholders voted to liquidate the company's assets.
    		•
