dissolve /dɪˈzɑːlv/ (v)
1. hòa tan
2. giải tán, giải thể, tan rã
 1.
  • Dissolve two spoons of powder in warm water.
  • Salt dissolves in water.
  • Sugar is a soluble substance, which means that it dissolves in water.
  • Pour the powder into the solution and stir it gently until the powder has dissolved.

    • 2.
  • Their marriage was dissolved in 1968.
  • When the ambulance had gone, the crowd dissolved.
  • Parliament has been dissolved.
  • The election was announced and parliament was dissolved.

    		•
    melt /melt/ (v) tan chảy, nấu chảy
  • The snow usually melts by mid March.
  • Melt the chocolate slowly so that it doesn't burn.
  • When the sun came out, the ice began to melt.
  • First, melt two ounces of butter.

    take in (phrasal verb)
    1. hiểu, hấp thu (understand & remember something)
    2. đi xem, tận hưởng
    - to go to watch a film or performance, or to visit a place such as a museum
    - to inhale, drink, or eat something.
    3. chấp nhận sự thật (to accept something as real or true)
    1 - 2 - 3: thu nạp, cho vào trong đầu, trong người
    4. chứa, cho ở chung (to allow someone to stay in your house)
    thu nạp, cho vào trong nhà
    5. bó (áo, quần) (to make a piece of clothing more narrow or tight, so that it fits you)
    thu kích cỡ vào
    6. bao gồm (to include something)
    => chỉ cần nhớ 2 nghĩa là "thu vào" và "bao gồm"
  • I had to read the letter twice before I could take it all in.
  • I’m not sure how much of his explanation she took in.
  • I know it's a lot to take in, so let me know if you have any questions.
  • I wasn't able to take every detail in, but I got the gist of it.

    • 2.
  • I thought we could get something to eat and then take in a movie.
  • We took in a couple of films.
  • The mountains are so beautiful! I need an hour or so to take it all in.
  • I want to sit here a minute and take in the view.
  • I think I'll go for a walk and take some fresh air in.

    • 3.
  • He still hasn’t really taken in his father’s death.
  • She was telling me he was dead, and I couldn't take it in.

    • 4.
  • We decided to take in lodgers.
  • I don't take in strangers.

    • 5.
  • I'll have to take this dress in at the waist - it's too big.
  • I'll have to take in these pants.

    • 6.
  • The new town takes in three former villages.

    absorb /əbˈzɔːrb/ (v) hút (nước), hấp thu, hấp thụ (chất, kiến thức ...)
    (to take something in, especially gradually)
    synonym: take in
  • Plants absorb carbon dioxide from the air.
  • It's hard to absorb so much information.
  • It's a lot of information to absorb all at once.
  • They spent a week in Paris just absorbing the atmosphere.
  • It took me several days to absorb the fact of her death.
  • (phrase) absorbed in sth: mải mê (very interested in something and not paying attention to anything else)
  • Judith lay on the settee, absorbed in her book.
  • My father was always too absorbed in his work.
  • Greg absorbed himself in video games every day after school.
  • I wanted to impress Juliet, so I absorbed myself in music by her favorite band.
    		•
