delay /dɪˈleɪ/ (n) sự chậm trễ
(v) trì hoãn, làm chậm trễ (to make something happen at a later time than originally planned or expected)
 (n)
  • We apologize for the delay in answering your letter.
  • Car accidents delays.

    • (v)
  • My plane was delayed by an hour.
  • I was delayed by traffic.
  • The judge temporarily delayed the hearing.
    		•
    put off (phrasal verb) trì hoãn, hoãn lại (to delay or move an activity to a later time, or to stop or prevent someone from doing something)
  • The meeting has been put off for a week.
  • They had to put the wedding off because the bride’s mother had an accident.
  • You can’t put the decision off any longer.
  • (phrasal verb) put someone off/put off someone: làm mất hứng, làm nản lòng
    (cause someone to lose interest or enthusiasm; to discourage someone from doing something)
  • I was put off by his appearance.
  • He keeps asking me out, and I keep putting him off.
  • I put him off the idea of going shopping with me.
  • Don’t let your failures put you off trying harder.
  • All this rain really puts me off going out after work.
    • làm mất tập trung (to prevent someone from concentrating on something so that they have difficulty doing it)
  • Stop laughing – you’ll put her off.
    		•
    postpone /pəstˈpəʊn/ (v) hoãn lại (to delay an event and plan or decide that it should happen at a later date or time) youglish.com
  • They decided to postpone their holiday until next year.
  • We've had to postpone going to France because the children are ill.
  • We decided to postpone the party until my parents had returned from America.
  • The exams have been postponed until next month.
  • The trip has been postponed twice.

    • trích từ sách English Phrasal Verb in Use
    defer /dɪˈfɜːr/ (v) hoãn lại (to delay something until a later time)
  • Can we defer making a decision until next week?
  • The doctor has decided to defer the surgery until my father’s health improves.
  • Because of my mother’s death, we will defer our vacation for a while.
  • In order to save money for college, James will defer his studies while he works fulltime for a year.
  • (phrasal verb) defer to: đồng ý, theo
    (to agree to accept what somebody has decided or what they think about somebody/something because you respect him or her or because of their higher rank, authority, knowledge, etc.)
  • We will defer to whatever the committee decides.
  • I have to defer to my boss on important decisions.
  • I defer to (= accept) your judgment.

    		•
    adjourn /əˈdʒɜːrn/ (v) hoãn lại, tạm dừng (phiên xét xử, cuộc họp...) (to have a pause or rest during a formal meeting or trial)
  • Shall we adjourn for lunch?
  • The meeting was adjourned until Tuesday.
  • The court adjourned for lunch.
  • The trial has been adjourned until next week.
  • (phrasal verb) adjourn to: to go to another place, especially in order to relax
  • I suggest we adjourn to the bar for a drink.
  • Shall we adjourn to the sitting room for coffee?
  • (n) adjournment: a pause or rest during a formal meeting or trial, or the act of giving a pause or rest
  • The defence attorney requested an adjournment.
    		•
