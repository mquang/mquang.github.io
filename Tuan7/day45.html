soak /səʊk/ (v) ngâm, nhúng (trong nước) (to put something in liquid for a time so that it becomes completely wet; to become completely wet in this way)
  • I usually soak the beans overnight.
  • I'm going to go and soak in the bath.
  • (phrasal verb) soak up: thấm, hút, hấp thụ
    - to take in or absorb liquid
    - to absorb something into your senses, your body or your mind
    - to breathe or inhale something.
  • I tried to soak up most of the spilled milk with a dishcloth.
  • We were just sitting soaking up the atmosphere.
  • (adj) soaked/soaking: ướt đẫm, ướt sũng (extremely wet)


  • She was soaked from head to foot.
