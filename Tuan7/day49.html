raise /reɪz/ (v)
1. nâng lên, giơ lên (to lift something to a higher position)


2. tăng, nâng

3. gây nên

4. nuôi lớn (to take care of a person, or an animal or plant, until they are completely grown)		 1.
  • He raised a hand in greeting.
  • She raised the gun and fired.
  • William raised his hat and smiled at her.
  • "Cheers, everyone!" said Larry, raising his glass.
  • Raise your hand if you know the right answer.



    • 2.
  • The government plan to raise taxes.
  • They raised their offer to $500.
    • raise your voice: to speak loudly or shout because you are angry
  • He’s never raised his voice to me.
  • Stop raising your voice, Amanda.

    • 3.
  • Her answers raised doubts/fears/suspicions in my mind.

    • 4.
  • Her parents died when she was a baby and she was raised by her grandparents.
  • He was raised by his grandmother.
    		•
    rise /raɪz/ (n) sự tăng
    (v) tăng     (chính nó - itself)
    rise - rose - risen
    (n)
  • Insulin is used to control the rise of glucose levels in the blood.

    • (v)
  • Sales rose by 20% over the Christmas period.
  • The research budget rose from £175,000 in 1999 to £22.5 million in 2001.
  • The book has risen to the top of best-seller lists.
    • YouTube: BBC Learning English
  • Rise and shine, Johnny! Time to go to school.
    		•
    arise /əˈraɪz/ (v) xuất hiện, xảy ra, nảy sinh (to happen, to occur)
    We use it with abstract nouns (e.g. problem).
    arise - arose - arisen
  • If any complications arise, let me know and I'll help.
  • A problem has arisen with my passport.
  • Should the opportunity arise, I'd love to go to China.
  • A storm arose during the night.
    		•
    growth /ɡroʊθ/ (n) sự tăng trưởng (the process of developing or of increasing in size, amount)
  • Plant growth is most noticeable in spring.
  • (v) grow:
    1. sinh trưởng, phát triển, lớn, tăng lên     (to increase in size or amount, or to become more advanced or developed)

    2. nuôi (if you grow your hair or nails, you do not cut them)
    • grow - grew - grown
    1.
  • Children grow so quickly.
  • She's grown three centimetres this year.
  • Stan grew two inches in six months.
  • Sales of new cars grew by 10% last year.
  • The number of students at the college has grown from 200 to over 500.

    • 2.
  • I’ve decided to grow my hair long.
  • Are you growing a beard?
  • Lottie wants to grow her hair long.
    		•
    increase /ɪnˈkriːs/ (v) tăng lên, tăng thêm
    to (make something) become larger in amount or size
    (n) sự tăng (a rise in the amount or size of something)
    		 (v)
  • The number of traffic accidents has increased in recent years.
  • We'll employ a new tool to increase productivity.
  • The number of people who go abroad has been increasing.
  • Production at this factory has increased by 20%.
  • They’ve increased the price of gas by two cents a gallon.
  • Sales have increased by 10%.

    • (n)
  • He asked for an increase in allowance.
  • There was a slight increase in unemployment last month.
  • Population growth has led to an increase in traffic.
  • (n, v) decrease: sự giảm, giảm sút
  • The number of new students decreased from 210 to 160 this year.
  • The price of wheat has decreased by 5%.
  • Average house prices decreased by 13% last year.
    		•
    proliferate /prəˈlɪf.ə.reɪt/ (v) sinh sôi, nảy nở, tăng nhanh (to increase a lot and suddenly in number, usually quickly)
    synonym: (v) multiply
  • Fast-food restaurants have proliferated in the area.
  • Computer courses continue to proliferate.
  • Amusement and theme parks are proliferating across the country.
  • (n) proliferation: sự sinh sôi, nảy nở, sự tăng nhanh
  • the proliferation of global media networks
  • attempts to prevent cancer cell proliferation
