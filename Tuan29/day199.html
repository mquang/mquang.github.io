cooped up /ˌkuːpt ˈʌp/ (adj) giam (mình)
  • I hate being cooped up inside working when its a sunny day outside.
  • It's such a tiny office - don't you ever feel cooped up here?
  • (phrasal verb) coop up: giam, nhốt (to keep a person or an animal inside a building or in a small space)
  • Just let the dogs run in the yard—they've been cooped up all day.
  • I’ve been cooped up in that office all day.
    		•
    beholden /bɪˈhəʊl.dən/ (adj) mang ơn, chịu ơn (feeling you have a duty to someone because they have done something for you)
  • Ludwig is beholden to the President, who gave him his job.
  • She wanted to be independent and beholden to no one.
  • Since Kevin is the company’s president, he feels beholden to his employees and works hard to ensure they are treated fairly.
    • neck-deep (adj) standing in something that reaches to one's neck, often used figuratively
  • She was neck-deep in water.
  • We were neck-deep in work. (=we were very busy)
    		•
    mortgage /ˈmɔː.ɡɪdʒ/ (n) khoản vay thế chấp (a legal agreement by which a bank or similar organization lends you money to buy a house, etc., and you pay the money back over a particular number of years)
  • He didn’t earn enough to support his family and pay the mortgage.
  • He wasn’t earning enough to pay the mortgage.
  • After falling behind with his mortgage repayments he now faces eviction from his home.
  • They fell behind with/​on their mortgage, so their home was repossessed.
    • Phân biệt loan và mortgage
    not be sb's cup of tea if someone or something is not your cup of tea, you do not like them or do not consider them interesting
  • Thanks for inviting me, but ballet isn't really my cup of tea.
  • Politics was not his cup of tea.
    		•
    practical /ˈpræk.tɪ.kəl/ (adj)
    1. thực hành, thực tiễn (relating to experience, real situations, or actions rather than ideas or imagination)

    2. phù hợp, hữu dụng (useful or suitable for a particular purpose or situation)    		 1.
  • Qualifications are important but practical experience is always a plus.
  • It's an excellent teaching course with plenty of opportunity for practical experience.

    • 2.
  • I tend to wear clothes that are practical rather than fashionable.
  • Heavy boots aren't very practical for running.
  • Skirts aren’t very practical in my kind of work.
  • (adv) practically: hầu như, gần như (almost or very nearly)
  • It's practically impossible for me to get home in less than an hour.
  • I visit Mum and Dad practically every day.
  • I’ve read practically all of his books.
  • (n, v) practice:
    (n)
    1. thực tế, thực tiễn     (action rather than thought or ideas)

    2. thông lệ, lệ thường, thói quen (something that is usually or regularly done, often as a habit, tradition, or custom)

    3. sự luyện tập, việc luyện tập (the act of doing something regularly or repeatedly to improve your skill at doing it)
    (v, US) luyện tập, tập luyện, rèn luyện
    • (n1)
  • I can't see how your plan is going to work in practice.
  • Officially, Robert's in charge, but in practice Hannah runs the office.
  • It frustrates me that I'm not able to put any of my ideas into practice.

    • (n2)
  • It's common practice in the States to tip the hairdresser.
  • It was normal practice for hotels to require confirmation in writing.
  • It was his usual practice to buy a newspaper every morning on the way to the office.

    • (n3)
  • She does an hour's piano practice every day.
  • With practice you will become more skilled.
  • It takes years of practice to get it right.

    • (v)
    (v, UK) practise
  • The team is practicing for their big game on Friday.
  • They practiced the dance until it was perfect.
  • I have practiced every day for fifteen years.
    		•
    external /ɪkˈstɜː.nəl/ (adj) bên ngoài
  • relating to the outside of something or of a person’s body
  • coming from or happening outside a particular place or organization

    		•
  • the external walls of the building
  • information from external sources
  • (adj, opp) internal: bên trong, nội bộ
  • the internal mail
  • This is for internal use only.
  • I'm afraid I have internal bleeding.
  • We dissected a frog to examine its internal organs.
