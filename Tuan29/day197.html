account for /əˈkaʊnt/ (phrasal verb)
1. chiếm (to be a particular amount or part of something)


2. là lí do của việc gì (to be the cause of something, to be the reason why something happens)

3. diễn giải, giải thích, đưa ra lí do (to give a reason or explanation for something)
  • The Japanese market accounts for 35% of the company's revenue.
    • (Thị phần Nhật chiếm tới 35% doanh thu của công ty)
  • Students account for the vast majority of our customers.
  • Afro-Americans account for 12% of the US population.

    • 2.
  • These new features account for the computer's higher price.
  • Recent pressure at work may account for his behavior.

    • 3.
  • How do you account for your success?
  • Can you account for the missing files?
    		•
    be + to + verb (grammar)
    1. nói về dự định, kế hoạch (to tell what is going to happen in the future)

    2. diễn đạt mệnh lệnh (to give an instruction or command)

    3. dùng trong câu điều kiện (Nếu muốn ... xảy ra thì phải có tiền điều kiện xảy ra)    		 1.
  • The Prime Minister is to visit India next month.
  • The plane is to take off at 9pm.
  • The new kitchen cabinets are to arrive before December 20th.

    • 2.
  • You are to do your homework without watching any TV.
  • You are to do your homework before you can go out.
  • You are not to leave this house without my permission. Is that clear?

    • 3.
  • If we are to get there by lunchtime we had better hurry.
  • He knew he would have to work hard if he was to pass the exam.
  • If we are to be in Paris tomorrow, we should buy a nonstop flight.
    		•
    prevalence /ˈprevələns/ (n) the fact of existing or being very common at a particular time or in a particular place
  • the prevalence of smoking among teenagers
  • the prevalence of lung cancer among the local population
  • (adj) prevalent: phổ biến (common at a particular time, in a particular place, or among a particular group of people)
    • (syn) common
  • The disease is more prevalent in younger women.
  • Trees are dying in areas where acid rain is most prevalent.
  • During the holidays, theft becomes more prevalent in low-income areas.
  • These days, laptop computers are prevalent on university campuses.
    		•
    alleviate /əˈliːvi.eɪt/ (v) giảm bớt (to make pain or problems less severe)
  • The drugs did nothing to alleviate her .
  • Take an aspirin to alleviate your headache.
  • A number of measures were taken to alleviate the problem.
  • To alleviate hunger in our town, each employee of our company donated five cans of food.
    		•
    burden /ˈbɜːdən/ (n) gánh nặng
  • My elderly mother worries that she's a burden to me.
  • Buying a house often places a large financial burden on young couples.
  • I don't want to be a burden to you.
  • His illness has placed a huge financial burden on the family.
    		•
    take up (phrasal verb)
    1. bắt đầu 1 sở thích, thói quen, công việc mới
  • to start doing something regularly as a habit
  • to start a new job or have a new responsibility

    • 2. chiếm (không gian, thời gian) (to fill space or time)    		 1.
  • They've taken up golf.
  • Chris has taken up jogging.
  • I took up smoking when I was at school.
  • The headteacher takes up her duties in August.
  • He takes up his duties next week.

    • 2.
  • These files take up a lot of disk space.
  • I’ll try not to take up too much of your time.
  • This desk takes up too much space.
    		•
    gather /ˈɡæðər/ (v)
  • tụ tập, tập trung
  • gom nhặt, thu thập (thông tin, chữ kí...)

    		•
  • A crowd gathered to watch the fight.
  • Thousands of people gathered outside the embassy.
  • Could the bride’s family all gather together for a photo?
  • I went to several libraries to gather information about the plans.
  • They had gathered 440,000 signatures to support their demand.
    		•
    outlook /ˈaʊt.lʊk/ (n)
    1. quan điểm (your general attitude to life and the world)

    2. viễn cảnh (the likely future situation)    		 1.
  • Despite her health problems, she has a positive outlook.
  • She still has an optimistic outlook for the future.
  • Education should give students a wider outlook on life.
  • He has a fairly positive outlook on life.
  • Losing his job changed his whole outlook on life.

    • 2.
  • The outlook for the region is very gloomy.
  • a gloomy economic outlook in Western Europe
  • Tomorrow's outlook is for rain in the morning, clearing up in the afternoon.
  • The outlook for the weekend is unsettled, with periods of heavy rain.
    		•
