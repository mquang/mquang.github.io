governor /ˈɡʌ.vər.nər/ (n) thống đốc
  • Governors from most of the 50 states will meet in Omaha this weekend.
  • He was elected governor of Texas in 1994.
  • Mr. Clinton was governor of Arkansas at the time.

    • governor Arnold Schwarzenegger (từng thủ vai chính trong phim kẻ hủy diệt)
  • (n) government: chính phủ, chính quyền
  • (n) governance: sự cai trị, sự cai quản (the activity of governing a country or controlling a company or an organization; the way in which a country is governed or a company or institution is controlled)
  • (v) govern: cai trị, quản lý, cai quản (to control and manage a country, city, group of people, etc.)

    resident /ˈrɛ.zəˌdɛnt/ (n) cư dân (a person who lives or has their home in a particular place)
    (adj) cư trú, thường trú (living or staying in a place)
  • As a former resident of Flint Michigan, the man was extremely concerned about the lack of clean water in his home town.
  • He had been resident in Brussels since 1967.
  • If you want to take a British driving test you must be resident in the UK.
  • (adj) residential: (thuộc) liên quan đến nhà ở, nơi cư trú
    • - residential area: khu dân cư
  • It was not safe to locate the chemical factory in a residential area.
  • The residential course for writers takes place in August.

  • (v) reside: ở, trú ngụ, cư trú
  • She resides in New York.
  • The family now resides in southern France.
  • (n) residence: nơi ở, nơi cư trú
  • Let us know if you change your residence.
  • She took up residence in Boston (= began living there).
    		•
    citizen /ˈsɪ.t̮ə.zn/ (n) công dân

    Matt Dumbo Nguyen and his wife Tessa
  • He entered the United States in 1988 as a permanent resident because of his marriage to a U.S. citizen.
  • Tom is a Canadian citizen.
  • Dumbo is a citizen of the United States.

  • (n) citizenship: quyền công dân, bổn phận công dân, tư cách công dân
  • Caroline had dual citizenship in both her native country of Ireland and in the United States.
  • Saudi Arabia grants citizenship to robot Sophia.
  • He was granted Canadian citizenship.

    		 - a inspector
  • Tom has been a safety inspector for three years.
  • The inspector arrived at the school late on Monday morning.

    (v) coi thi (to watch people taking an exam in order to check that they do not cheat)
  • If you need more paper, please ask the proctor.

    supervisor /ˈsupərˌvaɪ.zər/ (n) người giám sát
    		 - a supervisor
  • I have a meeting with my supervisor about my research topic.
  • All work is done under the guidance of a supervisor.
  • (adj) supervisory: (thuộc) giám sát
  • She has a supervisory role on the project.
  • He applied for a supervisory position in the bank.
  • (v) supervise: giám sát
    + to watch a person or activity to make certain that everything is done correctly, safely, etc.
    + to manage a department, project, etc. and make sure that things are done correctly and according to the rules
  • Tom was hired to supervise the production of Mary's music video.
  • I supervised the people fixing my bathroom.
