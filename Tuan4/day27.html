/ˌəʊ.vəˈsiː/		 (v) trông, giám sát (to watch somebody/something and make sure that a job or an activity is done correctly)
syn: supervise
  • As marketing manager, her job is to oversee all the company's advertising.
  • The financial managers oversee the preparation of financial reports, direct investment activities, and implement cash management strategies.
  • The ancient Greeks believed that Zeus oversaw and directed all things in our world.

    college /ˈkɑːlɪdʒ/ (n) trường đại học, cao đẳng
    - a place where you can study for higher or more specialist qualifications after you finish high school
    - a university where students can study for a degree after they have left school
  • I met my husband when we were in college.
  • Where is the college dormitory?
  • I can't afford college tuition.
  • We were roommates in college.
  • What was your major in college?
  • Tom majored in French in college.

    colleague /ˈkɑːliːɡ/ (n) đồng nghiệp (a person that you work with)
  • We were friends and colleagues for more than 20 years.
  • I don't know anything about this, but I'm sure my colleague here can help you.
  • Tom is my colleague.
  • Tom and Mary are both colleagues of mine.
  • Their colleague was transferred to an overseas branch.
    • YouTube: Dan Hauer
    in charge /ɪn tʃɑːr.dʒ/ (phrase) đảm nhận, phụ trách (responsible for something or someone)
  • (be) in charge (of something/someone): if you are in charge, you have control over someone or something and are responsible for them
  • Who’s in charge here?
  • The teacher put me in charge of organizing the project.
  • The nurse in charge explained what the treatment would be like.
  • Who’s in charge of making sure we don’t run out of toilet paper in the bathroom?

    headmaster /ˌhedˈmæstər/ (n, UK) ông hiệu trưởng (a male teacher who is in charge of a school, especially a private school)
  • (n, US) principal: hiệu trưởng (the person in charge of a college or university)
  • (n) headmistress: bà hiệu trưởng (a female headteacher)
  • Our teacher has been promoted to headmaster.
  • The headmaster encouraged the candidates for the national maths competition to strive for the best results.
    		•
    term /tɜːrm/ (n)
    1. thời hạn, kỳ hạn, nhiệm kì
    2. kì học     (one of the three periods in the year during which classes are held in schools, universities, etc.)
    3. thuật ngữ
    4. điều kiện, điều khoản     (the conditions that control an agreement, arrangement, or activity)    		 1.
    - short term/long term
  • The term of agreement can be for either two or three years.
  • His presidential term expires at the end of May.

  • He was sentenced to a 150-year prison term for cheating thousands of ordinary people out of their savings.

    • 2.
    - the term
  • In Britain, the spring term starts in January and ends just before Easter.
  • We're very busy in term-time (= during the term).
    • - term ở Anh
    - ở Mỹ (North American English) dùng trimester
    3.
    - a term
  • Nowadays, we often use the term "senior citizen" to refer to old people.

    • 4.
  • According to the terms of the contract, your payment was due on May 31st.
  • (n-s) terms: mối quan hệ
    If two people are on good/bad/friendly terms, they have a good/bad/friendly relationship with one another
  • (idiom) in terms of: liên quan đến vấn đề gì, về vấn đề gì (in relation to something)
  • We try to do what is best for our customers in terms of the quality of our products.
  • Did the experiment find any differences in terms of what children learned?
  • I don't know what to do in terms of John's problem.

    semester /sɪˈmes.tər/ (n) kì học (one of the two periods that the school or college year is divided into, especially in the US and Australia) - the first/second semester
