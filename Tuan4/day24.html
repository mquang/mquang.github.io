Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
recruit /rɪˈkrut/ (v) tuyển dụng, chiêu mộ (to find new people to join a company, an organization, the armed forces, etc.)
  • The company recruits 20 new staff each year.
  • He was recruited by the army after high school.
  • Public schools are recruiting new teachers.
  • Do you think Bobby was recruited?
  • (n) recruitment: sự tuyển dụng
  • There are some new positions in our company’s recruitment plan.
  • (n) recruiter: nhà tuyển dụng
  • He worked as a military recruiter in Texas.
  • Mr. David is the professional recruiter of our company.
    		•
    employ /ɪmˈplɔɪ/ (v) thuê, dùng (người) (to have someone work or do a job for you and pay them for it)
  • How many people does your company employ?
  • Can't we employ someone as an assistant to help with all this paperwork?
  • Our company used to employ him as a copywriter.
  • Many rich people employ servants to do household chores.
  • The number of people employed by the company has risen from 25 to 200 in three years.
  • (n) employee: nhân viên, người làm thuê
  • All new staffs should follow the employee regulations.
  • The company has over 500 employees.
  • Some of their employees do not have insurance.
  • This company has 50 employees.
  • He always pays the employees on time
  • (n) employer: ông chủ, chủ, người mướn (a person, company or organization that employs people)
  • This company is employer in the state.
  • He is really a good employer.
  • The boy I spoke to a moment ago is the son of my employer.
  • (n) employment:
    1. công việc, việc làm

    2. trạng thái đang đi làm, có việc, không thất nghiệp    (the state of being employed)
    • be in employment: đang đi làm, có việc (to have a job)
  • Are you in employment at the moment?
  • The new factory will provide employment for about a hundred local people.
  • She lost her employment when the firm closed.
  • The building project will create many employment opportunities.
  • (n) unemployment:
    1. tình trạng thất nghiệp     (the state of being unemployed)

    2. số lượng người thất nghiệp (the number of people who do not have a job)
  • The unemployment rate rose to 4.7 per cent.
    		•
    applicant /ˈæp.lɪ.kənt/ (n) người nộp đơn, người ứng tuyển, người dự tuyển
  • All applicants have to make a test.
  • How many applicants did you have for the job?
  • There were over 500 applicants for the job.
  • We interviewed 30 qualified applicants for the job.
  • (v) apply:
    1. xin việc, nộp đơn     (to request something, usually officially, especially in writing or by sending in a form)

    2. ứng dụng, áp dụng (to make use of something or use it for a practical purpose)
  • She's applied for a job with an insurance company.
  • You can apply for this position by the email.
  • The new technology was applied to farming.
  • He wants a job in which he can apply his foreign languages.
  • (n) application:
    1. đơn xin (việc, ...)     (an official request for something, usually in writing)

    2. chương trình ứng dụng (a computer program that is designed for a particular purpose)
  • a application
  • Have you completed your application form yet?
  • Tom's college application was rejected.
  • You need to attach your photo to the application form.
  • I'm developing an Android application.
    		•
    appliance /əˈplaɪ.əns/ (n) đồ điện, đồ gia dụng (a device, machine, or piece of equipment, especially an electrical one that is used in the house, such as a cooker or washing machine)
  • All household appliances are now on sale.
  • The firm manufactures household appliances.
  • Are all of the kitchen appliances included in the price of the house?(Giá thuê (mua) nhà có bao gồm luôn mấy cái đồ điện trong bếp không?)
    • YouTube: Khánh Vy OFFICIAL
    candidate /ˈkæn.dəˌdeɪt/ (n) thí sinh, người dự thi, người dự tuyển
  • a person who is competing to get a job or elected position
  • someone who is taking an exam
    		•
  • How many candidates are there for the job?
  • Candidates must write their names on the top page of the exam paper.
  • Nine of the ten candidates were women.
  • We think she is a qualified candidate for the job.
  • How are the candidates chosen?
  • Mr. Smith is a candidate for mayor.
  • (n) candidacy:
    - the fact of being a candidate in an election
    - the position of being one of the people who are competing in an election
  • She withdrew her candidacy.
  • He waited until the end of August to announce his candidacy.
    		•
    interviewee /ˌɪn.tər.vyuˈi/ (n) người được phỏng vấn (the person who answers the questions during an interview)
  • He asked the interviewee some very personal questions.
  • Interviewee should prepare for the interview.
  • (n, v) interview:
    (n) cuộc phỏng vấn

    (v) phỏng vấn
  • Who's the most famous person you've ever interviewed on TV?
  • He's a good candidate on paper, but he didn't interview well.
  • I have a job interview tomorrow.
  • The actor gave an interview about his latest movie.
  • The interview is scheduled for 10:00 a.m. tomorrow.
