Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
for the time being tạm thời (for a short period of time, until a situation changes)
  • I'm working here for the time being. (this would imply you don't plan on being there for an extended period of time or that you're actively looking for another job)
  • As Modi Scope does not cover "Approval" function, for the time being, we do not implement it.
  • You can leave your suitcase here for the time being.

  • "How are you getting to work?"
    "For the time being, I'm taking the bus." (you have plans to take something other than taking the bus in the future)
    		•
    from time to time thỉnh thoảng (sometimes, but not often)
  • She has to work at weekends from time to time.
  • From time to time I still think of her.
    		•
    back and forth qua lại (going in one direction and then in the opposite direction, and repeating this several times)
  • The back-and-forth movement of the ship caused those onboard to become seasick.
  • He was pacing back and forth.
  • To switch back and forth between the two documents, press Shift-F3.
    		•
    deem /diːm/ (v) xem là, cho là
    (syn) consider
  • She is currently deemed to be the best British athlete.
  • They were told to take whatever action they deemed necessary.
  • They were deemed to be illegal immigrants.
    		•
    concurrent /kənˈkɝːrənt/ (adj) đồng thời, cùng lúc, cùng thời điểm (existing or happening at the same time)
  • Fortunately my children have concurrent birthdays so I only have to buy one birthday cake.
  • Because the two exams are concurrent, I will have to talk to one of my professors about rescheduling a test.
    		•
    thingy /ˈθɪŋ.i/ (n) (used when you cannot remember or do not know the name of the thing you want to mention)
  • Can you pass me that thingy - you know, that clip thing for holding papers together.
  • Look at that woman over there—the one with the straw hat with the pink thingy around it.
    		•
    enlist /ɪnˈlɪst/ (v)
    1. kêu gọi, nhờ
  • to persuade someone to help you to do something
  • to ask for help or support from someone


    • 2. nhập ngũ (to join the armed forces)
    		 1.
  • The organization has enlisted the support of many famous people in raising money to help homeless children.
  • They hoped to enlist the help of the public in solving the crime.
  • We enlisted some people to help prepare the food.


    • 2.
  • Frank enlisted in the marines at the age of 19.
  • They both enlisted in the navy a year before the war broke out.
