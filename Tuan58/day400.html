So far, so good (the situation is alright/satisfactory up to now)

  • "How’s your new job?"
    "So far, so good."

  • A: "How's the project going?"
    B: "So far, so good. We just need to finalize the user interface."

  • "If you have any problems with the apartment, please don't hesitate to give us a call."
    "Well, so far, so good. I hope I won't need to get in touch but thanks again."

  • Don’t take it personally; she’s rude to everyone.
  • Anna took it personally when the boss said some people were not working hard enough.
  • These criticisms should not be taken personally (= they are not meant to criticize any one person in particular).

    proactive /ˌproʊˈæk.tɪv/ (adj) chủ động phòng tránh, chuẩn bị trước
    (of a person or policy) controlling a situation by making things happen in advance or by preparing for possible future problems rather than waiting for things to happen and then reacting to them
  • The wedding planner was proactive in selecting an alternate venue in case it rained on the outdoor ceremony.
