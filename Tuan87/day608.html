Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
room /ruːm/ (n)
1. phòng (a part of the inside of a building that has its own walls, floor, and ceiling)

2. khoảng trống (empty space that can be used for a particular purpose)

3. chỗ, không gian, cơ hội (If there is room for a particular kind of behaviour or action, people are able to behave in that way or to take that action)
 1.
  • a three-room apartment
  • a room

    • 2.
  • That sofa would take up too much room in the flat.
  • My suitcase was so full I didn’t have room for anything else.
  • Is there enough room for me in the car?

    • 3.
  • It is important to give children room to think for themselves.
  • Once the plaster was dry there was no room for correction.
  • There's some room for improvement in your work (= it is not as good as it could be).
    		•
    odd /ɒd/ (adj)
    1. dị (strange or unusual; different from what is normal or expected, especially in a way that you disapprove of or cannot understand)

    2. (số) lẻ ((of numbers) that cannot be divided exactly by the number two)

    3. khoảng (used after a number, esp. a number that can be divided by 10, to show that the exact number is not known)

    4. thi thoảng (happening or appearing occasionally; not happening often)

    5. cọc cạch, không cùng đôi, cùng loại (not with the pair or set that it belongs to; not matching)
    		 1.
  • Her father was an odd man.
  • I had a very odd dream about you last night.
  • That painting looks very odd.

    • 2.
  • 1, 3, 5 and 7 are odd numbers.
  • The houses on this side of the street all have odd numbers.

    • 3.
  • He holds another 50-odd acres of land, providing plenty of room for expansion.
  • He's worked there for twenty-odd years.

    • 4.
  • I like the odd glass of wine with my dinner.

    • 5.
  • You're wearing odd socks!
    		•
    little /ˈlɪt̬.əl/ (adj) nhỏ (về kích cỡ, số lượng, tuổi, mức độ quan trọng/nghiêm trọng)
    + small in size or amount
    + young
    + not very important or serious
  • a little
  • When you were little your hair was really curly.
  • I lived here when I was little.
  • I can't remember every little detail.
  • (comparative form) less: ít hơn
  • We must try to spend less money.
  • Exercise more and eat less.
  • In recent years she has appeared in public less frequently.
    • (superlative form) least
  • It was the answer she least wanted to hear.
    		•
    few /fjuː/ (determiner, pronoun, noun, adjective) ít, một vài
  • Very few people can afford to pay those prices.
  • I’ve read a few of her books.
  • (comparative form) fewer: ít hơn
  • Fewer than 3,500 tigers are left in the wild today.
  • I tried to contact him no fewer than ten times.
  • Fewer than 20 students passed all the exams.
  • (superlative form) fewest: ít nhất
  • Which picture got the fewest votes?
  • The shortest route has the fewest traffic lights.
    		•
    far /fɑːr/ (adv) xa
  • far from here.
  • (adv, comparative form) further/ farther: xa hơn
  • We watched their ship moving gradually farther away.
  • I'm too tired to go any further.
  • Can you stand a bit further away?
  • (adv, superlative form) furthest/ farthest: xa nhất
  • Go on, boys! Let’s see who can run furthest.
  • Viv took a corner seat farthest away from the door.
  • (adj) further: thêm (​more; additional)
  • Cook for a further 2 minutes.
  • For further information, please ring 095-6710090.
  • No further developments have been reported.
    		•
    every other day/ every second day (phrase) cách 1 lại lặp lại; 2 1 lần (If something happens every other day or every second day, for example, it happens one day, then does not happen the next day, then happens the day after that, and so on.)
  • Milk is delivered every other day.
  • We meet every second Friday.
  • I visit my parents every other weekend.
  • The conference used to be held every year, but now it takes place every other year.
    		•
    Are you down?/ Are you up for it? (spoken, slang) Bạn có muốn tham gia cùng không?
    have bigger/other fish to fry (idiom) bận làm việc khác quan trọng hơn (to have something more important to do)
  • I can't deal with this now - I've got other fish to fry.
    		•
