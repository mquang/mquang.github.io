momentous /məˈmen.təs/ (adj) vô cùng quan trọng, trọng đại
  • Several members of our center recently won a prestigious science award. We plan to celebrate this momentous occasion with a banquet. We would like to book a space that is large enough for about 150 guests. If that's possible, we will be holding our event on September 27 at your hotel.

    get to do something (ph.v) có cơ hội được làm gì (to have the opportunity to do something; to be given the opportunity to do something)
  • She gets to travel all over the place with her job.
  • I never get to see her now that she’s moved to California.
  • I’d like to get to know you better – could we have dinner sometime?
  • I enjoy my work as a translator because I get to work at home a lot.
  • If you don't like this song, you get to slap me in the face.
    		•
    condone /kənˈdəʊn/ (v) làm ngơ, chấp nhận (to ignore or accept behavior that some people consider wrong)
  • I don’t condone such rude behavior.
  • The teacher said that she would not condone cheating on the test.
    		•
    strike up (ph.v) bắt đầu (mối quan hệ, cuộc trò chuyện...)
  • It can be difficult to strike up a conversation with a complete stranger.
  • He gets really jealous if his girlfriend strikes up a friendship with another man.
  • The two friends struck up a conversation about their recent travels.
    		•
    sportsmanship /ˈspɔːts.mən.ʃɪp/ (n) tinh thần thể thao (behaviour in sport that is fair and shows respect to the other players)
  • The players showed great sportsmanship by shaking hands after the game, even though they lost.
  • Because the coach believes in the value of good sportsmanship, he constantly reminds his players to treat rival teams with respect.
  • Jared showed a high standard of sportsmanship when he abandoned the race to help a fellow runner who was injured.
    		•
    hitchhike /ˈhɪtʃhɑɪk/ (v) xin quá giang (to travel by asking for free rides in other people’s cars, by standing at the side of the road and trying to get passing cars to stop)
  • I hitchhiked to the next town because I didn't have any money for a bus ticket.
  • When my car broke down, I had to hitchhike home.
  • They would not let their teenage children hitchhike or go on holiday without an adult.
  • (n) hitchhiker: người xin đi quá giang (a person who travels by asking for free rides in other people's cars, by standing at the side of the road and trying to get passing cars to stop)
  • He picked up two hitchhikers on the road to Bristol.
    		•
