reasoning /ˈriː.zən.ɪŋ/ (n) lý luận, lập luận, suy luận (the process of thinking about things in a logical way; opinions and ideas that are based on logical thinking)
  • What is the reasoning behind this decision?
  • The word conclude means to arrive at a final judgement through research or reasoning.
  • The jury used logic and reasoning to reach a verdict.
  • The teacher's reasoning was clear and concise, and the students were able to follow her instructions easily.

    missile UK: /ˈmɪs.aɪl/
    US: /ˈmɪs.əl/    		 (n) tên lửa (a flying weapon that has its own engine so that it can travel a long distance before exploding at the place that it has been aimed at)
  • The new missile is faster and more manoeuvrable than previous models.
  • The missile was intercepted by the air defense system.
    		•
    exceptional /ɪkˈsep.ʃən.əl/ (adj)
    1. đặc biệt, vượt trội (much greater than usual, especially in skill, intelligence, quality, etc.)

    2. đặc biệt, hiếm khi xảy ra (unusual and likely not to happen often)
    		 1.
  • At the age of five he showed exceptional talent as a musician.
  • This is a wine of exceptional quality.

    • 2.
  • If an exceptional event occurs, such as the death of a family member, you can ask for the court case to be postponed.
  • This deadline will be extended only in exceptional circumstances.
    		•
    marine /məˈriːn/ (adj) liên quan đến biển, hàng hải (related to the sea or sea transport)
  • Marine life is the variety of plants and animals that live in the ocean.
    		•
    catastrophe /kəˈtæs.trə.fi/ (n) thảm họa, thảm kịch (a sudden event that causes great suffering or destruction)
  • The earthquake was a catastrophe that caused widespread damage and loss of life.
  • The oil spill was an environmental catastrophe that polluted the coastline and killed thousands of marine animals.
    		•
    troublesome /ˈtrʌb.əl.səm/ (adj) gây phiền toái, khó chịu, nhức nhối trong một khoảng thời gian dài (causing trouble, pain, etc. over a long period of time)
  • Her hip has been troublesome for quite a while, and she'll probably need surgery on it.
  • The troublesome cough kept me up all night.
  • The team is working on a fix for the troublesome software bug.
  • The troublesome neighbor is always making noise late at night.
    		•
    nothing to write home about (idiom) không có gì đặc sắc (not exciting or special)
  • We expected the movie to be great but it was nothing to write home about.
  • The new restaurant is nothing to write home about. (=the food/service isn't very good)
    		•
    shoot the breeze (idiom) tán dóc, chém gió (to spend time chatting, talking about things that are not important)
  • After the meeting, my colleagues and I shot the breeze for a while before heading home.
    		•
    the elephant in the room (idiom) vấn đề mà ai cũng né tránh, không muốn nhắc đến (an obvious problem that no one wants to discuss)
