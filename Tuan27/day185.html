emission /iˈmɪʃən/ (n) khí thải (gas, etc. that is sent out into the air)
  • carbon dioxide emissions
  • Britain agreed to cut emissions of nitrogen oxide from power stations.
  • By the end of the decade we could reduce toxic emissions by half.
  • (v) emit: phát ra, tỏa ra, thải ra (to send out light, sound, or a smell, or a gas or other substance)
  • The alarm emits a high-pitched sound if anyone tries to break in.
  • Lightbulbs emit heat.

  • virgin forest/land
  • a virgin sheet of paper (= one not written on)

    landfill /ˈlænd.fɪl/ (n) bãi thải (an area of land where large amounts of waste material are buried under the earth)
  • The idea is to transform the landfill into a park.
  • The landfill tax is a tax on the disposal of waste.
  • Landfill provides a safe disposal option for waste that can't be recycled, composted, or used to generate energy.
  • 90 percent of American rubbish is dumped in landfill sites.

  • The company is accused of incinerating hazardous waste without a license.
  • The entire neighborhood was incinerated in the first half hour of the fire.
  • If an atom bomb was dropped over Manhattan, the whole city would incinerate in a flash.
  • All the infected clothing was incinerated.

    bale /beɪl/ (n) kiện, bó (a large quantity of something such as paper or hay that is tightly tied together especially into a block)

    		 (n)
  • a bale of cotton

    • (v)
  • The waste paper is baled, then sent for recycling.

    load /loʊd/ (n)
    1. hàng, kiện hàng (trên xe, tàu...)
    something that is being carried (usually in large amounts) by a person, vehicle, etc.

    2. tải trọng
    (often in compounds) the total amount of something that something can carry or contain
    (v) đưa người/đồ/hàng lên xe/lên kệ (to put a large quantity of things or people onto or into something)    		 1.
  • The trucks waited at the warehouse to pick up their loads.
  • A lorry shed its load (= accidentally dropped its load) on the motorway.

    • 2.
  • The maximum load for this elevator is eight persons.


    • (v)
  • Can you help me load the dishwasher?
  • The paramedics loaded Jason into the ambulance.
  • At the docks, ships were loading and unloading.
