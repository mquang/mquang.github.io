privilege /ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/ (n)
1. đặc quyền, đặc lợi (quyền lợi đặc biệt, dành riêng cho một cá nhân hay một nhóm người nào đó, những người bình thường khác không thể có được.)
(an advantage that only one person or group of people has, usually because of their position or because they are rich)


2. may mắn có cơ hội, vinh dự (something that you are proud and lucky to have the chance to do, and that you enjoy very much)		 1.
  • Healthcare should be a right, not a privilege.
  • A good education should not just be a privilege of the rich.
  • Senior management enjoy certain privileges, such as company cars and health insurance.
  • You can enjoy all the benefits and privileges of club membership.

    • 2.
    (syn) honor
  • I had the privilege of interviewing Picasso in the 1960s.
  • It was a real privilege to meet her.
    • YouTube: English Speeches
    paradise /ˈperədaɪs/ (n) thiên đường
  • a place that is extremely beautiful and that seems perfect, like heaven
  • a perfect place for a particular activity or kind of person

    		•
  • This mall is a shopper's paradise.
  • The airport is a paradise for pickpockets.
    		•
    worship /ˈwɜː.ʃɪp/ (v)
    1. tôn kính, tôn thờ (to have or show a strong feeling of respect and admiration for God or a god)


    2. yêu quý, ngưỡng mộ (to love, respect, and admire someone very much, often without noticing the bad qualities of that person)    		 1.
  • On the island the people worshipped different gods.

    • 2.
  • As a child, I worshipped my older brother.
  • As kids, we worshiped our aunt Martha, who let us sleep late and took us to great places.
    		•
    make ends meet (phrase) đủ tiền để xoay xở, trang trải cuộc sống (to have just enough money to pay for the things that you need)
  • Although they are poor, they still make ends meet.
  • Being out of work and having two young children, they found it impossible to make ends meet.
  • My wages were so low that I had to take a second job just to make ends meet.
    		•
    famine /ˈfæm.ɪn/ (n) nạn đói (a situation in which there is not enough food for a great number of people, causing illness and death, or a particular period when this happens)
  • There were reports of refugees dying of famine.
  • Four million people are now facing famine.
    		•
    live out (phrasal verb) sống nốt quãng đời còn lại (to spend the last period of your life in a particular way)
  • She retired and lived out the rest of her days quietly in the country.
  • He lived out his life in solitude.
    		•
    poverty /ˈpɑː.vɚ.t̬i/ (n) cảnh nghèo nàn, bần cùng, đói kém, thiếu thốn (the condition of being extremely poor)
  • Millions of elderly people live in poverty.
  • He emigrated to Australia to escape the grinding poverty of his birthplace.
  • Many of the most vulnerable people in our society are facing a future of grinding poverty.
    		•
    faith /feɪθ/ (n) niềm tin, sự tin tưởng (great trust or confidence in something or someone)
  • She has no faith in modern medicine.
  • I have great faith in you—I know you'll do well.
  • I have little faith in doctors these days.
  • Politicians must start keeping their promises if they want to restore the people's faith in government.
    		•
    capture /ˈkæp.tʃər/ (v)
    1. tóm, bắt giữ (to catch a person or an animal and keep them as a prisoner or in a confined space)


    2. giành được (to succeed in getting something when you are competing with other people)    		 1.
  • Government troops have succeeded in capturing the rebel leader.
  • Three of the gang's members were captured and sent to prison.

    • 2.
  • The Democratic Party captured 70 percent of the vote.
  • The company has captured 90% of the market.
  • (adj) captive: bị giam giữ, giam cầm
    (of a person or an animal) having limited ability to move or act freely because of being kept in a space
  • She was kidnapped and held captive for over a week.
  • She was held captive in a castle.
  • Initially seen purely as centres of entertainment, zoos were often heavily criticised in society for keeping animals captive.
    		•
    mistreat /ˌmɪsˈtriːt/ (v) ngược đãi (to treat a person or animal badly, cruelly, or unfairly)
  • Security forces are accused of mistreating prisoners.
  • I think people who mistreat their pets should be banned from keeping them.
  • They accuse him of mistreating his wife.
