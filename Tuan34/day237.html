Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
memo /ˈmem.oʊ/ (n) thư báo sử dụng trong nội bộ công ty, hoặc tổ chức (an official note sent from one person or department to another within a company or organization)
  • Staff have received a memo saying that they will be getting end-of-year bonuses.
  • The manager of the finance department sent a memo out to employees telling them when the next meeting would be held.
    		•
    surmise /səˈmaɪz/ (v) phỏng đoán (to guess something, without having much or any proof)
  • The police surmise (that) the robbers have fled the country.
  • Jason knew it was wrong of him to surmise his brother had stolen his money without actual proof.
  • Although the police had no proof, they were quick to surmise Mr. Peterson had killed his wife.
  • Since Jose had never passed any of his other tests, the teacher could only surmise he had cheated to earn an A+ exam.
    		•
    conform /kənˈfɔːrm/ (v) tuân thủ (to obey a rule, law, etc.)
    (syn) comply
  • The building does not conform with safety regulations.
  • At my new job, I need to wear a suit and tie in order to conform to the company dress code.
    		•
    insolent /ˈɪn.səl.ənt/ (adj) xấc láo, lấc cấc, hỗn láo (rude and not showing respect)
  • an insolent tone of voice
  • Because William was insolent to the principal, he got suspended from school for three days.
  • an insolent
  • Joe was disciplined for being insolent toward the teacher.
    		•
    impertinent /ɪmˈpɜː.tɪ.nənt/ (adj) xấc xược, lấc cấc (rude and not showing respect, especially towards someone older or in a higher position than you)
  • Because he is generally disrespectful and impertinent with peers and customers, he has lost four jobs within the past year.
    		•
    captivate /ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪt/ (v) quyến rũ, làm xiêu lòng, làm say đắm (to hold the attention of someone by being extremely interesting, exciting, pleasant, or attractive)
  • With her beauty and charm, she captivated film audiences everywhere.
  • Her singing captivated audiences everywhere.
  • I was captivated by her smile.
  • The children were captivated by her stories.
    		•
    impound /ɪmˈpaʊnd/ (v) tịch thu (to take something away from someone because you have the legal right to do so)
  • The police impounded cars and other personal property belonging to the drug dealers.
  • Cars parked illegally on this street will be impounded.
    		•
    fortify /ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪ/ (v) củng cố, tăng cường, bổ sung
  • to strengthen something, to make something stronger
  • to increase the strength or quality of food or drink by adding something to it
    		•
  • Property owners have to fortify their oceanfront homes against weather damage.
  • a fruit drink fortified with vitamin C (= with vitamin C added)
    		•
    halt /hɑːlt/ (v) dừng lại, đứng lại (to stop; to make somebody/something stop)
    (n) (a stop or pause)
    		 (v)
    (syn) stop
  • "Halt!" called the guard. "You can't go any further without a permit."
  • Halt! Who goes there?

    • (n)
  • Work came to a halt when the machine broke down.
  • His career came to an abrupt halt when he was seriously injured in a road accident.
    		•
