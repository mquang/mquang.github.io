challenge /ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒ/ (n)
1. thử thách
(the situation of being faced with) something that needs great mental or physical effort in order to be done successfully and therefore tests a person's ability

2. lời thách thức, thách đấu, thách đố (an invitation to compete or take part, especially in a game or argument)
(v)
1. thách thức, thách đấu, thách đố (to invite somebody to enter a competition, fight, etc.; to suggest strongly that somebody should do something (especially when you think that they might be unwilling to do it))

2. (to question whether a statement or an action is right, legal, etc.; to refuse to accept something; to express or represent doubt about the truth of something)
 (n1)
  • Finding a cure for cancer is one of the challenges facing medical researchers.
  • I’m looking forward to the challenge of my new job.

    • (n2)
  • "I bet you can't eat all that food on your plate." "Is that a challenge?"

    • (v1)
  • They challenged us to a game of football.

    • (v2)
  • a boy with a reputation for challenging the authority of his teachers
  • It was courageous of her to challenge the managing director's decision.
  • No one has challenged the assumptions that are made in the report.
  • Are you challenging my authority?

    1. những công việc/cách sắp xếp thứ tự công việc diễn ra (hằng ngày), theo lịch trình (the usual order in which you do things, or the things you regularly do)

    2. hàm (a part of a computer program that does a particular operation)
    		 1.
  • There's no set/fixed routine at work - every day is different.
  • Make exercise a part of your daily routine.
  • We are trying to get the baby into a routine for feeding and sleeping.
  • A pressing work project had disrupted his normal sleep routine.

    • 2.
  • The programmer writes a single sort routine generic enough to allow the routine to be used in each type of sort.
