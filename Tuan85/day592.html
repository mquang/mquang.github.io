standard /ˈstæn.dəd/ (n) tiêu chuẩn (a level of quality, especially one that people think is acceptable)
  • We have very high safety standards in this laboratory.
  • A number of Britain's beaches fail to meet European standards on cleanliness.
  • You'd better lower your standards if you want to find somewhere cheap to live.
  • (adj) substandard: không đạt tiêu chuẩn (below a satisfactory standard, and not acceptable)
  • When the homeowner viewed the contractor’s substandard work on the house, she refused to pay him.
  • All substandard products should be recalled promptly.
    		•
    ingenious /ɪnˈdʒiː.ni.əs/ (adj) thông minh, khôn khéo, sáng tạo
  • (of an object, a plan, an idea, etc.) very suitable for a particular purpose and resulting from clever new ideas
  • ​(of a person) having a lot of clever new ideas and good at inventing things

    		•
  • ingenious ways of saving energy
  • Our captain’s ingenious plan would allow us to sneak around the enemy and capture the objective without a fight.
  • She's very ingenious when it comes to finding excuses.
  • Johnny is so ingenious - he can make the most remarkable sculptures from the most ordinary materials.
  • (n) genius: thiên tài; tài năng thiên bẩm
    + someone who has an unusually high level of intelligence, mental skill, or ability
    + a very high level of intelligence, mental skill, or ability, which only a few people have
  • The film reveals Fellini’s genius.
  • Freud was a genius.
    		•
    echo /ˈek.əʊ/ (n) tiếng vọng lại (a sound that is heard after it has been reflected off a surface such as a wall or a cliff)
  • The echoes of his scream sounded in the cave for several seconds.
  • There's nobody down the well - it's the echo of your own voice you are hearing.
    		•
    throughput /ˈθruː.pʊt/ (n) khối lượng, lưu lượng xử lý trong 1 đơn vị thời gian (the amount of work, goods, or people that are dealt with in a particular period of time)
  • The airport has a weekly throughput of 100,000 passengers.
  • We need to improve our throughput because demand is high at the moment.

    (v) thụt lề đầu dòng (to begin a line of written or printed text after leaving extra space, compared with the place where other lines begin)
    		 (n)
    also indentation

    (v)
  • The first line of each paragraph should be indented.

    poll /poʊl/ (n)
    1. cuộc khảo sát, thăm dò ý kiến (the process of finding out what people think about something by asking many people the same question)

    2. cuộc bỏ phiếu (the process of voting at an election)
    		 1.
  • A recent poll found that 80% of Californians support the governor.
  • We're a poll to find out what people think about abortion.

    • 2.
  • The final result of the poll will be known tomorrow.
  • Polls close (= voting ends) at 9 p.m.

    sparingly /ˈsperɪŋli/ (adv) một cách tiết kiệm (in small amounts, or without wasting any)
  • There wasn't enough coal during the war, so we had to use it sparingly.
  • As a result of the water shortage, citizens have been asked to use water sparingly.

    1. ngân hàng (an organization where people and businesses can invest or borrow money, change it to foreign money, etc., or a building where these services are offered)

    2. bờ sông (sloping raised land, especially along the sides of a river)
