coherence /kəʊˈhɪə.rəns/ (n) sự mạch lạc (the situation when the parts of something fit together in a natural or reasonable way)
  • There was no coherence between the first and the second half of the movie.
  • The points you make are fine, but the whole essay lacks coherence.

    inexplicable /ˌɪn.ɪkˈsplɪk.ə.bəl/ (adj) kì lạ khó hiểu, không thể lý giải (too unusual or strange to be explained or understood)
  • For some inexplicable reason he gave up a fantastic job.
  • Things keep vanishing inexplicably from their house.
  • For some inexplicable reason my mind went completely blank.
    		•
    recollection /ˌrek.əˈlek.ʃən/ (n) hồi ức, những gì nhớ (a memory of something)
  • I have absolutely no recollection of the incident.
  • Although she couldn't remember much about the robbery suspect, the witness described him to the best of her recollection.
  • Abigail had a vague recollection of meeting Gabby, but couldn’t recall exactly where they first saw each other.
    		•
    faithful /ˈfeɪθ.fəl/ (adj) trung thành (firm and not changing in your friendship with or support for a person or an organization, or in your belief in your principles)
  • a faithful
  • I have been a faithful reader of your newspaper for many years.
  • She was one of the party’s most faithful and hard-working members.
    		•
    relinquish /rɪˈlɪŋ.kwɪʃ/ (v) từ bỏ điều không mong muốn (to stop having something, especially when this happens unwillingly)
  • He was forced to relinquish control of the company.
  • They had relinquished all hope that she was alive.
  • The court ordered him to relinquish custody of his child.
    		•
    spawn /spɔːn/ (v)
    1. đẻ trứng (to produce eggs in large quantities at the same time)

    2. tạo ra (to make a series of things happen or start to exist)
    		 1.
  • The frogs haven't spawned yet.

    • 2.
  • Her death spawned countless films and books.
  • The new economic freedom has spawned hundreds of new small businesses.
    		•
    indiscriminate /ˌɪn.dɪˈskrɪm.ɪ.nət/ (adj) bừa bãi (not showing careful thought or planning, especially so that harm results)
  • The indiscriminate use of fertilizers can cause long-term problems.
  • Becca regretted her indiscriminate choice in men when her boyfriend cleared out her bank account.
  • Antibiotics used indiscriminately can build up resistant strains of bacteria.
    		•
    enumerate /ɪˈnjuː.mə.reɪt/ (v) liệt kê (to name a list of things separately, one by one)
  • The salesman enumerated the features of the car.
  • The salesman enumerated the benefits of the insurance scheme.
  • Let me enumerate many flaws in your hypothesis.
  • The errors are too many to enumerate.
    		•
    the latter /ˈlæt̬·ər/ (n) cái sau, nửa sau (the second of two people, things, or groups previously mentioned)
  • He directed "The Wizard of Oz" and "Gone with the Wind," receiving an Oscar for the latter.
  • My favorite fruits are apples and watermelon, especially the latter on a hot summer day.
  • She was born in the latter half of the 18th century.
  • The latter half of the game was very exciting.
