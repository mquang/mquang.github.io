syllable /ˈsɪl.ə.bəl/ (n) âm tiết (a single unit of speech, either a whole word or one of the parts into which a word can be separated)
  • The word 'button' has two syllables.
  • In the word 'engine', you should stress the first syllable.
    		•
    subjective /səbˈdʒek.tɪv/ (adj) chủ quan (based on your own ideas or opinions rather than facts and therefore sometimes unfair)
    (opp) objective
  • Everyone's opinion is bound to be subjective.
    		•
    be dead meat (informal phrase) chết chắc (to be in serious trouble; usually issued as a threat)
  • He'll be dead meat if his girlfriend finds out.
  • If you don't do exactly as I say, you are dead meat!
    		•
    empire /ˈem.paɪər/ (n) đế chế
  • a group of countries ruled by a single person, government, or country
  • a group of organizations controlled by one person

    		•
  • the Soviet/Roman Empire
  • His business empire is now worth over $20 billion.
    		•
    coercion /kəʊˈɜː.ʃən/ (n) biện pháp cưỡng bức, ép buộc (the use of force to persuade someone to do something that they are unwilling to do)
  • He claimed the police had used coercion, threats, and promises to obtain the statement illegally.
  • Torture is a form of coercion that is used to make people tell the truth, or at least give answers of some sort.
  • The school bully used coercion to force the other kids to give him their lunch money.
    		•
    longevity /lɑːnˈdʒev.ə.t̬i/ (n) tuổi thọ (the ability to last for a long time)
  • Sponsoring directly impacts the longevity of this project.
  • The politician’s longevity in office will be determined by voter popularity.
    		•
    If only (phrase) giá như (We use if only to express a strong wish that things could be different. It means the same as I wish but is stronger)
  • If only Anna had been able to come.
  • If only she had listened to me, she wouldn’t be in this mess.
  • If only I had more money.
  • If only I were rich.
    		•
    blockade /blɑːˈkeɪd/ (n) sự phong tỏa (the action of surrounding or closing a place, especially a port, in order to stop people or goods from coming in or out)
    (v) phong tỏa (to surround a country or place with soldiers, ships, etc. to stop people or goods from going in or out)
    		 (n)
  • The enemy troops formed a blockade around the city.

    • (v)
  • The army blockaded all roads in and out of the region.
    		•
    vouch for somebody/ something /vaʊtʃ/ (ph.v) đảm bảo; xác nhận
  • to say that you firmly believe that something is true or good because of your experience or knowledge of it
  • to say that you believe that someone will behave well and that you will be responsible for their behaviour, actions etc

    		•
  • Patricia has checked the reports and can vouch for the accuracy of the information.
  • We can vouch for the software – everyone here uses it.
  • I was in bed with the flu. My wife can vouch for that.
    		•
