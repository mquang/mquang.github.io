neighborhood /ˈneɪ·bərˌhʊd/ (n)
1. khu vực xung quanh, khu vực lân cận (the area around you or around a particular place)

2. vùng, làng, thôn (an area of a town or city)
UK: neighbourhood
US: neighborhood
1.
  • I wouldn't like to live in the neighbourhood of (= in the area around) an airport.
  • Is there a good Chinese restaurant in the neighbourhood?
  • Houses in the neighbourhood of Paris are extremely expensive.
  • We searched the surrounding neighbourhood for the missing boy.
  • We live in the neighborhood of the school.
  • There is a big supermarket in my neighborhood.


    • 2.
  • She grew up in a quiet neighborhood of Boston.
  • The company aims to set up a nursery school in every neighbourhood.
  • We grew up in the same neighbourhood.
  • We live in a quiet, residential neighbourhood in the south of the city.
  • a neighbourhood populated mainly by Mexican Americans
  • (n, US) neighbor: người hàng xóm, quốc gia láng giềng
    • UK: neighbour
    drop by (phrasal verb) ghé thăm, ghé chơi (to make a short visit somewhere, usually briefly and without a specific invitation)
  • Why don’t you drop by for coffee some time?
  • If you feel bored, just drop by for a beer.
    • YouTube: I Can Do English
  • (ph.v, syn) drop in (on someone): ghé thăm, ghé chơi
  • Ever since we gave your mother that key, she keeps dropping in on us.
  • I thought I'd drop in on you while I was passing.
  • I dropped in on George on my way home from school.
    		•
    hectic /ˈhek.tɪk/ (adj) bận rộn (very busy or full of activity)
  • a hectic schedule
  • I’ve had a pretty hectic day.
  • He'd just returned from a hectic 10-day trip to New York.
  • The last few days had been hectic and exhausting.
    		•
    by the way (phrase) nhân tiện, à mà
    - used when saying something that is not related to the main subject you were talking about before, you can use when you want to change the topic of conversation.
    - used to give further information
  • I think we've discussed everything we need to - by the way, what time is it?
  • By the way, have you seen my keys anywhere?
  • We're going to spend a day in Paris and then, I think, a day or two in Rome. Oh, by the way, how did Randy's interview go?
  • Oh, by the way, my name's Julie.
  • And it's Jonathan, by the way.
    		•
    once /wʌns/ (conjunction) ngay sau khi (from the time when something happens)
    (adv)
    1. một lần

    2. đã từng     (in the past, but not now)    		 (conjunction)
    = as soon as
  • Once I've found somewhere to live I'll send you my address.
  • I called Lara once he'd left.
  • Employees currently working in Ridge Manufacturing's branch offices will move into the new headquarters once the building is finished.
  • Once you get there, you’ll love it.

    • (adv1)
  • We have lunch together once a month.
  • Try it once again.
  • Read it once more.
  • I swim once a week.
  • Paul’s been to Wexford once before.

    • (adv2)
  • This house once belonged to my grandfather.
  • Sonya and Ida had once been close friends.
  • She and her husband had once owned a house like this.
  • I was once like you.
  • (phrase) at once:
    1. ngay     (immediately or without delay)
    2. cùng lúc (together, at the same time)
    • 1.
  • Now, go upstairs at once and clean your room!
  • When I saw him I recognized him at once.

    • 2.
  • I can’t do two things at once!
  • You're trying to do too many things at once.
  • Anyone know the answer? Don't all shout at once, put your hand up.
    • ngày 53 mình đã đề cập đến cụm at once 1 lần rồi

    scholarship /ˈskɑː.lɚ.ʃɪp/ (n) học bổng (an amount of money given by a school, college, university, or other organization to pay for the studies of a person with great ability)
  • He got/won a scholarship to Harvard.
  • Paula went to the Royal College of Music on a scholarship.
  • When she was 18, she won a scholarship to study at the Conservatoire in Paris.
    		•
    tuition /ˈtuːɪʃən/ (n)
    1. (US) học phí (the money you pay for being taught)

    2. sự dạy (kèm) (the act of teaching something, especially to one person or to people in small groups)
    		 1.
  • (n, UK) tuition fees: học phí (the money you pay for being taught)
  • When I started college, tuition was $350 a quarter.
  • Tuition is $2,800 per year.
  • Few can afford the tuition of $12,000 a semester.
  • Tom got a part-time job to help pay for his tuition.
  • The scholarship pays the tuition fees but students still need to find money for accommodation, meals and books.

    • 2.
  • She had become expert in Chinese cooking under the tuition of her aunt.
  • I had to have extra tuition in maths.
  • This course consists of 6 or 8 hours daily individual one-to-one tuition.
    		•

        Dave: Hotel management.

        Maria: Well, what do you want to do once you graduate?
