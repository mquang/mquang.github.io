Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
look up (phrasal verb) tra cứu (trong từ điển, danh sách) (to try to find a piece of information by looking in a book or on a computer)
  • If you don't know what the word means, look it up in a dictionary.
  • She looked herself up in the telephone book to make sure her name was spelled correctly.
    • YouTube: Shaw English Online
  • (phrasal verb) look up to someone: ngưỡng mộ, kính trọng; noi gương
    + to admire and respect someone
    + to view one as a role model. They're somebody you want to be like.
  • He'd always looked up to his .
  • He’s a role model for other players to look up to.
    • Diary of a Wimpy Kid (2010)
    look after (phrasal verb) trông, chăm sóc
    - to take care of someone by helping them, giving them what they need, or keeping them safe
    - to be responsible for dealing with something, to be in charge of someone or something
    take care of
  • We look after the neighbours' cat while they're away.
  • Don’t worry, I’ll look after the kids tomorrow.
  • I’m leaving you here to look after the business until I get back.
  • Don’t worry about Maisie – she can look after herself.
    		•
    look forward to (phrasal verb) mong đợi, mong chờ, ngóng đợi
    - to feel pleased and excited about something that is going to happen
    - used at the end of a formal letter to say you hope to hear from or see someone soon, or that you expect something from them
    look forward to +
  • We're looking forward to meeting my grandparents this summer.
  • My children are really looking forward to our vacation in Canada.
  • I’m looking forward to your visit.
  • I look forward to hearing from you.
    • YouTube:
    + Britain's Got Talent
    + The English Coach
    look into (phrasal verb) xem xét, nghiên cứu, điều tra
  • We're looking into buying a new computer.
  • He looked into hiring another secretary.
  • We're looking into the possibility of getting to New York for her wedding.
  • Police are looking into the disappearance of two children.
  • I'll have to look into that matter.
    • YouTube: LikeWhat?
    look through (phrasal verb) xem qua, đọc qua qua, đọc lướt (to read something quickly)
  • I've looked through some catalogues.
  • She looked through her notes before the exam.
    • YouTube: Rachel and Jun
    look out (phrasal verb)
    1. coi chừng! (said or shouted in order to tell someone that they are in danger)

    2. để ý (to what is happening and be careful)    		 1.
    = watch out
  • Look out! There's a car coming!

    • 2.
  • Please look out when you’re crossing streets.
  • Look out for your Aunt while you’re at the station.
  • (n) lookout:
    1. thằng đứng ngoài canh cửa, trông chừng (cảnh sát)     (a person who watches for danger)

    2. điểm (nơi) mà tại đó có thể nhìn xa, quan sát cảnh đẹp, ngắm view (a place where you can see a long distance)
  • The mountain road had several lookouts where you could enjoy the view.
    • YouTube:
    + English With Kris Amerikos
    + Blended (2014)
  • (idiom) be on the lookout for: be alert and careful about something, either because you do not want to miss it or because it will be unpleasant or harmful and you need to avoid it.
  • Be on the lookout for my email in the next few days. It will have all the instructions you need for the project.
  • She was always on the lookout for sales.
  • We’re always on the lookout for new business opportunities.
