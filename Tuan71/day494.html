Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
wonder /ˈwʌn.dɚ/ (v) tự hỏi; muốn biết
(n) (a feeling of great surprise and admiration caused by seeing or experiencing something that is strange and new)
  • Will this turkey be big enough for eight, I wonder?
  • He's starting to wonder whether he did the right thing in accepting this job.
  • ‘Why do you want to know?’ ‘No particular reason. I was just wondering.’

    • (n)
    (syn) awe
  • The boys gazed in wonder at the shiny red Ferrari.
  • She gazed down in wonder at the city spread below her.
    		•
    narrow /ˈneroʊ/ (adj) (khoảng cách) bé, hẹp (measuring only a small distance from one side to the other, especially in relation to the length)
    (opp) wide
  • a narrow
  • The stairs were very narrow.
  • The boats all have to pass through this narrow channel.
    		•
    alley /ˈæl.i/ (n) ngách, hẻm (a narrow road or path between buildings)
  • A narrow alley led up between the houses to the main street.
  • This is a dead-end alley.
  • Tom was killed in a dark alley.
    		•
    avoid /əˈvɔɪd/ (v) tránh
  • to stay away from someone or something
  • to prevent something bad from happening

    		•
  • Do you think Lukas is avoiding me? I haven't seen him all day.
  • I try to avoid going shopping on Saturdays - they're always so busy.
  • I left the pub to avoid a fight.
  • We must find a way to avoid similar problems in future.
    		•
    unique /juːˈniːk/ (adj)
    1. độc nhất, duy nhất (being the only one of its kind)

    2. độc, lạ (very special or unusual)
  • I'd recognize your handwriting anywhere - it's unique.
  • Each person’s is unique.
  • The magnificent light make this place unique.
  • (n) uniqueness: (the quality of being unique (= unusual, or special in some way))
  • The government doesn't understand the uniqueness of our situation and the need for bigger subsidies.
  • the uniqueness of his style of writing

    joy /dʒɔɪ/ (n) niềm hạnh phúc (great happiness and pleasure)
  • They were filled with joy when their first child was born.
  • She began to cry again, but they were tears of joy.
  • (adj) joyful: rất hạnh phúc, vui sướng
  • Christmas is a joyful occasion for children.
  • It was a joyful reunion of all the family.
    		•
    hospitality /ˌhɑːspɪˈtælə.t̬i/ (n) lòng mến khách, lòng hiếu khách (friendly behaviour towards guests and visitors)
  • The local people showed me great hospitality.
  • You must allow me to repay your hospitality.
  • Thanks for your hospitality over the past few weeks.
    		•
    repel /rɪˈpel/ (v) xua đuổi, đẩy lùi (to force someone or something to stop moving towards you or attacking you)
  • It is not true that eating garlic repels mosquitoes.
  • (n) repellent: (dụng cụ, chất) xua đuổi (muỗi/côn trùng) (a substance that is used for keeping insects away from you)
