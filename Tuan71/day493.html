troops /trups/ (n) quân đội, quân (soldiers on duty in a large group)

    extent /ɪkˈstent/ (n)
    1. mức độ (how large, important, serious, etc. something is)

    2. diện tích, chiều dài (the physical size of an area)
    		 1.
  • She was exaggerating the true extent of the problem.
  • We don't know the extent of his injuries at this point.
  • I was amazed at the extent of his knowledge.

    • 2.
  • From the top of the Empire State Building, you can see the full extent of Manhattan.
  • The island is 300 square kilometres in extent.
  • The River Nile is over 6,650 kilometres in extent.
  • (phrase) to a certain extent/to some extent/to an extent: ở một mức độ (nhất định) nào đó (=partly)
  • To some extent what she argues is true.
  • To some extent, she was responsible for the accident.
  • What you say is true to some extent, but it’s not the whole picture.
  • To a certain extent, we are all responsible for this tragic situation.
  • (phrase) to what extent: đến mức độ nào (how much?)
  • To what extent will the budget have to be modified?
  • (phrase) the extent to which: (=how much)
  • The book discusses the extent to which (= how much) family life has changed over the past 50 years.
  • The report focused on the use of speed cameras, particularly the extent to which they reduced accidents.
    		•
    mucus /ˈmjuː.kəs/ (n) chất nhầy, niêm dịch (a thick liquid produced inside the nose and other parts of the body)
  • This drug reduces mucus production in the gut.
  • Coughing clears the lungs of mucus.
    		•
    phlegm /flem/ (n) đờm (the thick yellowish substance produced in your nose and throat, especially when you have a cold)
  • His throat congested with phlegm.
  • Blood in phlegm can be a sign of lung cancer.
    		•
    pus /pʌs/ (n) mủ (thick, yellowish liquid that forms in and comes from an infected cut or injury in the body)
  • The wound had not healed properly and was oozing pus.
    		•
    booger /ˈbuː.ɡɚ/ (n) gỉ mũi (a piece of dried mucus from inside the nose)
  • There is a huge booger in the professor's nose!
  • Little kids like to eat their boogers.
    		•
    pick one's nose (phrase) ngoáy mũi (to remove mucus from your nose with your finger)
  • I just saw a man trying not to pick his nose behind his newspaper!
  • Davey, don't pick your nose!
  • (phrase) blow one's nose: xì mũi (clear one's nose of mucus by forcing air through it)
  • He took out a handkerchief and blew his nose loudly.

    pest /pest/ (n) côn trùng, sâu bọ, động vật gây hại mùa màng (an insect or small animal that is harmful or damages crops)

  • The pesticides that farmers spray on their crops kill pests, but they can also damage people's health.

    tank /tæŋk/ (n) thùng chứa, bình chứa, bể chứa (a container that holds liquid or gas)

  • There was a tank of live lobsters in the restaurant.

    celebrate /ˈsel.ə.breɪt/ (v) đi chơi, ăn mừng nhân dịp ngày lễ, ngày kỉ niệm, ngày/sự kiện đặc biệt... (to show that a day or an event is important by doing something special on it)
