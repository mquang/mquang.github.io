    periodical /ˌpɪriˈɑː.dɪ.kəl/ (n) báo, tạp chí khoa học (a magazine or newspaper, especially on a serious or technical subject, that is published regularly)
  • (adv) periodically: định kỳ (in a way that is repeated after a particular period of time)
  • Mailing lists are updated periodically.
  • Teachers meet periodically to discuss progress.

    briefly /ˈbriːf.li/ (adv)
    1. (nói) qua, ngắn gọn, vắn tắt (in few words)

    2. for a short time    		 1.
  • Let me tell you briefly what happened.
  • We chatted briefly about the weather.

    • 2.
  • She met John briefly on Friday night.
  • We stopped off briefly in London.
  • She worked briefly as a journalist before getting into politics.
  • (adj) brief: ngắn (về thời gian, độ dài)
  • I had a brief look at her report before the meeting.
  • It'll only be a brief visit because we really don't have much time.

  • Let’s keep this conversation brief; I have a plane to catch.

  • She was wearing a fairly brief skirt.

    plenty of /ˈplen.t̬i/ (pronoun) rất nhiều (a large quantity that is enough or more than enough)
    (grammar) plenty of đứng trước danh từ không đếm được (uncountable noun) và danh từ số nhiều (plural noun)

  • There's plenty of cupboard space in the kitchen for all your pots and pans.
  • There are plenty of opportunities to improve your skills.
  • (adj) plentiful: rất nhiều (available or existing in large amounts or numbers)
  • During the summer are plentiful and cheap.
