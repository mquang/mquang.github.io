concern /kənˈsɜːn/ (v)
1. ảnh hưởng, liên quan, dính líu đến (to affect somebody/something; to involve somebody/something)

2. về việc, về vấn đề (=is about)

3. làm lo lắng (to cause worry to someone)
(n) mối băn khoăn, lo lắng, lo ngại		 1.
  • I didn't go to the meeting because nothing on the agenda seemed to concern me.
  • I'll answer any questions that specifically concern this week's homework.
  • Don't interfere in what doesn't concern you.
  • Please pay attention because this information concerns all of you.

    • 2.
    = is about
    (if a story, book, report etc concerns someone or something, it is about them)
  • The story concerns the prince's efforts to rescue Pamina.
  • The documentary concerns the eating habits of Britain's teenagers.
  • The report concerns the drug traffic on the Mexican–US border.

    • 3.
  • What concerns me is our lack of preparation for the change.
  • His attitude concerns me.
  • Her illness concerned my parents.

    • (n)
  • The report raises serious concerns over safety at the plant.
  • Don't hesitate to ask if you have any questions or concerns.
  • The continuing drought is causing concern among farmers.
  • (preposition) concerning: về/liên quan đến (việc, vấn đề) (about or relating to)
  • We have several questions concerning the report.
  • I've had a letter from the tax authorities concerning my tax payments.
    		•
    transition /trænˈzɪʃən/ (n, v) (sự) chuyển đổi, chuyển tiếp
    - the process or a period of changing from one state or condition to another
    - to change or to make something change from one state or condition to another
  • the transition from school to full-time work
  • You need a better transition between the second and third paragraphs.
  • In order to transition from a meat-eater to a vegetarian, the woman gradually ate less chicken and beef but more vegetables.
  • The company was slow to make the transition from paper to computer.
  • The 51-year-old is currently transitioning from male to female.
    • in transition: trong quá trình chuyển đổi (in the process of changing)
  • This course is useful for students who are in transition (= in the process of changing) from one training programme to another.
  • The country is in transition from an agricultural to an industrial society.
    		•

    1. danh sách, bản kiểm kê hàng hóa
    2. (US) kho    		 1.
  • Some of the things in the shop were not listed in the inventory.
    • do/make/take an inventory
  • She made an inventory of everything in the apartment.
  • The store is closed while we’re taking inventory (= counting and listing all the goods).
  • We need to take a hardware inventory to estimate the hardware upgrade costs.

    • 2.
    syn: stock
  • The dealer keeps a large inventory of used cars and trucks.
  • Our inventory of used cars is the best in town.
    		•
    warehouse /ˈweə.haʊs/ (n) kho chứa hàng (a large building for storing things before they are sold, used, or sent out to shops)
  • Textbooks are sent right from the warehouse to the schools.
  • The warehouse was completely destroyed by fire.
  • The building is currently being used as a warehouse.
    		•
    track /træk/ (v) theo dõi, truy vết
    - to search for a person or animal by following the marks they leave behind them on the ground, their smell etc
    - to record or study the behaviour or development of someone or something over time
    - to follow the movements of an aircraft or ship by using radar
    (n)
    1. đường ray (the pair of long metal bars fixed on the ground at an equal distance from each other, along which trains travel)


    2. dấu vết, dấu chân (a mark or line of marks left on the ground or on another surface by an animal, person, or vehicle that has moved over it, showing the direction they moved in)
  • The military use radar satellites to track targets through clouds.
  • hunters tracking and shooting bears
  • The progress of each student is tracked by computer.
  • The research project involves tracking the careers of 400 graduates.
  • We continued tracking the plane on our radar.
  • When you surf the web, you may be tracked by websites.

    • (n1)
  • The train ran off the tracks.

    • (n2)
  • deer tracks in the snow
  • The burglars were careful not to leave any tracks behind them.
  • The hunters followed the tracks of the deer for hours.
    		•
    substantially /səbˈstæn.ʃəli/ (adv) đáng kể, nhiều (very much; a lot)
    significantly
  • The quality of their products has improved substantially over the last five years.
  • House prices have risen substantially over the last six months.
  • Their new house is substantially bigger than their previous one.
    		•

  • Regardless of what you say, I'm still going to the club tonight.
