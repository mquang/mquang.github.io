Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
settle /ˈset̬əl/ (v)
1. (làm cho trở nên) ổn định, yên ổn, yên vị
- to relax into a comfortable position


- định cư (to go and live somewhere, especially permanently)
- to become quiet and calm, or to make something or someone do this

2. dàn xếp, giải quyết, hòa giải (to reach a decision or an agreement about something, or to end a disagreement)
  • After they got married, they settled in Brooklyn.
  • After dinner we settled in front of the television for the evening.
  • The dog settled on the grass to enjoy its bone.
  • A nurse settled the old man into a chair.
  • I'll call you back as soon as I've settled the kids for the night.
  • He settled down in his armchair to listen to the music.
  • You have to settle down and study for the history test.

    • 2.
  • The dispute was finally settled.
  • The problem is not settled yet.
  • How do you suggest we settle this problem?
  • I am considering how to settle the matter.
  • (ph.v) settle down: ổn định (cuộc sống, công việc)
    to begin to live a quiet and steady life by getting a regular job, getting married, etc.
  • They’d like to see their daughter settle down, get married, and have kids.
  • Tom is ready to settle down and start a family.
  • His second son married and settled down.
    		•
    find /faɪnd/ (v)
    1. thấy, nhận thấy

    2. cho thấy

    3. tìm kiếm, tìm thấy, tìm được, tìm ra
    find - found - found
    1.
  • Do you find John difficult to talk to?
  • I don't find him an easy person to get on with.
  • I find living in the city very stressful.
  • I found them quite easy to use.
  • I had never found that I was famous until that moment.
  • When they got back to their hotel, they found that their rooms had been burglarized.

    • 2.
    found that
  • In a recent survey, the IoD found that 43% of its female members were childless.
  • The study found that men who were married lived longer than those who were not.
  • Researchers found that smokers were more likely to get depressed than non-smokers.
  • Researchers have found that 67% of all American mothers now work outside the home.

    • 3.
  • Have you found your passport yet?
  • Look what I've found!
  • Can you find ?
  • We've found a great new restaurant near the office.
  • Scientists still haven't found a cure for AIDS.
    		•
    muddle /ˈmʌdəl/ (n) lộn xộn, nhầm lẫn, lẫn lộn lung tung
    (v) làm lộn xộn, nhầm lẫn, lẫn lộn lung tung
    		 (n)
  • The documents were in a muddle.
  • There was a bit of a muddle over our hotel reservations.

    • (v)
    also muddle up
  • The twins are so alike that it’s easy to muddle them up.
  • Someone’s muddled up all the papers on my desk.
  • (ph.v) muddle along: mất phương hướng, làm việc không mục đích (to continue doing something with no clear purpose or plan)
  • She muddled along for a year before going to college.
  • Decide what you want in life - don't just muddle along.
    		•
    aspect /ˈæs.pekt/ (n) mặt, khía cạnh
  • Which aspects of the job do you most enjoy?
  • His illness affects almost every aspect of his life.
  • The divorce affected every aspect of her life.
  • The book aims to cover all aspects of city life.
  • We must consider every aspect of the problem.
    		•
    outline /ˈaʊt.laɪn/ (n) ý chính, nét chính, nét phác
  • This is a brief outline of the events.
  • Some novelists start by writing an outline (= plan of the main points of the story).
  • Here’s the plan in outline.
  • Write an outline for your essay.
  • Write a short outline covering the main points before you start on your essay.
  • a brief outline of Chinese history
  • She drew the outline of the boat and then coloured it in.
    		•
    outset /ˈaʊt.set/ (n) từ ban đầu (the beginning)
    at/from the outset
  • I told him at/from the outset I wasn't interested.
  • I made it clear right from the outset that I disapproved.
  • From its very outset, the company has produced the highest quality products.
  • You should have mentioned it at the outset.
    		•
    useless /ˈjuːs.ləs/ (adj) vô dụng, vô ích, không có tác dụng
  • She suggested he take the cell phone but then remembered it was useless in the high mountains.
  • The doctor concluded that further treatment would be useless.
  • The treatment is completely useless.
  • a website full of useless information
  • This book is useless! I can't find any of the information I need.
  • (adj, opp) useful: hữu ích, có ích
  • Your advice was very useful, saving me a lot of time.
  • useful information
  • Their website has some useful tips on selling your home.
  • Keeping lists of the words you learn is a useful way of remembering vocabulary.
  • Don't just — make yourself useful!
  • Can you come and make yourself useful (=help people to do something)?
